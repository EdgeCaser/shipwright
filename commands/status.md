# /status - Stakeholder Status Update

Run this command to produce a structured status update for stakeholders that leads with what matters most.

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Gather Context
Ask the PM:
- What reporting period does this cover? (week, sprint, month, quarter)
- Who is the audience? (exec, team, board, cross-functional partner)
- Which workstreams should be covered?
- Any blockers, escalations, or decisions needed?
- Any wins worth highlighting?

Also check for existing artifacts from recent Shipwright sessions (PRDs, OKRs, sprint plans, retros) that can inform the update.

### Step 2: Draft the Update
Read and apply the framework from `/skills/measurement/stakeholder-communication/SKILL.md`.

Structure the update in this order:
1. **Progress against goals/OKRs** - what moved, by how much, on track or off
2. **Blockers** - each with an owner and a specific resolution path
3. **Decisions needed** - what the decision is, who needs to make it, and by when
4. **Next period's priorities** - the 3-5 things that will get focus next

Lead with the most important information even if it's bad news. Match detail level to the audience: execs get outcomes and asks, teams get operational detail, board gets strategic narrative.

### Step 3: Review and Sharpen
Audit the draft against these failure modes:
- Bad news buried in the middle or softened into "considerations"
- Blockers described without a named owner or resolution path
- Decisions listed without a deadline
- Tone mismatched to the audience (too casual for board, too formal for team)
- Vague progress claims with no metric or evidence attached

Fix any issues found before finalizing.

## Output

Produce a **Status Update** containing:
1. Summary line (one sentence: are we on track, behind, or ahead?)
2. Progress section with metrics or evidence
3. Blockers with owners and resolution paths
4. Decisions needed with deadlines
5. Next period's priorities
