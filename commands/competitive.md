# /competitive - Competitive Intelligence Workflow

Run this command to produce a complete competitive analysis package: landscape overview, detailed battlecard, and positioning implications.

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Landscape Mapping
Read and apply the framework from `/skills/discovery/competitive-landscape/SKILL.md`.

Ask the PM:
- Who are the 3-5 competitors you track most closely?
- Are there indirect competitors or alternatives (spreadsheets, manual processes, adjacent tools)?
- What dimensions matter most to your buyers? (price, features, integrations, support, brand)

Produce a feature matrix and positioning map.

### Step 2: Battlecard Creation
Read and apply the framework from `/skills/gtm/competitive-battlecard/SKILL.md`.

For the top 2-3 competitors, produce sales-ready battlecards with:
- Their pitch vs. ours
- Where we win and where they win
- Objection handling scripts
- Landmine questions to plant in buyer conversations

### Step 3: Positioning Review
Read and apply the framework from `/skills/strategy/positioning-statement/SKILL.md`.

Based on the competitive landscape, review or draft positioning:
- What category do we compete in (or should we redefine)?
- What is our competitive alternative?
- What is our unique differentiation?
- Does our current positioning still hold given the competitive landscape?

## Output

Produce a **Competitive Intelligence Package** containing:
1. Competitive landscape matrix with positioning map
2. Battlecards for top 2-3 competitors
3. Positioning statement (drafted or reviewed)
4. Recommended actions (gaps to close, advantages to emphasize)
