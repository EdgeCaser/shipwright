# /competitive - Competitive Intelligence Workflow

Run this command to produce a complete competitive analysis package: landscape overview, detailed battlecard, and positioning implications.

When fresh external competitive evidence is needed, do not jump straight into recommendations. Start with a bounded evidence pass, then synthesize from that evidence.

## Workflow Steps

### Phase 1: Evidence Basis

### Step 1: Define the Competitive Question
Read and apply the framework from `/skills/discovery/competitive-landscape/SKILL.md`.

Ask the PM:
- Who are the 3-5 competitors you track most closely?
- Are there indirect competitors or alternatives (spreadsheets, manual processes, adjacent tools)?
- What dimensions matter most to your buyers? (price, features, integrations, support, brand)

Produce a short competitive brief covering:
- the market question to answer
- the competitors and alternatives in scope
- whether fresh external evidence is required or existing evidence is sufficient

### Step 2: Collect Competitive Evidence
If fresh public-web evidence is required, use the local research collector first and only use interactive browsing for unresolved gaps.

Read and apply `/skills/discovery/competitive-landscape/SKILL.md`.

Produce an evidence basis containing:
- competitor positioning and segmentation
- major feature, pricing, and packaging signals
- notable strengths, weaknesses, and proof points
- evidence gaps that still matter to the decision
- retrieval notes only when notable, for example cache refresh, collector fallback, or interactive follow-up

If the PM already has current competitive evidence, skip the public-web pass and cite the supplied inputs instead.

### Phase 2: Synthesis

### Step 3: Battlecard Creation
Read and apply the framework from `/skills/gtm/competitive-battlecard/SKILL.md`.

For the top 2-3 competitors, produce sales-ready battlecards with:
- Their pitch vs. ours
- Where we win and where they win
- Objection handling scripts
- Landmine questions to plant in buyer conversations

### Step 4: Positioning Review
Read and apply the framework from `/skills/strategy/positioning-statement/SKILL.md`.

Based on the evidence basis, review or draft positioning:
- What category do we compete in (or should we redefine)?
- What is our competitive alternative?
- What is our unique differentiation?
- Does our current positioning still hold given the competitive landscape?

## Output

Produce a **Competitive Intelligence Package** containing:
1. Evidence Basis
2. Competitive landscape matrix with positioning map
3. Battlecards for top 2-3 competitors
4. Positioning statement (drafted or reviewed)
5. Recommended actions (gaps to close, advantages to emphasize)
