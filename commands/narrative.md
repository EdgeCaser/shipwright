# /narrative - Product Narrative Workflow

Run this command to produce a structured written narrative for a product decision, proposal, or strategic recommendation.

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Determine Format and Audience
Ask the PM:
- Who is this for? (exec team, board, cross-functional leads, your own team)
- What decision or action are you trying to drive?
- How much context does the audience already have?

If the audience is time-constrained or you need quick approval, use the executive briefing format. If the decision is complex and needs thorough reasoning, use the 6-pager.

### Step 2a: Executive Briefing (Short Form)
Read and apply the framework from `/skills/communication/executive-briefing/SKILL.md`.

Produce a one-page SCR (Situation-Complication-Resolution) document:
- Situation: what's happening
- Complication: why it matters now
- Resolution: what you're proposing and what you need

### Step 2b: 6-Pager (Long Form)
Read and apply the framework from `/skills/communication/product-narrative/SKILL.md`.

Produce a full narrative memo:
- Opening: the customer or business problem in concrete terms
- Context and data: what we know and how we know it
- Approach: what we're proposing and why this approach over alternatives
- Trade-offs: what we're giving up and why that's acceptable
- Plan: how we execute and what success looks like
- Risks: what could go wrong and how we mitigate

### Step 3: Review and Sharpen
Read the draft critically:
- Is the ask clear in the first paragraph?
- Could someone unfamiliar with the project follow the argument?
- Are claims supported by evidence or clearly flagged as assumptions?
- Is the tone appropriate for the audience?

## Output

Produce either:
- **Executive Briefing** (1 page, SCR format) with clear ask and supporting evidence, or
- **Product Narrative** (4-6 pages, memo format) with full reasoning chain

Include an appendix of supporting data if referenced in the narrative.
