# /personas - User Persona Workshop

Run this command to build structured user personas grounded in evidence rather than assumptions.

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Gather Inputs
Ask the PM:
- Who are your primary users today? (roles, titles, segments)
- What research do we have? (interviews, support tickets, analytics, survey data)
- Are there personas we think exist but haven't validated?

### Step 2: Build Persona Profiles
For each persona (aim for 2-4), produce a structured profile:

- **Name and role** (use a memorable label, e.g., "Compliance Officer Carmen")
- **Demographics** relevant to product use (company size, industry, team structure)
- **Goals** - what they're trying to accomplish
- **Pain points** - what frustrates them today
- **Current workflow** - how they solve the problem without your product (or with it)
- **Evaluation criteria** - what matters when they assess tools
- **Quote** - a real or representative quote that captures their mindset

### Step 3: Map Personas to Jobs-to-Be-Done
Read and apply the framework from `/skills/discovery/jobs-to-be-done/SKILL.md`.

For each persona, identify their primary job-to-be-done and 2-3 outcome expectations.

### Step 4: Validate and Prioritize
Ask the PM:
- Which persona represents the most revenue or growth potential?
- Which persona do we understand least well?
- Where are the gaps between what we assume and what we know?

Flag assumptions that need validation through research.

## Output

Produce a **Persona Document** containing:
1. 2-4 structured persona profiles with evidence tags (confirmed vs. assumed)
2. Persona-to-JTBD mapping
3. Prioritized persona list with rationale
4. Research gaps and recommended next steps
