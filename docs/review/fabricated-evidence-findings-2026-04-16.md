# Developer Report: Fabricated Evidence in Harness Artifacts

**Date:** 2026-04-16  
**Author:** Claude Sonnet 4.6  
**Audience:** Shipwright developers  
**Status:** Findings ready for review

---

## Summary

Artifacts generated by the conflict harness contain two distinct layers that should be evaluated separately:

1. **The structural layer** — decision frames, evidence gaps, pass/fail gates, recommended next artifacts. This layer is consistently well-formed and grounded in the scenario constraints. It is the part most useful to PM practitioners.

2. **The specificity layer** — numbers, benchmarks, citations, thresholds embedded within those structures. This layer regularly contains fabricated content: figures invented by the model to make the argument feel more rigorous than the available evidence supports.

**The practical implication:** The harness reliably produces correctly structured PM thinking. It does not reliably produce correct specific claims. Any number, citation, or benchmark in a generated artifact should be treated as invented until verified against an external source.

---

## Evidence

### Example 1 — Fabricated citation (`retention-vs-growth-tradeoff`)

The scenario has zero context files (`"context_files": []`). Side A's final artifact cites:

> "Retention investment generates higher ROI vs. new acquisition spend (internal modeling, **ctx-1**)"
> "CAC payback period is materially shorter when churn is suppressed (**ctx-1**)"

`ctx-1` does not exist. The model invented a citation to a non-existent source document and used it to support two key claims in the artifact. The judge did not flag this explicitly — Side A was docked for decision-deferral, not for fabricating a source.

### Example 2 — Fabricated industry benchmarks (`multi-tenant-shared-learning`)

The scenario provides no market data. Side A's final artifact states:

> "Enterprise customers in payments, lending, and marketplace verticals lose **0.3–1.8% of GMV** to fraud."
> "Near-parity with centralized training at sufficient tenant count (**≥20–30 active tenants**)"

Neither figure is in the case packet. Both are plausible-sounding industry statistics that the model generated to support its path-selection framework. The judge *did* penalize this: *"Side A repeatedly introduces specific numeric thresholds and model-performance assertions without supporting evidence in the packet."* That penalty was part of why Side B won. But the fabricated numbers still appear in the committed artifact.

### Example 3 — Fabricated cost estimate (`mobile-first-single-survey`)

The scenario states only "6 months of engineering effort." Side A adds:

> "A 6-month engineering commitment at typical team sizing represents **$600K–$1.2M in fully-loaded cost** and near-complete opportunity cost for the team."

The cost range is not in the scenario. The model invented it to strengthen its argument for the evidence gate. The direction (this is expensive, raise the evidence bar) is correct. The number is made up.

### Example 4 — Fabricated segment size (`churn-correlation-causation`)

The scenario states the 18-point churn gap and the self-selection risk. Side A's artifact adds:

> "closing the 18-percentage-point churn gap on even **20% of the inactive-collaboration segment**"

The 20% figure is invented. The point (even partial improvement is valuable) is correct. The specific claim is not grounded in the packet.

## The Pattern

Fabrication is not random. It follows a consistent model behavior:

**The model generates a structurally correct argument, then populates it with invented specifics to make the argument feel grounded.**

The structure — "this is the decision, here is what we know, here is what we don't, here is the gate" — comes from the scenario constraints and is generally sound. The numbers inside that structure come from the model's training data and are essentially plausible hallucinations.

This pattern is more pronounced in:
- **executive_ambiguity** scenarios (where evidence is sparse by design, so the model fills gaps)
- **historical_strategy** scenarios (where the model can draw on real-world analogues and cite them as if they apply)

It is less pronounced in:
- **evidence_fragile_prd** scenarios, where the prompt explicitly establishes that evidence is weak. Models in these scenarios are more likely to correctly frame absence rather than invent presence — because the scenario contract makes fabrication less available as a rhetorical move.
- **contradiction_or_boundary_prd** scenarios, where the constraint is structural (spec A conflicts with spec B), so the winning argument is about resolving the contradiction rather than citing data.

---

## What This Means for Verdict Reliability

The judge evaluates `evidence_discipline` as one of three rubric dimensions. When fabricated specifics are caught, they reduce the score on that dimension. But the judge does not always catch them — particularly when:

- The fabricated number is plausible and consistent with the argument
- The fabricated citation (`ctx-1`) looks like a valid reference rather than an invented one
- The overall artifact is otherwise strong, so the judge penalizes the losing side for its actual weakness (e.g., decision-deferral) rather than tracing every claim

**Consequence:** Verdicts are more reliable as signals about *reasoning quality and decision usefulness* than as signals about *factual accuracy*. A side can win by being more decision-useful while still containing fabricated supporting data. A side can lose by being too deferential while having more honest evidence handling.

This does not invalidate the verdicts, but it means the harness is measuring something more like *PM reasoning quality* than *artifact factual accuracy*.

---

## What This Means for Artifact Usability

The user's framing is correct: **the epics and stories themselves may still be correctly written even when the supporting data is fabricated.**

More precisely:

| Artifact element | Reliability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Decision frame (the question being answered) | High | Grounded in scenario constraints |
| Evidence gap list (what's missing) | High | Correctly identifies absent information |
| Pass/fail gates | High | Structurally correct even if gate thresholds are invented |
| Recommended next artifact | High | Logical follow-on from the stated unknowns |
| Functional requirements | Medium | Structure is correct; specific constraints may be over-specified |
| Specific numbers, thresholds, benchmarks | Low | Treat as invented until verified |
| Citations to context files | Low | Must be verified; ctx-N references may point to non-existent documents |
| Industry statistics | Low | Plausible-sounding but unverified; model draws from training data |

A PM using a harness-generated artifact as a *template* — adopting the decision frame, the evidence gap structure, the pass/fail gate format — is on solid ground. A PM treating the specific numbers inside that template as facts is not.

---

## Recommendations for Developers

### 1. Add a fabrication detection pass to the judge prompt

The judge currently evaluates evidence discipline as a holistic dimension. It should additionally be instructed to explicitly flag any specific claim (number, citation, benchmark) that cannot be traced to the case packet. A simple addition to the judge prompt:

> "For each piece of quantitative evidence cited by either side, note whether it appears in the case packet. Flag any claim that introduces specificity not present in the packet."

This would surface fabricated ctx-N citations and invented benchmarks as a structured finding, not just a holistic dimension score.

### 2. Validate context file references before generating sides

The harness knows which context files exist (`case_packet.evidence`). Before the first-pass prompt, it could inject an explicit constraint:

> "The only evidence available to you is contained in the case packet above. Do not introduce statistics, benchmarks, or citations that are not present in the packet."

And post-generation, scan for `ctx-N` references and verify each one against the actual evidence array. Flag or reject artifacts with phantom citations before they reach the judge.

### 3. Add an `unsupported_claim_count` metric to the verdict schema

The run schema already tracks `disagreement_rate`, `declared_adoption_rate`, and `substantive_revision_rate`. An `unsupported_claim_count` field — populated by the judge's explicit count of claims not traceable to the packet — would make fabrication a first-class measurable output rather than a side effect of holistic scoring.

### 4. Treat artifact specifics as illustrative, not factual, in documentation

If harness-generated artifacts are used in any downstream documentation, demos, or examples, they should carry an explicit caveat that specific numbers and citations within them are illustrative and require verification against real sources before any business decision is made.

---

## What Is Not Broken

The harness is working correctly for its stated purpose: generating two opposing, structured PM artifacts and evaluating which demonstrates stronger reasoning. The fabrication problem is a known and expected behavior of language models operating without grounded evidence sources.

The structural outputs — decision frames, gap analyses, readiness gates, escalation paths — are sound and useful. The harness correctly identifies which reasoning approach is more decision-useful, even when both approaches are contaminated by invented specifics.

The finding here is a calibration note, not a system failure. The harness should be positioned as a **reasoning quality evaluator**, not a **factual accuracy engine**.
