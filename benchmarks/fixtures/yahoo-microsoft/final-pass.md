# Strategic Recommendation: Yahoo-Microsoft Acquisition (Revised)

## Decision Frame

Recommendation: Sell to Microsoft. The independent path has no credible search strategy and display advertising alone cannot sustain Yahoo's current valuation.

The strongest argument for independence is the Alibaba stake — potentially worth $50B+ at IPO. But that value exists regardless of whether Yahoo is independent or acquired; Microsoft would inherit the stake or it could be spun out pre-acquisition. The operating business (ex-stakes) is a declining search player and a display leader under siege from social platforms.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

- Microsoft's actual willingness to return after public rejection
- Whether Alibaba stake can be separated in deal structure to capture upside
- Facebook's impact on display ad pricing over 2009-2012
- Whether a new CEO (Yang is likely to be replaced) could execute an independent turnaround

## Pass/Fail Readiness

PASS — the board should authorize management to reopen negotiations. Icahn's 5% stake and public pressure create a governance risk if the board appears to be entrenching itself against shareholder interests.

## Recommended Next Artifact

Financial advisor engagement: Goldman Sachs / Morgan Stanley mandate to evaluate revised Microsoft terms, Alibaba stake carve-out structures, and alternative acquirer landscape.
