# Strategic Recommendation: Blockbuster Total Access Program

## Decision Frame

Recommendation: Continue funding Total Access at current levels through Q2 2008, then reassess based on subscriber trajectory and Netflix streaming adoption curves.

Total Access is Blockbuster's only competitive weapon against Netflix's subscription model. Killing it saves $400M/year but concedes the online rental market entirely, leaving Blockbuster as a pure brick-and-mortar retailer in a declining physical media market.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

- Netflix streaming adoption curve is uncertain — only 1,000 titles available, broadband at 50% penetration
- Whether Total Access subscribers convert to profitable long-term customers or remain loss-leaders
- Franchisee willingness to absorb cannibalization if given revenue-sharing incentives
- Whether Redbox kiosk expansion will erode store-visit traffic independent of Total Access
- Icahn's willingness to sustain losses beyond 2007

## Pass/Fail Readiness

CONDITIONAL PASS — the program is competitively effective but financially unsustainable at current burn rate. Requires cost optimization (reduce free in-store exchanges from unlimited to 3/month) to extend runway.

## Recommended Next Artifact

Financial model comparing three scenarios: (1) Continue at current burn, (2) Continue with caps, (3) Kill program — each with 3-year subscriber and revenue projections.
