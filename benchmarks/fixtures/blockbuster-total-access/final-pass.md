# Strategic Recommendation: Blockbuster Total Access Program (Revised)

## Decision Frame

Recommendation: Continue Total Access with modified economics — cap free in-store exchanges at 3/month, negotiate franchisee revenue-sharing, and allocate 15% of online budget to streaming R&D as a hedge.

The core insight: Total Access is not a product — it is Blockbuster's transition bridge from physical retail to digital delivery. Killing it does not save Blockbuster; it merely accelerates the timeline to irrelevance. Netflix's streaming pivot will take 3-5 years to mature. That window is Blockbuster's to use or lose.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

- Netflix streaming quality and library expansion timeline (currently 1,000 titles vs. 90,000+ on DVD)
- Consumer willingness to shift from ownership/physical to streaming-only
- Whether capped Total Access retains competitive pull (risk: Netflix markets "unlimited" advantage)
- Broadband infrastructure readiness for HD streaming at scale
- Icahn bloc's patience horizon — will they force a CEO change before the strategy matures?

## Pass/Fail Readiness

CONDITIONAL PASS — requires board commitment to 18-month runway with modified cost structure. The $400M annual burn can be reduced to ~$250M with exchange caps while retaining ~80% of competitive effect. If subscriber growth stalls after caps, reassess in Q3 2008.

## Recommended Next Artifact

Board presentation with three-scenario financial model and explicit decision triggers (subscriber floor, burn ceiling, streaming adoption threshold) that would warrant killing the program.
