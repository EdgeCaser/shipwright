# Strategic Recommendation: Netflix Qwikster Split (Revised)

## Decision Frame

Recommendation: Reverse the Qwikster split. Maintain operational integration under one brand while quietly building internal separation.

The market has spoken: 800K lost subscribers, 77% stock decline, and 27K negative comments. The strategic intent (decouple declining DVD from growing streaming) is correct but the execution vehicle (public brand split with degraded UX) is wrong. The right approach is internal organizational separation with a unified customer-facing brand.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

- Streaming content cost trajectory — $2B projected for 2012 licensing, can DVD margins cover the gap?
- International expansion ROI timeline — early markets (Canada, Latin America) not yet at scale
- Whether Amazon Prime Video bundling with Prime shipping creates an unbeatable value proposition
- Whether the subscriber base stabilizes after reversing the split or continues declining from the price increase

## Pass/Fail Readiness

CONDITIONAL PASS — reverse the split immediately, hold the price increase (it is strategically necessary even if painful), and communicate a clear "we listened" narrative. Monitor subscriber trajectory for two quarters.

## Recommended Next Artifact

Board presentation with three-year financial model: streaming content investment funded by DVD cash flows, international subscriber projections, and break-even timeline for streaming as a standalone business.
