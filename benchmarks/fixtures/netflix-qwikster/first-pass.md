# Strategic Recommendation: Netflix Qwikster Split

## Decision Frame

Recommendation: Reverse the Qwikster split immediately. Keep both services under the Netflix brand.

The strategic logic of decoupling streaming from DVD is sound long-term, but execution timing is catastrophic. Splitting during a subscriber revolt after a 60% price increase compounds customer anger. The Qwikster brand carries zero equity and the operational separation (separate queues, logins, billing) destroys the integrated experience that retains dual-service customers.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

- Whether subscriber losses are price-driven (recoverable) or trust-driven (structural)
- How quickly streaming content library can close the gap with DVD (20K vs 100K titles)
- Competitor response timeline — will Amazon/Hulu capitalize on the disruption?
- Whether DVD cash flows can fund streaming content acquisition for 2-3 more years

## Pass/Fail Readiness

FAIL on execution. The split should be revisited when streaming is self-sustaining, not while it depends on DVD cross-subsidy.
