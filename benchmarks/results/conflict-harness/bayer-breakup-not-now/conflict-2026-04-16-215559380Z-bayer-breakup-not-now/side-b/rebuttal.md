# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Net debt of ~€34.5B rising 8.5% YoY, combined with EBITDA guidance below 2023 levels, makes debt allocation across separated entities structurally impractical until the €2B restructuring savings materialize from 2026.

## Evidence Or Reason
This overstates what the visible evidence supports. The packet shows high debt, weaker near-term EBITDA, and future cost savings, but it does not show Bayer could not allocate debt across entities, that any specific successor would be non-investment-grade or unable to refinance, or that waiting until 2026 materially improves separability. It also assumes the €2B savings would flow cleanly to standalone credit profiles rather than being offset by separation dis-synergies, continued litigation drag, or weak end markets. Without segment-level leverage capacity, pro forma capital structures, or creditor constraints, 'structurally impractical' is asserted rather than demonstrated.
