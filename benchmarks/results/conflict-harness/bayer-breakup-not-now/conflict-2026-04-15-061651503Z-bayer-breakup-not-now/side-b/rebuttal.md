# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-4
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
€2B annual cost savings from restructuring (realizable from 2026) improve the standalone profiles of separated entities, making 'fix first, split later' a value-maximizing sequence rather than avoidance

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible record only says Bayer expected about €2B of annual cost savings from 2026 through restructuring efforts. That does not establish that the savings will be realized on time, that they are separation-relevant rather than merely offsetting ongoing weakness, or that they will materially improve standalone valuations enough to make delay value-maximizing. The claim jumps from a cost-savings target to a strong sequencing conclusion without showing bridge evidence on execution credibility, valuation impact, or whether investors would give management credit before results are proven.
