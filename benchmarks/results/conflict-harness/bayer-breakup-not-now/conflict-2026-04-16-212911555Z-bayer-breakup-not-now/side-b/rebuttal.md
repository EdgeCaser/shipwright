# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Bayer's approximately 34.5 billion euro net debt load, rising year-over-year, means any structural separation would be negotiated from a position of weakness — creditors, not shareholders, would capture the value unlock.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible evidence shows high debt, but it does not establish the key causal leap that a breakup would necessarily transfer most of the value unlock to creditors rather than shareholders. That requires segment-level cash flow and leverage capacity, debt covenant constraints, refinancing terms, and plausible separation structures. Without those, the claim overstates certainty: high leverage can complicate a split, but it does not by itself prove that creditors would dominate the economics or that a separation could not still unlock net equity value.
