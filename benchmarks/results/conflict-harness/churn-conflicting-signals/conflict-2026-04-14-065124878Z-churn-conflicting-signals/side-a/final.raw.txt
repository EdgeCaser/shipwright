Create a baseline view that compares:\n- observed churn cohorts\n- current strategy-priority segments\n- revenue and expansion value by segment\n3. Require every retention proposal to specify:\n- target segment\n- evidence source\n- hypothesized churn driver\n- expected retention impact\n- effect on strategic fit\n4. Gate intervention rollout in two stages:\n- Diagnostic stage: collect segment baseline and validate whether strategy targets match retained-value cohorts.\n- Action stage: run reversible experiments only for segments with sufficient evidence.\n6. Track guardrails so retention gains are not produced by excessive discounting, support burden, or preservation of persistently low-fit customers.\n\n## Success Metrics\n- Primary: revenue churn reduction in evidence-validated priority segments.\n- Secondary: activation rate, save rate, retained revenue after intervention, and time-to-diagnosis of churn driver.\n- Guardrails: support cost per retained account, discount dependency, retention of low-fit cohorts, and false-positive intervention rate.\n\n## Rollout\n- Phase 1: establish segment and strategy-alignment baseline.\n- Phase 2: classify churn drivers and identify whether the issue is retention execution or strategy fit.\n- Phase 3: run targeted, reversible experiments in validated priority segments.\n- Phase 4: scale successful interventions or escalate to strategy revision.\n\n## Decision Frame\nThe first decision is not which segment to save. The first decision is whether the current strategy targets are valid retention priorities once churn, value, and fit are viewed together.
