## Claim Under Attack
The NPS-churn divergence is best explained by survivorship bias in NPS sampling — churning customers either do not respond or churn between survey windows, creating an artificially positive NPS trend that masks a distinct at-risk segment.

## Evidence Or Reason
This asserts a specific causal explanation and a hidden-segment diagnosis without any cited sampling analysis, response-rate breakdown, survey-window timing evidence, or account-level join showing churners are underrepresented in NPS. The artifact itself lists this as an open question, which means the claim is still a hypothesis, not the best-supported explanation. Other visible alternatives, including billing-cycle effects or non-satisfaction churn drivers, are not ruled out.
