# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
NRR can mask dangerous logo churn: 105% NRR with 81% logo retention means expansion revenue from survivors is papering over a worsening retention problem, and this will cliff when contracts renew.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible artifact shows a correlation between healthy NRR and weaker logo retention, but it does not establish that expansion revenue is merely masking churn rather than reflecting legitimate account growth in a different segment mix. The stronger leap is the prediction that retention 'will cliff when contracts renew': no contract-timing data, renewal cohort analysis, or breakdown of expansion into true upsell versus pricing/renewal mechanics is provided. Without that evidence, the claim overstates both mechanism and forward risk.
