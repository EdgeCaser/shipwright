- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
When churn signals conflict, the PRD should focus on hypothesis-led, segmented interventions with explicit measurement instead of assuming a single root cause.

## Evidence Or Reason
The claim advocates hypothesis-led interventions but the artifact itself provides no evidence basis for its three hypotheses (H1-H3) or its three chosen MVP intervention plays. The PRD assumes activation failure, value-gap, and save-offer interventions are the right bets without explaining why these were selected over alternatives like pricing experiments, contract-structure changes, or competitive-displacement responses. A hypothesis-led approach that cannot justify its own hypothesis selection is functionally equivalent to the single-root-cause assumption it criticizes — it simply distributes the unsupported assumption across three buckets instead of one. The artifact also lacks any prioritization framework for sequencing the three interventions, meaning resource allocation defaults to equal-weight parallelism with no stated rationale.
