# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The 34% rise in cancellation-intent tickets with stable CSAT indicates silent churn — customers deciding to leave before engaging support — which justifies accelerated investigation even without a full retention pivot.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible evidence does not establish silent churn. A rise in cancellation-intent tickets shows more customers are engaging support about cancellation, not that customers are leaving without engaging support. The artifact gives no denominator, base volume, linkage between these tickets and completed churn, timing relative to cancellation decisions, or comparison to churned customers who never opened tickets. Stable resolved-ticket CSAT also may not represent cancellation-intent interactions specifically.
