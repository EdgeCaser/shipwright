# Revised artifact

## Decision Frame
Proceed with a churn-reduction PRD that authorizes only low-regret, segmented retention interventions that can be evaluated against strategy fit, while explicitly blocking broad changes that would commit the product to an unverified churn narrative. The decision is not to wait for certainty; it is to act through constrained interventions that preserve strategic direction until the conflict among signals is clarified.

## Product Goal
Reduce churn without solving for retention in ways that undermine current strategy targets.

## Problem Statement
The prompt requires a churn reduction PRD in a situation where signals conflict with strategy targets. That creates a product decision problem, not just a research problem: the team must choose what kind of retention action is acceptable now, what kind is prohibited now, and how to judge whether actions should expand or stop.

## Product Decision
The product will pursue a bounded retention track with three rules:
- Launch only interventions that are reversible, segment-specific, and compatible with current strategy targets.
- Do not approve broad pricing, packaging, positioning, or roadmap shifts based solely on unresolved conflicting signals.
- Require each intervention to be evaluated on two dimensions: retention effect and strategy fit.

## Scope
In scope:
- Segment-specific retention interventions
- Explicit hypothesis statements for each intervention
- Decision checkpoints to continue, revise, or stop interventions
- Documentation of strategy risks tied to each intervention

Out of scope:
- Company-wide strategic changes justified by a single unresolved churn explanation
- Permanent rollout of retention actions before they are reviewed for both retention effect and strategy fit
- Reframing strategy targets inside this PRD

## Requirements
1. Every proposed intervention must name the customer segment it targets.
2. Every proposed intervention must state the churn hypothesis it is intended to test or address.
3. Every proposed intervention must state the strategy risk it could create.
4. Each intervention must have a predefined continue, revise, or stop checkpoint.
5. The PRD must distinguish between interventions that are safe to run under uncertainty and interventions that require stronger evidence before approval.
6. The PRD must end with a portfolio decision on which interventions are approved now, which are blocked now, and what evidence would be required to unblock them.

## Approved-Now Intervention Class
Approve interventions that are narrow, reversible, and do not require abandoning current strategy targets.

## Blocked-Now Intervention Class
Block interventions that would materially redirect pricing, packaging, positioning, or roadmap strategy before the conflicting signals are resolved.

## Success Criteria
- The team can execute retention work now without making strategy-breaking commitments.
- Each intervention receives a continue, revise, or stop decision.
- Leadership can distinguish between actions that are acceptable under uncertainty and actions that are not.

## Risks
- Narrow interventions may miss a larger churn driver.
- Teams may label interventions as reversible even when strategic spillover is real.
- Strategy-fit judgments may remain ambiguous if targets are not clearly articulated.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- Which specific signals are in conflict
- Which segments are driving the churn concern
- Which strategy targets are most at risk from retention actions
- Which interventions are already under consideration

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if:
- The PRD clearly states which intervention classes are approved now and which are blocked now
- Each approved intervention has a target segment, hypothesis, and decision checkpoint
- Strategy risk is part of the approval decision, not an afterthought

Fail if:
- The PRD defers all action pending further diagnosis
- The PRD allows broad strategy-changing interventions without stronger support
- The PRD defines success only as churn reduction and ignores strategy fit

## Recommended Next Artifact
Create an intervention decision table that lists candidate retention actions, marks each as approved now or blocked now, states the segment and hypothesis for each approved action, and names the evidence needed to revisit blocked actions.
