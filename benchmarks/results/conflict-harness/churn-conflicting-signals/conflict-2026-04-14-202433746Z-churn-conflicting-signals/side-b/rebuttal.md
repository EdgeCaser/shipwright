# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
A 4-week diagnostic sprint with 6 explicit pass/fail criteria is the minimum viable path to an evidence-based retention decision, avoiding premature commitment in either direction.

## Evidence Or Reason
The artifact provides no visible basis for why 4 weeks is sufficient or why these 6 criteria are the minimum required set. Several criteria depend on capabilities the same artifact says do not currently exist, such as revenue-weighted churn dashboards, validated usage-to-churn correlation, and cost-to-retain measurement. That makes the timeline and gating logic asserted rather than evidenced. The fallback rule is also inconsistent: if only 4 of 6 criteria are met, the team proceeds, which undermines the claim that all 6 are necessary for decision readiness.
