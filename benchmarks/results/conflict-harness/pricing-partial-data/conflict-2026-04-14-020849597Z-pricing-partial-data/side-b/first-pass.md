# Pricing Strategy Recommendation

## Recommendation
Adopt a guarded pricing-change approach: run a limited, reversible price test on one customer segment or plan tier rather than a full rollout. Keep packaging stable, preserve grandfathering for existing customers, and define explicit stop conditions before launch.

## Rationale
With partial market data, the highest-value decision is not selecting an aggressive new price point with false precision; it is reducing downside while learning quickly. A segmented test preserves revenue, generates willingness-to-pay evidence, and avoids contaminating the entire customer base with an under-validated pricing move.

## Proposed Move
- Test one moderate increase or packaging simplification on new customers only.
- Hold enterprise and strategic accounts out of scope until sales feedback is collected.
- Use a 2-4 week measurement window focused on conversion, expansion intent, churn risk signals, and sales-cycle friction.
- Pre-commit rollback thresholds if conversion or win rate degrades beyond an agreed band.

## Decision Frame
The decision is whether to change pricing now despite incomplete market evidence. The recommended decision is yes, but only through a bounded experiment. This balances speed with risk control and produces decision-grade evidence for a later broader rollout.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- No direct competitor price benchmark is available in the packet.
- No customer willingness-to-pay research is provided.
- No baseline elasticity, conversion, or churn sensitivity data is provided.
- No segment-specific margin or sales-assist cost data is provided.

These gaps mean the current recommendation should optimize for learning and reversibility, not full-market confidence.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass for a limited experiment if the team can isolate a segment, instrument conversion and retention signals, and enforce rollback rules. Fail for a full pricing migration because the available evidence is insufficient to justify broad exposure.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A pricing experiment brief with test cell design, target segment, guardrails, success metrics, rollback thresholds, and a follow-up decision memo for full rollout, revision, or cancellation.
