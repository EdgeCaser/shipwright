# Revised artifact

## Recommendation
Recommend a conditional pricing path, not a blanket rule: use a staged, reversible price test only if the business can isolate a meaningful segment, observe results in a useful timeframe, and tolerate short-term learning cost. If those conditions are not true, the better move is either an immediate corrective repricing with close monitoring or a short, focused evidence sprint before any price change. [ctx-1][ctx-2]

## Decision Frame

### Decision
Proceed with a staged pricing test only when uncertainty is localized and test economics are viable; otherwise choose a different path.

### Why This Is The Best Current Move
Partial market data does not by itself determine one correct pricing action. The right decision depends on what is missing, how costly delay is, and whether a test can produce decision-quality signal fast enough. A staged test is strongest when the company needs elasticity learning, can limit exposure, and can reach interpretable sample sizes. It is weaker when the business is clearly underpriced, facing an imminent market move, or operating at volumes too low for a useful cohort test. [ctx-1][ctx-2]

### Recommended Operating Shape
- Use a staged test if a high-intent segment can be isolated and measured cleanly.
- Skip or compress the test if unit economics are already unacceptable and delay destroys margin.
- Avoid a segment test if transaction volume is too low to generate actionable signal in the decision window.
- Pair any price increase with clearer packaging so value communication is not fully confounded with price.
- Precommit rollback or expansion triggers before launch. [ctx-1][ctx-2]

### Strategic Rationale
Under partial data, the objective is not always to move slowly; it is to choose the cheapest path to a decision that is still good enough for the business context. Sometimes that means experimentation. Sometimes it means a monitored full reprice or a rapid benchmarking sprint. The key distinction is whether the cost of inaction exceeds the cost of learning. ## Pass/Fail Readiness

### Pass Conditions
- The company can isolate a test cohort without material leakage.
- Expected volume is sufficient to reach an interpretable result in the decision window.
- Baselines exist for conversion, churn, win rate, and sales-cycle length.
- The business can tolerate temporary volatility while learning.
- Pricing and packaging can be changed together with a consistent field narrative. [ctx-1]

### Fail Conditions
- Margin pressure makes delay materially more expensive than a monitored broad change.
- Competitor timing makes a slow test strategically costly.
- Volume is too low for a segment test to produce usable signal.
- Systems cannot isolate cohorts or monitor outcomes reliably.
- Leadership expects certainty from a test design that cannot realistically provide it. [ctx-1][ctx-2]
