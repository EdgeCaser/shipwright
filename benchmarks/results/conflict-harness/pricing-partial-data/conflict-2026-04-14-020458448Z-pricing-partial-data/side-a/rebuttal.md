## Claim Under Attack
Under partial market data, the best recommendation is a staged, reversible pricing test rather than a full repricing.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim presents staged testing as universally optimal under uncertainty, but provides no evidence or reasoning for when a staged test would be the wrong move. Several common conditions undermine it: (1) if the company is severely underpriced and losing money per unit, delay costs real margin daily; (2) if competitors are about to reprice, a slow test cedes positioning; (3) if the product has low transaction volume, a segment test will never reach statistical significance within a useful timeframe. The artifact treats 'partial data' as a monolithic condition warranting one response, when the optimal action depends on which data is missing and what the cost of inaction is. The citations ctx-1 and ctx-2 are self-referential context markers with no external evidence supporting the claim that staged testing dominates other approaches (e.g., conjoint analysis, competitive benchmarking, or even a confident full reprice with monitoring).
