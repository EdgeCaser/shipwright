# Pricing Strategy Recommendation Under Partial Market Data

## Decision Frame

The core decision is whether to act on a pricing change now, defer pending further data collection, or move forward with a staged approach that preserves optionality. This recommendation addresses that choice directly.

**Decision type:** Reversible vs. irreversible matters here. A price increase that alienates customers at scale is harder to walk back than a targeted pilot. A price decrease trains buyer expectations and is costly to reverse. The approach taken must account for the asymmetry of downside risk on each direction.

**Framing constraint:** No external market data has been provided in this case packet. This recommendation is therefore a decision-process artifact — it specifies what the pricing team must resolve before a number can be defensibly set, and what conditions make a partial-data move acceptable versus reckless.

---

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

The following gaps are material to any pricing recommendation. Each represents a decision blocker or a required assumption that must be made explicit:

1. **Competitive price floor/ceiling** — Without knowing where comparable offerings are priced, any proposed number is untethered. This is the most critical missing input.

2. **Willingness-to-pay signal** — No customer survey data, cohort elasticity analysis, or win/loss records appear in the packet. Any price move is a bet on an unstated assumption about buyer sensitivity.

3. **Current margin structure** — Whether a price change is necessary for unit economics vs. aspirational for revenue growth changes the urgency and acceptable risk profile entirely.

4. **Churn trigger threshold** — At what price point do current customers defect? Without this, a price increase could inadvertently clear the customer base rather than grow revenue.

5. **Segment heterogeneity** — If buyer segments have meaningfully different price sensitivity, a single-price strategy forfeits value from high-willingness segments while risking defection from low-willingness ones.

**Evidence status:** The case packet contains zero evidence entries. All claims in this artifact are structural/methodological. No quantitative specifics are introduced.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

A pricing change recommendation is **ready to execute** only if the following conditions are met:

| Condition | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| At least one competitive price reference | Unknown | Not in packet |
| Internal unit economics known | Unknown | Not in packet |
| Directional WTP signal (any source) | Unknown | Not in packet |
| Reversibility mechanism defined | Required | Pilot scope, rollback clause, or sunset trigger |
| Decision owner identified | Required | Pricing unilateral vs. cross-functional sign-off |

**Current readiness verdict: Not ready for a committed price change.** The data environment does not support a defensible number. What it does support is a structured data-collection sprint with a fixed decision deadline.

**Acceptable partial-data move:** A time-bounded pricing pilot on a defined sub-segment is acceptable if: (a) the pilot size is small enough that rollback is feasible, (b) a specific learning objective is attached (e.g., "measure conversion rate change at +15%"), and (c) the decision deadline is set before the pilot bleeds into de facto full rollout.

## Recommended Next Artifact

**Produce:** A Pricing Readiness Brief — a one-page document that resolves the five evidence gaps above before a strategy recommendation is finalized.

**Contents of the Pricing Readiness Brief:**
- Three competitive price references with source and date
- Internal margin floor (minimum acceptable price)
- One directional WTP signal (survey, win/loss, or cohort analysis)
- Proposed pilot scope and rollback definition
- Named decision owner and sign-off date

**Hand-off condition:** Once the Pricing Readiness Brief is complete, the receiving team can produce a committed pricing recommendation with a specific number, rationale, and risk-adjusted scenario analysis. That artifact supersedes this one.

**Timeline guidance:** This brief should be completable within one sprint if the data exists internally. If it requires primary research, a scope decision is needed first — the cost of the research must be weighed against the value of the pricing decision.
