# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
A broad permanent pricing change is not justified because the case packet contains no evidence to support any specific pricing direction.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B treats absence of evidence in the case packet as evidence of absence — concluding 'no change justified' — but this conflates a data-collection problem with a strategic conclusion. The claim that no pricing direction is supportable is itself a directional claim requiring evidence: specifically, that current pricing is not already misaligned. Side B never establishes a baseline (current pricing performance, churn signals, sales friction) that would license the hold-current posture. Holding price is a decision with real cost if pricing is already broken; treating it as a neutral default without grounding is the same evidentiary gap Side B attributes to a change recommendation.
