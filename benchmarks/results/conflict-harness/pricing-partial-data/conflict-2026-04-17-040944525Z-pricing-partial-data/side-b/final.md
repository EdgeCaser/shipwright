# Revised artifact

## Decision Frame
Recommendation: do not make a broad permanent pricing commitment on the basis of this packet. The packet does not establish whether current pricing is sound or broken, so it also does not support treating a hold-current posture as a fully validated endpoint. If a decision can wait, defer the permanent pricing decision until the missing internal and market inputs are assembled. If a decision cannot wait, use only a reversible, tightly scoped interim move rather than a durable price or packaging reset.

Rationale: the case packet contains no evidence records. That means the packet does not support a grounded recommendation for a price increase, a price decrease, a packaging shift, or a confident keep-as-is conclusion. The most decision-useful recommendation under these conditions is to avoid converting uncertainty into an irreversible commitment.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The key missing inputs are the decision objective, current pricing performance, signs of sales friction or retention risk, customer willingness to pay, and competitor positioning. Without those inputs, the unresolved question is not which exact price is best, but which pricing direction, if any, is warranted.

Because the packet is evidence-empty, any strong directional claim would outrun the record. That includes both "change now" and "current pricing is fine."

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass: this recommendation is actionable now if leadership accepts a risk-limiting posture and is willing either to defer the permanent pricing decision or to use only an interim reversible measure.

Fail: this is not ready for a full rollout decision if leadership wants a confident recommendation on final price levels, durable packaging changes, or a defended keep-current decision. The packet does not support that level of commitment.

## Recommended Next Artifact
Create a pricing decision memo that answers four questions: what must be decided now, what internal pricing-performance evidence already exists, what external market inputs are missing, and what reversible interim options are available until a permanent decision is supportable.
