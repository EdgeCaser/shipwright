# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
A price move of ≤5% is within typical demand noise tolerance and functions as a learning investment that generates elasticity signal without high risk of demand destruction.

## Evidence Or Reason
The artifact provides no evidence, citations, or product-context qualifiers for the specific ≤5% threshold, yet treats it as generally safe. That threshold can fail badly in categories with annual contracts, high price salience, procurement review, or psychologically important price points, where even small changes can materially affect conversion or churn. Under this case's explicitly partial data conditions, presenting ≤5% as 'typical noise tolerance' is an unsupported generalization rather than a defensible inference.
