# Revised artifact

## Recommendation
Do not commit immediately to an upward pricing move. Under partial market data, the most defensible recommendation is a controlled pricing decision process: gather the minimum evidence needed to choose among a modest increase, hold, or targeted decrease for the in-scope segment, then launch only a reversible cohort-based test. If leadership must act before fuller research is available, prefer a narrow new-customer-only pilot with pre-set stop criteria rather than a broad repricing. [ctx-1]

This revision changes the core posture from directionally specific to directionally conditional. The case provides no competitor benchmarks, elasticity evidence, segment willingness-to-pay data, or win/loss signals. In that condition, the highest-confidence decision is not "raise price," but "avoid irreversible pricing exposure until direction is supported by minimal observable evidence." [ctx-1]

## Decision Frame
The decision is which pricing posture best balances revenue opportunity, downside risk, and learning speed when market evidence is incomplete. The recommended choice is a staged pricing decision: first establish minimum decision criteria, then choose direction, then test only within a reversible scope.

## Operating Recommendation
- Define a minimum evidence gate before launch: current conversion, ASP, discounting, objection reasons, renewal/churn baseline, and any available competitor references.
- If early evidence suggests underpricing, test a modest increase on new customers only in one package or segment.
- If evidence is mixed or unavailable, hold price and improve instrumentation before changing headline price.
- If evidence suggests price resistance or weak differentiation, test targeted relief through packaging or discount structure before changing the base list price upward.
- Pre-commit rollback thresholds so the business can reverse quickly if conversion, sales-cycle length, or objection rates worsen materially. [ctx-1]

## Why This Is Decision-Useful Now
A directionally agnostic staged approach is more defensible than either freezing indefinitely or asserting an increase without support.

- Freezing indefinitely preserves risk but produces little learning.
- Raising price immediately without evidence introduces avoidable direction risk.
- A staged test approach preserves optionality, creates observable learning, and limits blast radius if the initial direction proves wrong. [ctx-1]

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- No competitor price benchmark is available, so relative positioning is unknown.
- No elasticity or conversion sensitivity data is available, so price direction and magnitude are both uncertain.
- No segment-level willingness-to-pay evidence is available, so the right move may differ by cohort.
- No churn or renewal sensitivity data is available, so installed-base repricing risk cannot be estimated.
- No win/loss or objection data is available, so current price pressure may already be harming pipeline.
- No information is provided on product differentiation, sales motion, or contract structure, all of which materially affect the correct pricing posture. [ctx-1]

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if:
- The organization can collect at least a minimum baseline on conversion, ASP, discounting, and objection reasons.
- Leadership accepts that price direction is contingent on evidence rather than predetermined.
- The initial change can be isolated to a new-customer cohort, package, or segment.
- Stop and rollback thresholds are defined before launch.

Fail if:
- The business must apply a pricing change broadly without baseline measurement.
- Leadership requires an increase regardless of early evidence.
- Sales and support cannot execute a controlled cohort test consistently.
- The company cannot observe early pipeline or renewal effects quickly enough to manage downside.

## Recommended Next Artifact
Create a pricing decision memo that includes:
- in-scope segment and package
- minimum evidence gate required before choosing direction
- explicit decision rules for increase, hold, or targeted decrease
- test design for the first cohort
- success, stop, and rollback thresholds
- review checkpoints at 30 and 60 days

That memo should be the gating artifact before any launch.
