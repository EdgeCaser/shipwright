# Pricing Strategy Recommendation

## Recommendation
Adopt a staged pricing change rather than a full repricing now: keep list prices stable for the current base plan, introduce a higher-value premium tier, and run controlled packaging and discount tests before any broad price increase. This preserves revenue upside while limiting irreversible churn risk under incomplete market data.

## Decision Frame

### Objective
Improve monetization without taking avoidable retention or conversion risk while market evidence is incomplete.

### Recommendation
1. Hold core entry pricing constant in the near term.
2. Add or sharpen a premium tier with differentiated value, not just higher price.
3. Use targeted experiments on new prospects, selected segments, or renewal cohorts before changing headline pricing broadly.
4. Tighten discount policy and packaging first, because those typically create monetization gains with lower customer shock than an immediate across-the-board price hike.

### Why This Is The Best Near-Term Move
- Partial data weakens confidence in willingness-to-pay estimates.
- A broad list-price increase is hard to reverse cleanly if conversion or retention drops.
- Packaging and tiering create learning while still capturing upside from high-value customers.
- Controlled tests generate the missing evidence needed for a later, firmer pricing decision.

### Practical Plan
- Near term: define value metrics, premium feature fences, and discount guardrails.
- Test phase: run A/B or cohort-based offers on limited traffic or selected accounts.
- Decision gate: expand only if conversion, expansion revenue, and churn guardrails stay within target.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- No direct competitive pricing evidence is available in the packet.
- No customer willingness-to-pay research is provided.
- No segmentation data is provided to identify which customers are least price-sensitive.
- No baseline conversion, churn, or expansion metrics are provided to set safe guardrails.
- It is unclear whether current monetization problems come from price level, packaging design, or discount leakage.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

### Pass Conditions
- Baseline funnel and retention metrics are available.
- Clear premium-tier value differentiation is defined.
- Test cohorts can be isolated operationally.
- Success and rollback thresholds are set before launch.

### Fail Conditions
- The business cannot isolate experiments from the full customer base.
- Feature differentiation is too weak to justify a premium tier.
- Revenue pressure is so immediate that the team would skip testing discipline.
- Instrumentation is insufficient to measure conversion, churn, and expansion reliably.

### Readiness Verdict
Conditionally pass for a staged test-led pricing strategy. Not ready for a universal price increase.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A pricing experiment plan covering target segments, hypotheses, offer variants, success metrics, guardrails, sample requirements, and rollback criteria.
