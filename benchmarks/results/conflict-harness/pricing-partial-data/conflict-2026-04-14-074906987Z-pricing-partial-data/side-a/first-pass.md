# Pricing Strategy Recommendation Under Partial Market Data

## Recommendation
Adopt a staged pricing change rather than a full repricing. Keep current list pricing in place for existing customers, introduce a limited-scope test for new customers only, and evaluate 2 to 3 pricing/package variants against explicit guardrails before broader rollout. Under partial market data, the objective is not to find the theoretically optimal price immediately; it is to reduce downside risk while learning fast enough to support a confident follow-on decision.

## Proposed Strategy
1. Test on new business only for a fixed period.
2. Compare one control and two variants:
   - Control: current pricing and packaging
   - Variant A: moderate price increase with no packaging change
   - Variant B: same headline price with packaging/value metric adjustment
3. Grandfather existing customers until evidence shows expansion and retention effects are acceptable.
4. Set stop conditions before launch:
   - Material drop in conversion beyond tolerance
   - Sales cycle elongation beyond tolerance
   - Early signs of higher churn risk or discount pressure
5. Require both quantitative and qualitative readout:
   - Win rate, conversion, ACV, time-to-close
   - Sales objections, willingness-to-pay signals, buyer confusion

## Why This Is The Right Move
A partial-data environment does not justify a permanent pricing reset. It does justify a disciplined market-learning motion. Pricing changes are high leverage but also hard to reverse cleanly once they affect customer expectations, sales behavior, and renewal conversations. A controlled test preserves option value, contains revenue risk, and generates the evidence needed for a stronger second decision.

## Decision Frame
The decision is whether to make a pricing change now despite incomplete market evidence. The recommended choice is a bounded experiment, not a full rollout. This balances three goals: protect near-term revenue, improve pricing confidence, and avoid locking the business into a poorly supported pricing architecture. The threshold for success is not simply higher price realization; it is improved monetization without unacceptable damage to conversion, sales efficiency, or retention risk.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
Key missing evidence includes competitor price-position context, customer willingness-to-pay by segment, elasticity by deal size, likely discount behavior under new pricing, and retention impact at renewal. There is also no direct evidence in the packet on current pricing performance, segment mix, or packaging fit. These gaps mean any recommendation for a broad rollout would be overconfident. The current recommendation is therefore contingent on a short-cycle test designed to close those gaps.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass for a limited experiment: yes. There is enough context to justify a risk-bounded test with predefined guardrails.

Pass for full market rollout: no. The evidence base is insufficient for a durable pricing decision across the full customer base.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A pricing experiment brief. It should specify target segments, variants, success metrics, guardrails, sample requirements, sales enablement changes, customer communication rules, and the decision rule for expand, revise, or stop.
