# Pricing Strategy Recommendation

## Decision Frame
Recommend a controlled pricing move rather than a full repricing. Hold core list prices for the current cycle, introduce a limited test on one segment or package boundary, and use packaging/discount controls to improve monetization while market evidence is incomplete. This is the highest-utility decision under partial data because it preserves upside learning while limiting broad revenue and conversion risk.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The case packet provides no market evidence, customer willingness-to-pay data, win/loss pricing patterns, competitive price map, elasticity estimates, or segment-level retention risk. The key unresolved questions are which customer segment has the lowest price sensitivity, whether current underpricing is material, and whether the larger opportunity is price increase, packaging redesign, or discount discipline.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass for a bounded experiment decision. Fail for a market-wide pricing reset. The available information is sufficient to authorize a time-boxed test with explicit guardrails, but insufficient to support a broad permanent change across the full customer base.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A pricing experiment brief with target segment, hypothesis, test cell design, success thresholds, guardrails, and data collection plan.
