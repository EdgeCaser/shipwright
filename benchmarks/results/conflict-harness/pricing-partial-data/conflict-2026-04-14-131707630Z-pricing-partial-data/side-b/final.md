# Revised artifact

## Decision Frame
Do not approve a market-wide pricing reset on the current record. Approve a staged pricing decision instead: keep current core list prices in place, immediately assess whether a bounded pricing experiment is operationally feasible, and only run that experiment if minimum feasibility gates are met. If those gates are not met, use the same cycle to close the data gap through faster non-experimental inputs such as transaction-cohort analysis, discount leakage review, targeted willingness-to-pay interviews, and competitive proxy benchmarking. This is the most decision-useful path under partial data because it limits downside risk from an uninformed repricing while avoiding the circular mistake of prescribing an experiment that the organization may not be able to execute well.

Minimum experiment-feasibility gates: a clearly targetable segment, enough sample volume to observe directional movement in conversion or expansion, instrumentation to measure the outcome, and a rollback path if conversion or retention deteriorates. Without those conditions, the right decision is not "test anyway" but "collect the fastest credible evidence short of a full rollout."

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The case packet provides no market evidence, customer willingness-to-pay data, win/loss pricing patterns, competitive price map, elasticity estimates, transaction-cohort behavior, or proof that pricing experiments are technically and operationally feasible. The key unresolved questions are: which segment is least price-sensitive, whether current monetization leakage comes from price level versus packaging versus discounting, whether internal data can support directional inference quickly, and whether the business has sufficient sample size and instrumentation for a bounded test.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass for a staged evidence plan and for an experiment only if feasibility gates are met. Fail for a broad permanent pricing reset and fail for an experiment mandate that assumes capabilities not yet established. The current information is sufficient to choose a low-regret decision path, but not sufficient to choose the final pricing move itself.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A pricing evidence brief that first answers experiment feasibility, then specifies either: 1. a bounded pricing experiment design with target segment, hypothesis, success thresholds, guardrails, and rollback plan, or 2. a rapid non-experimental evidence plan covering transaction analysis, discount audit, customer interviews, and competitive proxy benchmarking.
