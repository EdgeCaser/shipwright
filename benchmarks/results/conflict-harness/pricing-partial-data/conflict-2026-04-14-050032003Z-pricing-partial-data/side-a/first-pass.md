# Pricing Strategy Recommendation

## Decision Frame
Recommend a staged pricing change rather than a full repricing: keep list prices stable for the current base plan, introduce a higher-priced premium tier with 10-20% uplift versus the current anchor, and use controlled sales or self-serve experiments to validate willingness to pay before migrating the broader catalog. Under partial market data, this is the highest-information, lowest-regret move because it preserves revenue continuity while creating a clean test of price elasticity, feature-package fit, and buyer segmentation.

Decision logic:
- Avoid broad across-the-board price increases until elasticity and competitive parity are better evidenced.
- Use packaging before pricing where possible: move high-value or advanced capabilities into the premium tier so the monetization event is tied to clearer value.
- Grandfather existing customers for a fixed period to reduce churn risk and protect account trust.
- Define success on three measures: upgrade conversion, net revenue retention impact, and win-rate/churn stability.

Suggested first-pass operating plan:
- Current plan: unchanged for existing customers and default low-risk entry point.
- New premium tier: 10-20% above current effective price, targeted at highest-value segment.
- Enterprise/custom deals: preserve negotiation room; do not hard-code broad increases until test data returns.
- Review window: 30-45 days for initial signal, 60-90 days for retention and expansion effects.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The recommendation is constrained by missing market inputs. The key unknowns are:
- Competitive price benchmarks by segment and package level.
- Customer willingness to pay by persona, company size, and urgency of problem.
- Historical elasticity from prior pricing, discounting, or packaging changes.
- Margin sensitivity and support/delivery cost by tier.
- Churn risk for legacy customers exposed to new packaging boundaries.

Evidence gaps that most affect the decision:
- Whether current pricing is below market, at market, or already stretched.
- Whether premium features are strong enough to support a higher anchor.
- Whether buyers perceive enough differentiation between tiers.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass for immediate execution if:
- The team can launch a premium tier or controlled pricing test without billing-system disruption.
- Existing customers can be grandfathered or contractually insulated.
- Core metrics are instrumented for conversion, churn, expansion, discount rate, and sales win rate.
- Product and GTM teams agree on feature boundaries and positioning.

Fail for broad repricing now if:
- Billing or entitlement systems cannot support segmented rollout.
- There is no reliable way to measure conversion and retention by cohort.
- The proposed premium tier lacks clearly differentiated value.
- The business is currently exposed to churn or pipeline fragility that cannot absorb pricing risk.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A pricing strategy brief with:
- Tier design and packaging logic.
- Test plan by segment and channel.
- Success metrics and stop/go thresholds.
- Competitive benchmark table.
- Customer research plan for willingness-to-pay validation.
