# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Keeping the base plan stable and introducing a premium tier with a 10-20% uplift is the most decision-useful near-term move under uncertainty.

## Evidence Or Reason
The 10-20% uplift figure is asserted without any evidential basis — no willingness-to-pay data, no competitive benchmarks, no historical elasticity analysis. Under the scenario's own premise of partial market data, this number is indistinguishable from a guess. If current pricing is already 30% below market, 10-20% leaves significant revenue on the table; if pricing is at or above market, even 10% risks stalling adoption of the new tier entirely. The artifact acknowledges these exact unknowns in its Evidence Gaps section (competitive price position, WTP by persona, whether current pricing is below/at/above market) yet still commits to a specific numeric range in its recommendation. A decision-useful artifact under genuine uncertainty would either present the range as explicitly contingent on assumptions that must be validated first, or propose the test without anchoring on a number that carries false precision.
