# Pricing Strategy Recommendation Under Partial Market Data

## Recommendation
Adopt a staged pricing change rather than a full repricing now: keep the current list price in place for existing customers, introduce a modest increase for new customers only, and pair it with tighter packaging and discount controls. This preserves downside protection while generating real willingness-to-pay data before a broader rollout.

## Rationale
With no market evidence in the packet, the decision should optimize for reversibility and learning speed rather than theoretical price maximization. A limited new-customer price increase is the highest-utility move because it tests elasticity without immediately exposing the installed base to churn risk. Packaging and discount discipline should do part of the monetization work because they are often less destructive than a blunt headline price jump.

## Proposed Move
- Raise entry pricing moderately for new customers only.
- Preserve legacy pricing for current customers until retention and conversion effects are measured.
- Reduce ad hoc discounting by requiring explicit approval above a defined threshold.
- Clarify plan boundaries so higher-value usage is captured in higher tiers.
- Set a short review window with predeclared rollback criteria.

## Decision Frame
Recommendation: run a controlled, reversible price increase for new customers only, combined with packaging cleanup and discount controls.

Why this decision: the available evidence is materially incomplete, so the correct strategy is to trade some upside for lower regret, faster learning, and clearer causal readout.

What would change this recommendation: credible evidence of severe price sensitivity, major competitive undercutting, or a retention model showing even small pricing changes would materially increase churn risk.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- No direct competitor pricing or packaging benchmarks were provided.
- No win/loss data exists to estimate current price pressure in active deals.
- No conversion funnel data exists to model sensitivity for new buyers.
- No cohort retention or expansion data exists to estimate downstream risk.
- No customer-segment breakdown exists, so differentiated pricing by segment cannot yet be defended.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass conditions:
- The business can isolate new-customer performance after the price change.
- Sales or self-serve flows can enforce the new packaging and discount guardrails.
- Leadership agrees in advance on rollback thresholds and review timing.

Fail conditions:
- Pricing cannot be segmented between new and existing customers.
- Discounting is too uncontrolled to preserve experiment integrity.
- The organization expects a definitive long-term pricing architecture from this move rather than a bounded learning step.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A pricing experiment brief covering target segments, proposed price points, package definitions, discount policy, success metrics, guardrails, review cadence, and explicit rollback triggers.
