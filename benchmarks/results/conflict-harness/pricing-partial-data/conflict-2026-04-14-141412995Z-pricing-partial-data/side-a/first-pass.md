# Pricing Strategy Recommendation

## Decision Frame
Recommend a staged pricing change rather than a full rollout under current evidence scarcity. Adopt a limited-scope test with a simple packaging structure, a moderate price increase on the highest-value tier, and explicit guardrails on conversion, retention, and sales-cycle friction. This is decision-useful because it preserves upside while containing downside when market willingness-to-pay, competitor reactions, and segment elasticity are only partially known.

Recommended action:
- Hold entry pricing stable to protect top-of-funnel volume.
- Increase price on the tier with the clearest differentiated value.
- If enterprise or custom plans exist, use quote-based pricing to absorb uncertainty instead of publishing an aggressive list-price shift.
- Run the change first on a bounded segment, new customers only, with a pre-declared review window.

Rationale:
- Partial market data is usually sufficient to reject extreme moves, but not sufficient to justify irreversible broad changes.
- Stable entry pricing reduces acquisition risk while allowing monetization learning higher in the stack.
- A bounded test produces cleaner elasticity evidence than a portfolio-wide change with many confounders.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
Critical unknowns remain:
- True willingness-to-pay by segment.
- Competitor effective pricing after discounts, not just list prices.
- Sensitivity of conversion to packaging complexity versus price level.
- Retention impact for customers exposed to higher renewal or expansion pricing.
- Sales and support burden created by exceptions, grandfathering, or custom quotes.

Implication of the gaps:
- Do not commit to a company-wide repricing.
- Do not combine multiple major changes at once, such as packaging redesign plus seat minimums plus annual-only discounts.
- Treat any forecasted revenue uplift as provisional until test data confirms both monetization lift and acceptable funnel health.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass for controlled launch if all are true:
- The proposed change is limited to one clear tier or segment.
- Success metrics and guardrails are defined before launch.
- Baseline conversion, expansion, and retention metrics are available for comparison.
- Commercial teams have a clear exception policy and positioning script.
- Instrumentation can attribute outcomes to the pricing test.

Fail for broad rollout if any are true:
- No segment-level baseline exists.
- Packaging and pricing are both being changed without a way to isolate effects.
- Leadership expects immediate full-funnel revenue certainty from partial data.
- There is no rollback plan or no customer communication plan.

## Recommended Next Artifact
Create a pricing experiment brief. It should specify:
- Test audience and exclusion rules.
- Current versus proposed price points.
- Primary metric: net revenue per new account.
- Guardrails: conversion rate, early retention, sales-cycle length, and discounting behavior.
- Decision thresholds for expand, hold, or revert.
- Required qualitative follow-up from prospects, sales, and customer success.
