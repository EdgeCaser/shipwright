# Pricing Strategy Recommendation Under Partial Market Data

## Decision Frame
Recommend a guarded, reversible pricing change rather than a full repricing. Keep the current list price in market-facing materials, introduce a limited-scope packaging and discount restructure, and run a time-bounded test on 1-2 customer segments before any global rollout.

Rationale:
- Partial market data is not strong enough to justify a broad price increase or a category-level repositioning.
- A packaging-first move improves monetization with lower downside risk than changing headline price.
- A controlled test preserves learning velocity while limiting revenue and conversion damage.

Recommended decision:
- Hold base price for existing core plan.
- Create a higher-value tier or add-on bundle for customers with clearer willingness-to-pay signals.
- Tighten ad hoc discounting with explicit approval thresholds.
- Test the new package on a narrow segment with pre-defined stop/go metrics.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
Key missing evidence:
- Competitor pricing coverage is incomplete, so relative market position is uncertain.
- Willingness-to-pay by segment is not validated.
- Price sensitivity for new buyers versus existing customers is unknown.
- Sales-cycle and win-rate impact from packaging complexity is unmeasured.
- Expansion and churn effects are not yet modeled.

Implication:
These gaps make a full list-price reset too risky. The recommendation therefore favors an experiment that generates decision-grade evidence.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if all are true:
- The test segment is clearly defined.
- Baseline metrics exist for conversion, average selling price, discount rate, churn, and expansion.
- Finance and sales agree on guardrails and rollback conditions.
- The new packaging can be implemented without billing or contract friction.

Fail if any are true:
- The business is depending on this change to close a near-term revenue gap immediately.
- Systems cannot support segment-specific packaging or rollback.
- Stakeholders want a company-wide price move before test results are available.
- No owner is assigned for experiment design and readout.

## Recommended Next Artifact
Create a pricing experiment brief.

It should include:
- Hypothesis and target segment
- Current versus proposed package structure
- Success metrics and guardrails
- Rollout and rollback rules
- Evidence plan for competitor checks, customer interviews, and win/loss review
