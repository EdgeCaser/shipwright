# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
OpenAI should preserve nonprofit control because governance credibility is a strategic asset in frontier AI, not merely a symbolic mission feature.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B asserts governance credibility drives competitive capacity with regulators, enterprise buyers, and civil society, but provides no empirical evidence that nonprofit control has materially influenced any of these outcomes. The memo acknowledges in its own Unknowns section that 'whether nonprofit control can credibly constrain major commercialization decisions in practice' remains unresolved. OpenAI's most visible governance failure — the 2023 board crisis that nearly destroyed the company — occurred under nonprofit control. If nonprofit oversight could not prevent that instability, the claim that it produces durable 'governance credibility' is asserted but undemonstrated. The memo conflates the legal structure of nonprofit control with the functional outcome of credible governance, without showing the causal link between them.
