# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
OpenAI's mission-first credibility with regulators, safety researchers, and enterprise buyers is a structural competitive moat that cannot be rebuilt once abandoned — making nonprofit control preservation strategically superior to for-profit conversion despite capital access friction.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim overreaches well beyond the case packet. The packet says keeping nonprofit control may preserve mission legitimacy, safety credibility, and political resilience, but it does not show that these audiences currently confer a durable competitive moat, that the moat is materially stronger than what a PBC alone could preserve, or that it would be irrecoverable after a governance change. The memo itself concedes key gaps on regulatory goodwill, talent-market behavior, and whether nonprofit control has ever constrained commercialization in practice. Without evidence that buyers, regulators, or researchers make consequential decisions based specifically on nonprofit control, the asserted moat is still a hypothesis rather than a demonstrated strategic advantage.
