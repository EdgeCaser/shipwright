# Launch Decision Memo

## Decision Frame
Do not commit to a full product launch at the conference in 3 weeks. Reset the external plan to a staged path: use the conference for a forward-looking announcement only if the message can be kept truthful and narrow, preserve Q1 customer conversations by immediately reframing sales commitments around access timing and scope, and anchor the actual general launch decision to the engineering 8-week delivery path.

This is the least damaging option because the current constraints are mutually incompatible as stated. Treating all three as fixed creates a credibility failure somewhere: product delivery, customer commitments, or executive messaging.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The packet does not establish what was promised to customers, what level of product completeness is required for launch, or whether the conference announcement can be limited to roadmap intent rather than immediate availability. It also does not clarify whether the 8-week engineering estimate is for a minimum viable release or a broader launch bar. These gaps matter because they determine whether a staged announcement is viable or whether even that would create avoidable risk.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if leadership aligns on one external narrative, sales is given a corrected customer message immediately, and the conference appearance is constrained to claims the team can support without implying availability inside 3 weeks. Pass if the 8-week engineering estimate is accepted as the operating date for shipped readiness unless new delivery evidence appears.

Fail if the organization continues to treat all three demands as non-negotiable, if sales keeps existing commitments unchanged, or if the conference plan implies launch readiness that engineering does not support.

## Recommended Next Artifact
Produce a one-page launch alignment brief that defines: the external message for the conference, the customer-facing sales script for committed accounts, the internal launch date assumption, the exact scope of what can be shown or promised before general availability, and the single decision owner for further tradeoff calls.
