# Revised artifact

## Decision Frame
Do not publicly commit to a full general launch at the conference in 3 weeks based on the current record. Instead, force an immediate decision on launch scope: either validate that engineering can support a narrower, truthfully described launch within 3 weeks, or reset the external plan around a staged announcement now and a later shipped launch decision.

The core issue is not simply that engineering said "8 weeks." It is that the organization is currently operating with three conflicting non-negotiable positions and no shared definition of what "launch" means. Until leadership resolves whether the conference event is meant to announce roadmap intent, limited access, or general availability, any hard public commitment creates avoidable credibility risk.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The packet does not establish what the 8-week estimate includes, whether scope can be narrowed, whether additional resourcing is feasible, or what sales has already promised customers. It also does not define whether the CEO's conference objective requires shipment, a demo, or only an announcement. These are the key gaps because they determine whether a limited launch path is real or whether the only defensible option is to defer launch claims.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if leadership resolves the definition of launch immediately, engineering revalidates timing against that scoped definition, and sales and executive messaging are updated to match the same external promise.

Pass if any conference statement is explicitly limited to what can be supported truthfully in 3 weeks.

Fail if the company continues to use one word, "launch," to describe different outcomes across engineering, sales, and the CEO.

Fail if public or customer commitments are made before scope, readiness, and ownership are aligned.

## Recommended Next Artifact
Produce a launch alignment brief that answers five points: the exact launch type under consideration, the engineering scope behind the current estimate, the smallest shippable scope that could plausibly support the conference objective, the corrected customer message for sales, and the single executive owner for the final call.
