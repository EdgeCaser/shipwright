# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Nissan's financial collapse eliminated the real-world option value of independence, making the Honda subsidiary structure the higher-expected-value path despite its governance costs.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible evidence establishes severe underperformance, but it does not establish that independence had no viable option value or that the Honda-led subsidiary was the higher-expected-value alternative. The artifact itself admits key missing facts on liquidity runway, subsidiary governance terms, Honda's integration intent, and alternative partners. Without those decision-critical variables, it cannot support the stronger conclusion that Nissan had to accept subordination rather than pursue an independent turnaround, outside capital, or a different alliance structure.
