# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Honda's commercial incentives were to maintain Nissan's operational viability for scale benefits, so subsidiary status would not automatically produce asset stripping and was more protective than independent deterioration.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim overreaches beyond the packet. The visible evidence says Honda wanted more control, analysts saw the deal as a scale/survival play, and the talks collapsed partly over balance-of-power disputes and Nissan's resistance to deeper cuts. None of that demonstrates that Honda would preserve Nissan's operational footprint rather than rationalize plants, brands, headcount, or product programs after gaining parent control. 'Scale benefits' is compatible with aggressive consolidation, and the memo provides no concrete governance terms, integration commitments, or protections showing Nissan would be more protected inside the structure than outside it.
