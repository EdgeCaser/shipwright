## Claim Under Attack
The most decision-useful PRD is a narrowly scoped handoff workflow focused on structured context transfer, ownership clarity, and measurable operational outcomes rather than broader workflow redesign.

## Evidence Or Reason
The artifact asserts 'decision usefulness' without establishing the decision context it is meant to serve. A PRD is decision-useful only relative to a specific decision (build vs. defer, scope A vs. scope B, fund vs. kill). Side B names no such decision, no decision-maker, and no threshold that would cause a 'build' vs. 'do not build' outcome. The 'Pass/Fail Readiness' section sets conditions (pilot team agreement, baseline definition) but does not surface who must meet those conditions or by when — making the readiness gate advisory rather than actionable. Without a named decision owner and a forcing event, 'decision-useful' is a quality claim without evidence.
