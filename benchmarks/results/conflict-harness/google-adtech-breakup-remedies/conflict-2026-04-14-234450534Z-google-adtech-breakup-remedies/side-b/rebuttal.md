# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Proactively conceding structural separation sets a precedent that weakens Google's negotiating posture across concurrent antitrust proceedings in search, app stores, and platform access.

## Evidence Or Reason
This is asserted as a cross-portfolio strategic certainty, but the visible record only establishes that Google faces broader antitrust pressure, not that a negotiated ad-tech separation would materially prejudice its legal or negotiating position in unrelated remedy phases. The claim skips the key causal proof: regulators and courts usually evaluate each market, record, and remedy on its own facts. Without concrete evidence that an ad-tech concession would be treated as an admission of structural liability elsewhere, this reads as speculative spillover rather than supported strategy analysis.
