# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Behavioral remedies — interoperability and bid-transparency — can address the competition harm the court identified without destroying integrated tooling that publishers and advertisers depend on.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim asserts efficacy, not just plausibility, but the visible artifact provides no evidence that interoperability and bid-transparency would actually cure the monopoly-maintenance harms the court found in publisher ad servers and ad exchanges. The case packet only says Google supports those remedies, not that they are sufficient, enforceable, or likely to satisfy the court or DOJ. It also leaves unproven the premise that customers materially depend on the current integration in a way that outweighs competition-restoring structural relief.
