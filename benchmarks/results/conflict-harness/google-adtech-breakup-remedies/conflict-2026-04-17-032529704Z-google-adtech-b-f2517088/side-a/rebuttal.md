- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Google should proactively move toward structural separation of key ad-tech assets rather than fight exclusively for behavioral remedies.

## Evidence Or Reason
The memo asserts that proactive separation improves Google's standing in the remedies phase and reduces regulatory overhang across adjacent matters, but cites no precedent where voluntary divestiture by a defendant during an active remedies proceeding produced measurably better outcomes versus a negotiated conduct decree. The case packet itself lists no such evidence. Without a comparable case — Microsoft (2001 consent decree), Meta/FTC, or otherwise — the causal claim that 'moving first' converts legal exposure into 'optionality' is speculative. The memo concedes the degree of benefit in adjacent antitrust matters 'remains uncertain,' which directly undermines the primary strategic rationale for accepting known costs (execution risk, revenue pressure, customer disruption) in exchange for unquantified spillover benefits.
