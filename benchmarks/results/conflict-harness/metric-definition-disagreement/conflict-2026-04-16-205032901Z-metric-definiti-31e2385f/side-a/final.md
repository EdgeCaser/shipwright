# Revised artifact

## Decision Frame
Leadership has already made the immediate decision purpose clear: it wants one number that can align finance, product, and growth. The recommendation is to standardize a single company-level active-user definition now for leadership reporting, while explicitly retaining finance and growth variants as subordinate companion metrics for their operating contexts.

The key decision is therefore not whether a single number is needed, but what kind of definition is appropriate for a company-level north star when the current candidates differ enough to imply growth, flatness, or decline. On the case packet alone, the defensible recommendation is to select the definition that best represents sustained product engagement rather than a function-specific reporting lens, and to prohibit reuse of the same metric name for incompatible definitions. That narrows the decision space without pretending the exact winning rule can be chosen from the evidence provided here.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The unresolved issue is not leadership intent; it is the underlying definition logic. The case packet does not specify:

- the rule used by finance, product, and growth to count an active user
- whether the 40% spread is driven by timing windows, event thresholds, identity rules, or inclusion and exclusion logic
- which candidate most faithfully reflects enduring product usage rather than a finance or growth convenience view

These gaps matter because they determine which existing definition can legitimately serve as the company-level metric and which should remain companion metrics.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if:

- leadership agrees to one company-level definition for executive reporting
- the three existing definitions are decomposed into their actual counting rules
- only one definition is allowed to use the company-level active-user label, with the others renamed as companion metrics

Fail if:

- incompatible definitions continue to be reported under the same label
- the company chooses a definition mainly because it changes the growth narrative rather than because it is the best company-level operating measure
- the definition differences are not broken down far enough to explain the observed spread

## Recommended Next Artifact
Create a metric adjudication memo that compares the finance, product, and growth definitions on four points: counting rule, intended use, source of divergence from the others, and whether it is fit for company-level reporting. End with a final designation of one leadership metric and two explicitly named companion metrics.
