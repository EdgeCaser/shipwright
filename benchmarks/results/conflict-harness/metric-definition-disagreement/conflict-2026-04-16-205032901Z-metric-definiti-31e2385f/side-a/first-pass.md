# North Star Metric Recommendation

## Decision Frame
Leadership should not force a single undifferentiated "active user" number until the company first decides what decision the metric is meant to serve. The recommendation is to adopt one canonical north star metric owned by product strategy, publish the finance and growth variants as labeled companion metrics, and require every leadership readout to state the definition explicitly. A single number is only decision-useful if it preserves meaning; in this case, the current 40% spread means collapsing the definitions into one unlabeled figure would create false precision and likely drive the wrong conclusion about whether the product is growing, flat, or declining.

The canonical choice should be the definition that best reflects repeat customer value creation and product usage, not the one that makes the trend look strongest or aligns most cleanly to reporting convenience. Finance and growth still need their own operational views, but those should not substitute for the company-level north star.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The case packet does not specify the three definitions, what behaviors each counts, or which business decisions each team is optimizing for. That leaves unresolved:

- which definition best tracks durable product value rather than acquisition or reporting artifacts
- whether one definition is materially more vulnerable to channel mix, seasonality, or accounting treatment
- how leadership intends to use the metric: board reporting, roadmap prioritization, forecast setting, or growth execution
- whether the 40% gap comes from timing windows, event thresholds, user identity rules, or inclusion/exclusion logic

These gaps do not block the governance recommendation to separate a canonical north star from companion metrics, but they do block naming the exact winning definition with high confidence.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if:

- the company agrees that the north star metric must answer a single primary question about product value creation
- each existing definition is documented in plain language with its counting rules and intended use
- leadership accepts that one canonical metric can coexist with other functional metrics without pretending they are interchangeable

Fail if:

- leadership insists on one unlabeled number regardless of decision purpose
- teams continue presenting incompatible definitions under the same metric name
- the chosen north star is selected mainly because it makes the business appear healthier, rather than because it is the best operating measure

## Recommended Next Artifact
Create a one-page metric decision memo with four fields for each candidate definition: what it measures, what decision it is for, what it excludes, and what failure mode it introduces. End the memo with a formal designation of:

- canonical north star metric
- finance companion metric
- growth companion metric
- standard labeling language for dashboards and exec reviews
