# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The immediate decision should be to establish a canonical definition through explicit metric governance rather than arbitrarily selecting one existing definition.

## Evidence Or Reason
Leadership has already asked for a single number — the artifact reframes that request as premature rather than answering it. The recommendation to stand up a governance process before committing to any definition is itself a decision with real costs: it defers the north star, extends the period of incompatible reporting, and may not be feasible on leadership's timeline. The artifact provides no evidence that governance scaffolding is faster or safer than picking the strongest candidate definition now and refining it. It asserts governance is required without acknowledging that a reasonable interim choice — even an imperfect one — can unblock downstream decisions while the formal process runs. The claim also conflates 'arbitrary selection' with 'selection without full documentation,' ignoring that one of the three definitions may already be obviously better-aligned to value delivery without a formal process to confirm it.
