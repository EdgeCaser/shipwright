# Revised artifact

## Decision Frame
Leadership has asked for a single number, but the case packet does not provide enough evidence to name any one of the three existing "active user" definitions as the permanent north star. The definitions are materially incompatible and produce opposite narratives about performance, so choosing one now as the enduring north star would embed an unresolved semantic dispute into executive decision-making.

Recommendation: do two things at once.

1. Name a temporary executive reporting number immediately so leadership has one number to use now.
2. Do not treat that interim number as the permanent north star until the company completes a short, explicit decision on the canonical definition.

This preserves decision velocity without pretending the underlying disagreement is resolved. The permanent north star should be whichever definition best represents product value and can be used consistently across finance, product, and growth, but the case packet does not contain enough detail to determine which current definition meets that bar.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The case packet does not describe the three definitions in operational terms, the behaviors they count, or why each function chose them. That prevents a grounded recommendation for which existing definition should become the permanent north star.

Key missing evidence:
- The exact logic for each current definition
- Which definition best reflects meaningful product value rather than a functional reporting need
- Which definition is most explainable and reproducible across teams
- How each option would affect historical trend interpretation, targets, and incentives
- Whether one current definition is already being used as the de facto executive reference point

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass:
- Leadership explicitly distinguishes between an interim single number and the permanent north star decision
- One owner is assigned to finalize the canonical definition
- The final metric choice is documented with clear inclusion and exclusion rules
- The company aligns on how historical reporting and targets will be handled after the decision

Fail:
- The interim number is presented as a settled north star without resolving the definition conflict
- A function's preferred definition is adopted permanently without explicit rationale
- Teams continue using incompatible local definitions while claiming executive alignment
- The company makes the permanent choice without documenting ownership, rules, and downstream reporting implications

## Recommended Next Artifact
Create a metric decision memo that does two jobs:
1. Declare the interim executive number and why it is temporary
2. Compare the three current definitions and recommend the permanent canonical north star

That memo should include the exact definitions, the selection criteria for the permanent choice, the recommendation, and the reporting transition plan. The immediate output is one temporary executive number for near-term use and one pending decision on the permanent north star.
