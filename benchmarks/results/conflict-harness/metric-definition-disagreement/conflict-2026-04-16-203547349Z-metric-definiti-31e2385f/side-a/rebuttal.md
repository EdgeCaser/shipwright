## Claim Under Attack
A provisional adoption of the Growth definition is the least-bad holding position if leadership requires an immediate number, because it uses the largest denominator, avoids false alarms during transition, and preserves the other two definitions as diagnostics without foreclosing them.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim introduces a concrete fallback recommendation without visible support for any of its key premises. The artifact provides no evidence that the Growth definition is actually the largest denominator in this case, that a larger denominator reduces decision error rather than just masking deterioration, or that external benchmarking conventions favor Growth's definition. It also conflicts with the artifact's own framing that the core issue is strategic meaning, not denominator size. If the definitions imply materially different user populations, choosing the broadest one as a temporary default could systematically understate churn or product-value loss rather than serve as a neutral holding position.
