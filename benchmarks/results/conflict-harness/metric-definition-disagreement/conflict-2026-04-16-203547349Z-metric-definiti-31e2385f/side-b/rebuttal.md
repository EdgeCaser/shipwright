# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
A single leadership metric should be adopted, but the existing finance, product, and growth definitions should remain as labeled companion metrics rather than competing headline numbers.

## Evidence Or Reason
The artifact asserts that retaining all three definitions as 'labeled companion metrics' resolves the problem, but provides no evidence this reframing actually reduces confusion in practice. If the three definitions produce outcomes differing by 40%, finance and growth teams will still cite their own numbers in leadership discussions, undermining the canonical metric's authority. The claim assumes that relabeling competing definitions as 'supporting metrics' eliminates the organizational behavior driving the conflict—but naming discipline alone does not change incentive structures. The artifact offers no governance mechanism (ownership, arbitration process, enforcement) to prevent teams from reverting to their preferred numbers when results are unfavorable. The recommendation is structurally sound as a framing device but is unsupported as an operational solution.
