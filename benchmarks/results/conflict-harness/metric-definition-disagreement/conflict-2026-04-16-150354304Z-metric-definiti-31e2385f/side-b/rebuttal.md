# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The north star metric must be selected based on retention and revenue predictive power, not team preference — the definition with the strongest empirical correlation to 30/60/90-day retention and LTV should win, regardless of whether it shows growth, flat, or decline.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim asserts a selection rule without showing that retention and LTV prediction are the governing criteria for a north star in this case. The prompt is about leadership needing a single company number amid incompatible definitions, which also requires comparability, explainability, timeliness, and cross-functional operational usefulness. A metric can correlate best with retention or revenue yet still be a poor executive north star if it is lagging, hard to compute, unstable across periods, or weakly actionable for teams outside monetization. No evidence is provided that maximizing predictive correlation alone resolves the leadership decision.
