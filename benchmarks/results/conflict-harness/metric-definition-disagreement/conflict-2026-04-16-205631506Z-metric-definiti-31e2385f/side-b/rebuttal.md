# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The immediate decision should be to resolve metric governance first, because the case packet does not provide enough evidence to justify which exact definition should win on substantive grounds.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim inverts the actual decision urgency. Leadership asked for a single number precisely because the governance ambiguity is causing real harm—the same business reads as growing, flat, or declining. Recommending a governance process before picking a definition delays the resolution, not accelerates it. More critically, the artifact never attempts to reason about which definition is most likely to reflect durable value even under acknowledged uncertainty. A strong strategy artifact would offer a provisional recommendation with explicit assumptions (e.g., 'if engagement depth is the goal, product's definition is the strongest candidate') rather than deferring entirely to a reconciliation exercise. Deferring the substantive question to a future memo under the guise of evidence gaps is a decision-avoidance move, not a decision-useful one.
