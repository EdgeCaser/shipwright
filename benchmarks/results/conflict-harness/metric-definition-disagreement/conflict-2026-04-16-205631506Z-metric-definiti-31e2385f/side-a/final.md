# Revised artifact

## Decision Frame
Leadership should make an immediate north-star decision now: adopt one canonical active-user definition for company-level reporting and stop presenting the finance, product, and growth versions as interchangeable measures of the business. The recommended substantive standard is to choose the definition that best reflects enduring product value at the company level, not the one optimized for a single function's operating needs.

Because the three current definitions can make the business appear growing, flat, or declining, the company does not currently have a usable north star. The decision is therefore two-part but immediate: first, pick one definition now as the company number; second, explicitly demote the other two to diagnostic supporting metrics.

What should be decided now:
1. One active-user definition becomes the only company north star.
2. The selection criterion is company-level decision usefulness and alignment to durable customer value.
3. The non-selected finance, product, and growth definitions remain available only as labeled functional diagnostics.
4. Leadership reporting must stop collapsing incompatible definitions into a false single truth.

This does not justify indefinite process. It requires an immediate choice, but that choice should be made against an explicit standard rather than by function-specific preference.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The case packet does not include the actual finance, product, and growth definitions of "active user," so there is not enough evidence to name which specific function's definition should win. That is a real limitation, but it does not block the core recommendation above.

The main unresolved questions are:
- What user behavior each definition counts as "active"
- Whether each definition is primarily designed for finance, product engagement, or growth reporting rather than company-level value measurement
- Which rule differences create the reported spread across outcomes
- Which definition is most credible as the enduring company-level signal once functional incentives are stripped away

Given those gaps, the best current recommendation is not to defer the decision, but to make the decision rule explicit now: the north star should be the definition that best represents durable product value, while the others remain secondary lenses.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass:
- Leadership is willing to choose one company-level definition immediately
- The chosen metric is explicitly framed as the only north star for executive reporting
- The other two definitions are preserved only as functional diagnostics
- Future reporting distinguishes the canonical metric from function-specific views

Fail:
- Leadership asks for one number but allows multiple incompatible meanings to persist
- Teams continue using different active-user counts as interchangeable claims about company performance
- The company selects a definition based on local functional convenience rather than company-level usefulness
- The canonical metric is not clearly separated from diagnostic sub-metrics

Produce a metric selection memo that does not reopen the need for a single number, but resolves which definition wins. It should contain:
1. A side-by-side comparison of the three current definitions
2. A recommendation naming the canonical definition against the standard of durable company-level value
3. A clear designation of the two non-selected definitions as diagnostic metrics only
