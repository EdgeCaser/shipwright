# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The right compromise is internal operational separation without a public brand, billing, and experience split.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B asserts that internal separation delivers the strategic discipline leadership wants, but provides zero evidence that internal-only separation actually works for declining-versus-growing business pairs. The artifact acknowledges the core counterargument — that bundling creates organizational drag — and dismisses it as a timing issue, yet offers no mechanism to prevent the slower DVD business from continuing to constrain streaming product decisions when they share a single customer-facing brand, website, and queue. If a unified interface means every streaming UX change must be evaluated for DVD impact, the organizational drag persists regardless of separate P&Ls. Side B's own open questions reveal this gap: they admit they don't know whether investor confidence can recover with an internal-separation narrative, and they have no data on whether internal separation actually reduces the product-decision friction they concede is real. The recommendation rests on an untested assumption that separate P&Ls and leadership structures behind the scenes will produce the same strategic clarity as a clean external split, while the only evidence cited (ctx-3) is the counterargument itself, not support for the internal-separation alternative.
