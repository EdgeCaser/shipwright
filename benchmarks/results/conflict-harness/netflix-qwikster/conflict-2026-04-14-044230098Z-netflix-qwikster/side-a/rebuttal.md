# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The DVD business is the cash engine financing the streaming transition; separating it into a visibly declining standalone entity accelerates subscriber churn and reduces the capital available for content acquisition during the most critical competitive window.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible evidence supports that DVD currently generates high-margin cash and that streaming costs are rising, but it does not establish the claimed causal chain that a corporate split would itself accelerate DVD churn or materially reduce capital available for streaming content. Qwikster changes branding and UX, not the underlying economics of DVD cash generation. No cited evidence quantifies how much cash transfer flexibility would be lost under separation, whether intercompany funding could continue, or whether churn would worsen specifically because DVD became a standalone entity rather than because of the already-announced price increase and UX friction.
