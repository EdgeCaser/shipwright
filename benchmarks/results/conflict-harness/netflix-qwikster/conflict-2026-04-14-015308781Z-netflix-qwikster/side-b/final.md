# Revised artifact

## Reverse Course on Qwikster

### Recommendation
Reverse the Qwikster split immediately and keep both services under the Netflix brand. Do not proceed with two companies, two websites, two bills, and two queues. Instead, pair the reversal with a hard internal operating-model reset: separate business-unit leaders, separate P&Ls, explicit transfer-pricing rules for shared costs, and a board-level review cadence on streaming growth, DVD cash flow, and migration economics. The strategic logic behind reducing DVD drag on streaming is valid, but the announced customer-facing split destroys trust faster than it creates strategic clarity. [ctx-1] [ctx-2] [ctx-3] [ctx-4]

### Why reverse now
Netflix is in a trust and retention crisis, not primarily a legal-entity-design crisis. After the effective 60% price increase, the company is already facing projected Q3 subscriber losses of 800,000, an ACSI drop from 79 to 52, and intense public backlash. Qwikster adds a second insult by making the service harder to use precisely when customers are already angry that it became more expensive. [ctx-1] [ctx-2] [ctx-4]

DVD is still the transition engine. It is declining, but it remains large, high-margin, and cash generative at a moment when streaming content costs are surging from $180M in 2010 toward a projected $2B in 2012. A customer-facing split that accelerates DVD churn weakens the funding bridge that streaming still depends on. [ctx-3] [ctx-5] [ctx-6]

The proposed product experience is materially worse. Streaming offers about 20,000 titles versus roughly 100,000 on DVD, so many households still need both. Separate logins, queues, reviews, and billing add friction without adding customer value. [ctx-4] [ctx-5]

Competitive timing also favors simplification. Amazon Prime Video, Hulu Plus, and HBO GO are entering the category while Netflix is expanding internationally. This is the wrong moment to fragment the consumer story and management attention. [ctx-6] [ctx-7]

### Strongest counterargument
The strongest argument for proceeding is that streaming and DVD have different economics, product needs, and futures, and that only a formal split will force real accountability. That concern is legitimate. The recommendation to reverse is therefore not "keep the old bundled operating model." It is "keep one customer brand, but impose a much stricter internal separation than Netflix has had to date." If leadership is unwilling to create real internal operating independence, then reversal becomes much weaker. [ctx-3] [ctx-4] [ctx-6]

### Required internal operating model
To make reversal credible, leadership should implement four mechanisms within 30 days:

1. Separate general managers for Streaming and DVD with independent operating targets.
2. Separate segment P&Ls with explicit allocation rules for shared marketing, tech, and support costs.
3. A standing capital-allocation forum that forces streaming content investment decisions to be evaluated separately from DVD optimization.
4. A shared-customer council whose mandate is to preserve one account, one brand, and the lowest-friction cross-service experience.

This does not prove internal separation will work as well as a formal split. It does, however, make the recommendation decision-useful: reverse externally, but only alongside a defined governance design that addresses the original strategic problem rather than pretending it disappears. ## Decision Frame
Decision: Reverse the Qwikster launch and retain one Netflix brand and customer experience.

Condition: Only do this if leadership simultaneously installs a formal internal two-business operating model with separate leaders, P&Ls, and capital-allocation discipline.

Why now: Customer harm is immediate and visible; any structural benefits from Qwikster are slower and would be overwhelmed by churn, trust erosion, and brand damage if pursued in the announced form.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- We do not have direct evidence that Netflix's existing leadership bench can execute genuine internal separation without reverting to bundled tradeoffs.
- We do not have a quantified estimate of churn specifically caused by the Qwikster mechanics versus the July 2011 price increase.
- We do not have a full standalone financial model showing streaming cash needs under multiple DVD-decline cases.
- We do not know whether content partners or capital markets would assign material value to a cleaner formal separation beyond narrative clarity.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if:
- Leadership accepts that reversing Qwikster must be paired with a real internal governance reset, not a return to the status quo.
- Netflix can name accountable streaming and DVD leaders immediately.
- Finance can stand up segment reporting and shared-cost rules quickly enough to support disciplined decisions.
- Management can communicate one simple customer promise: one Netflix relationship, improving streaming, continued DVD access where needed.

Fail if:
- Leadership wants to reverse the split publicly but preserve the old blended decision model internally.
- Netflix cannot create credible segment accountability without a formal external split.
- DVD churn is already too advanced for preserving the integrated customer relationship to matter financially.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A 12-month operating-model and cash-bridge plan covering segment governance, capital-allocation rules, churn-recovery actions, and downside triggers that would force reconsideration of a formal separation later.
