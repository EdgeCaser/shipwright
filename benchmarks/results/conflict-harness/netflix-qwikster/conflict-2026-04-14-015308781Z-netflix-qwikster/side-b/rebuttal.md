# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
DVD-by-mail generates ~$1B in high-margin revenue that subsidizes streaming's operating losses and $2B content acquisition spend; spinning it off as Qwikster jeopardizes the financial engine funding Netflix's transition to streaming.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible evidence establishes that DVD cash flow subsidizes streaming, but it does not show that a legal/brand split would actually block that subsidy or materially reduce available cash in the decision window. The memo adds unsupported mechanisms like transfer-pricing complexity, split investor attention, and loss of financial flexibility without case evidence that these effects would be large enough to outweigh the strategic benefits of separation. As written, it jumps from 'DVD funds streaming today' to 'Qwikster jeopardizes funding' without proving the causal link.
