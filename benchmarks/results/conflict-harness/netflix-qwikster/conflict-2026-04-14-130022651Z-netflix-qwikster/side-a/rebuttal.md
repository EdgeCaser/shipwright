# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Netflix can preserve the strategic logic of separation by creating internal operating accountability without splitting the brand, billing, login, and queues.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B asserts that internal P&L separation captures 'most of the managerial benefit' of a full split, but provides zero evidence or precedent for this claim. The artifact names no company that successfully ran two fundamentally different supply chains (physical logistics vs. digital streaming) under one brand with only internal separation and avoided the organizational drag problem. More critically, Side B's own artifact acknowledges the counterargument — that bundling 'creates organizational drag and obscures the economics' — and never quantifies how much drag internal separation actually removes versus a full split. The phrase 'most of the strategic benefits' is doing enormous load-bearing work with no supporting analysis. Without evidence that internal-only separation has worked at comparable scale or complexity, this claim is an untested assertion dressed as a recommendation, and the entire memo's alternative path rests on it.
