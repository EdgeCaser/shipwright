# Reverse Course Now; Separate Internally First, Not Customer-Facing

Netflix should reverse the Qwikster split immediately and keep DVD-by-mail and streaming under one Netflix brand, one account, and one customer relationship. The strategic case for sharper separation is real: streaming is the future, DVD is declining, and the two businesses run on different economics and operating models. But the September 2011 decision is not between perfect structures. It is between a customer-facing split that is already destroying trust and an interim model that preserves flexibility while stabilizing the business. Given 800,000 subscriber losses in Q3 2011, a 77% stock collapse from the July peak, a drop in customer satisfaction from 79 to 52, and severe backlash to separate sites, queues, billing, and logins, Netflix should reverse the external split and solve the org-design problem internally first. [ctx-1][ctx-2][ctx-4][ctx-5][ctx-6]

The core strategic fact is sequencing. Netflix already imposed a 60% effective price increase on the combined plan. Adding Qwikster asks customers to pay more while accepting a worse experience and a weaker brand architecture. That is especially dangerous because streaming is not yet strong enough to stand alone on customer value: the streaming catalog is about 20,000 titles versus roughly 100,000 on DVD, while streaming content costs are rising from $180M in 2010 toward a projected $2B in 2012. In other words, Netflix is trying to force organizational purity before the streaming product has earned the right to absorb more friction. [ctx-2][ctx-3][ctx-4][ctx-7]

Reverse course is therefore the better decision, but the supporting logic should be narrower than claiming internal separation captures nearly all benefits of a full split. The evidence in hand does not prove that. What it does support is a more defensible conclusion: internal operating separation is the lower-regret interim move because it addresses the most urgent need for better accountability and economic clarity without compounding customer harm during an acute trust crisis. DVD is still a meaningful cash-flow engine, with about $1B in revenue and high margins, while streaming is loss-making and faces surging licensing costs and rising competition from Amazon, Hulu, and HBO. Preserving one front-end relationship protects the subsidy and the brand while management improves segment reporting, incentives, and leadership focus behind the scenes. [ctx-3][ctx-7][ctx-8]

The strongest counterargument is that only a full split forces the discipline required to optimize two incompatible businesses. That concern is valid. A unified brand can mask tradeoffs, and executives may continue to prioritize hybrid convenience over streaming speed. But that is an argument for staged separation, not immediate customer-facing fragmentation. The available evidence shows the external split is already imposing heavy, measurable damage; it does not show that a same-brand interim structure is unworkable over the next 12 months. Given the current crisis, Netflix should choose the option that restores trust, retains hybrid households, preserves DVD cash flow, and keeps open the ability to revisit a harder structural split later if internal measures fail. [ctx-1][ctx-2][ctx-3][ctx-5][ctx-6]

Management should announce four linked actions: cancel Qwikster; keep one Netflix identity, login, and billing relationship; introduce clearer internal business-line accountability and external segment reporting; and set a time-bounded review point to reassess whether stronger structural separation is needed after churn, satisfaction, and streaming economics stabilize. That path is strategically serious without being operationally reckless.

## Decision Frame

Decision: Reverse course on Qwikster now and retain a unified Netflix customer experience while separating accountability internally as an interim step.

Why this is the right call:
- The immediate priority is stopping trust erosion and churn, not maximizing structural purity. The damage from the announced split is already visible in subscriber losses, customer satisfaction decline, and brand backlash. [ctx-2][ctx-5][ctx-6]
- Streaming is not yet strong enough in catalog depth or economics to justify adding friction to the customer experience. Customers are being asked to accept more complexity while receiving less value. [ctx-2][ctx-4][ctx-7]
- DVD remains an important high-margin cash source funding streaming losses and expansion. Breaking the customer relationship now risks weakening the profit pool financing the transition. [ctx-3][ctx-7][ctx-8]
- Internal separation should be treated as a risk-reduction step, not as a proven full substitute for external separation. It improves visibility and accountability while preserving the option to revisit a harder split later. [ctx-1][ctx-3][ctx-8]

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

- How much of churn is attributable to the price increase versus the Qwikster announcement and resulting friction.
- Whether internal reporting and leadership separation will materially reduce decision drag in practice.
- How long DVD cash flows can continue subsidizing streaming under rising content costs.
- What threshold of churn reduction and customer-satisfaction recovery would justify keeping the businesses externally unified for longer.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

Pass if:
- Leadership is willing to reverse the customer-facing split immediately.
- Netflix can implement clear internal ownership, incentives, and segment reporting for DVD and streaming.
- Management commits to a staged review rather than pretending the org-design question is permanently settled.

Fail if:
- Leadership insists on immediate external separation despite clear customer harm.
- Churn and satisfaction do not improve after canceling Qwikster and simplifying the experience.
- Internal accountability proves too weak to improve streaming focus within a defined review window.

## Recommended Next Artifact

A 12-month transition memo with three components: customer recovery plan, segment-level operating model and reporting design, and decision triggers for whether to reconsider a harder structural separation after stabilization.
