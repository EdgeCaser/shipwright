# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Netflix can achieve internal strategic clarity without imposing separate brands, logins, queues, and billing on customers.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B asserts internal P&L separation is a viable substitute for the Qwikster split but provides no evidence that Netflix's organizational structure, technology stack, or management incentives could actually deliver this. In September 2011, Netflix's product, engineering, and content teams were deeply integrated around the combined service. The claim that internal separation is achievable without customer-facing change is asserted, not demonstrated — Side B names no precedent, no structural mechanism, and no timeline. The very fact that Hastings chose a public split suggests internal-only separation had already been considered and rejected as insufficient. Without evidence that clean internal separation was operationally feasible, this claim functions as an untested assumption carrying the full weight of the reversal recommendation.
