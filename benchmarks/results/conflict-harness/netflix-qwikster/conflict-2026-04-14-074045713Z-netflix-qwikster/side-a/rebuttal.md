# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Netflix can achieve strategic separation internally without imposing a damaging public brand and product split.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim is asserted without any evidence that internal-only separation actually works at companies facing a similar legacy-to-future business transition. The artifact provides zero examples, no organizational research, and no mechanism design for how internal P&L separation would produce genuinely independent decision-making while sharing a brand, website, queue, and billing system. The case packet shows the core problem is real: streaming product decisions are constrained by DVD logistics. Side B acknowledges this drag exists but hand-waves the solution as 'separate P&Ls, leadership owners, roadmaps, and operating metrics internally' — a list of org-chart labels, not an operational plan. History at companies like HP, eBay/PayPal, and IAC shows that when two businesses share customer-facing infrastructure, the declining business routinely vetoes or delays changes that would accelerate the growth business, regardless of internal reporting lines. Without evidence that internal separation can overcome shared-infrastructure coupling, this claim is an untested assumption dressed as a recommendation. The artifact's own Unknowns section implicitly concedes this gap by listing 'whether a lighter-weight separation could preserve clarity without brand damage' as an open question — meaning even Side B is uncertain its central mechanism works.
