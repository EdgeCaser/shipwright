# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The strategic need for organizational focus does not require a customer-facing brand split; Netflix can separate internal accountability while preserving one brand and one account experience.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim is asserted without any evidence that internal-only separation actually delivers the operating benefits that motivated the split. The artifact acknowledges the counterargument that bundling blurs accountability and traps streaming inside DVD economics, yet offers zero examples, precedents, or mechanisms showing that separate P&Ls under one brand resolve those problems. Internal separation without customer-facing separation creates dual-reporting complexity, shared engineering resources fighting over priorities, and a single product surface that must serve two diverging user bases. The artifact lists 'build internal separation' as an action item but never addresses the core organizational design challenge: how do you prevent a declining DVD business from consuming executive attention and product roadmap bandwidth when both share a brand, website, queue, and billing system? This is the crux of the pro-split argument, and Side A hand-waves past it with an unsupported assertion that internal separation is sufficient. The absence of any structural detail or analogous case makes this claim speculative rather than evidence-based.
