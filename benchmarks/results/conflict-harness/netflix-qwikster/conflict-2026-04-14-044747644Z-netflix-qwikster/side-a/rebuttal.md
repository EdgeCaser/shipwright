# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The DVD business generates ~$1B in high-margin revenue that directly subsidizes streaming's operating losses and content cost surge ($180M to $2B); a hard brand split accelerates DVD subscriber churn beyond the organic ~5%/quarter decline, destroying the cash engine before streaming achieves self-sufficiency.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim bundles two supported premises with an unsupported causal leap. The packet supports that DVD is high-margin, that streaming costs are rising, and that DVD is declining organically. But it does not provide evidence that the Qwikster split would specifically accelerate DVD churn beyond the existing decline rate, nor that any such acceleration would be large enough to 'destroy' the cash engine before streaming matures. The artifact itself admits the key attribution gap elsewhere: it does not know how much churn came from the July price increase versus the September Qwikster announcement. Without isolating churn by plan type and cause, the conclusion that the split materially collapses DVD cash flow is asserted rather than demonstrated.
