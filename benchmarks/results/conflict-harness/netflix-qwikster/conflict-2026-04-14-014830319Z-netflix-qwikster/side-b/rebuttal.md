# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
DVD-by-mail cash flow (~$1B revenue, high margins) is currently subsidizing the money-losing streaming business during a critical period of surging content costs ($180M to $2B) and international expansion — splitting eliminates this cross-subsidy at the worst possible time.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible evidence shows DVD profits currently subsidize streaming, but it does not show that a Qwikster-style separation would necessarily eliminate access to that cash in the relevant decision window. The claim jumps from 'separate companies/websites' to 'streaming can no longer be funded' without evidence on proposed capital structure, intercompany agreements, balance-sheet allocation, or whether Netflix could still direct DVD cash flows during a transition. As written, it overstates a financial consequence that is not established by the cited facts alone.
