- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The strongest pro-split argument, organizational clarity for streaming, is valid in principle but can be achieved internally without fragmenting the customer relationship or weakening the brand.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B asserts that internal separation delivers the same strategic benefits as a public split, but provides zero evidence that any company has successfully run two businesses with fundamentally different operational models (physical logistics vs. digital delivery), different cost structures, and diverging customer bases under one brand without the slower business dragging product decisions for the faster one. The claim is stated as self-evident when it is actually the central empirical question. Internal P&L separation does not solve the core problem: when a single product team must serve both DVD and streaming customers through one interface, feature prioritization, engineering resources, and UX design inevitably compromise toward the larger installed base. Side B acknowledges the pro-split logic is 'real' but then hand-waves the solution as 'distinct operating goals and reporting' — which is exactly the organizational status quo that Hastings identified as insufficient. Without a concrete mechanism explaining how internal separation avoids the product-decision contamination that motivated the split, this claim is an assertion dressed as a rebuttal.
