# Reverse Course on Qwikster

## Recommendation
Reverse the Qwikster split immediately and keep DVD-by-mail and streaming under the Netflix brand, with one account, one bill, and one queue experience where technically feasible. Preserve internal operating separation and P&L transparency, but do not force a customer-facing corporate separation now.

The strategic logic behind separation is real: streaming is the future, DVD is declining, and the two businesses have different economics. But the announced form of separation is value-destructive because it compounds a severe trust crisis, weakens the brand at the exact moment Netflix needs customer patience, and risks disrupting the cash engine that still funds streaming and international expansion.

## Why Reverse Course
### 1. The current plan destroys customer value faster than it creates strategic clarity
The company has already imposed a 60% effective price increase on combined users, lost 800,000 subscribers in Q3 2011, and seen customer satisfaction collapse from 79 to 52 in a single quarter. Adding separate websites, separate billing, separate queues, and separate logins asks angry customers to absorb even more friction for less perceived value. That is not a positioning reset; it is a forced downgrade in convenience. The market reaction indicates customers understand the move as Netflix making its own org chart their problem.

### 2. DVD cash flow is still strategically essential
DVD is declining organically, but it remains a large, high-margin business generating roughly $1B in revenue and subsidizing a streaming business that is still loss-making while content licensing costs surge from $180M in 2010 toward a projected $2B in 2012. Separating the businesses in a way that accelerates DVD churn or reduces attachment to Netflix weakens the very funding source needed to win streaming. Leadership should harvest DVD, not stigmatize or orphan it.

### 3. Brand fragmentation is especially dangerous in a trust crisis
Netflix is trying to navigate price backlash, a weaker streaming catalog than DVD, new streaming competition from Amazon, Hulu, and HBO, and simultaneous international expansion. This is the worst possible moment to fracture the master brand. Qwikster adds confusion without adding meaningful customer benefit, and the botched handle situation reinforces the impression of poor execution. Strategic clarity for investors does not offset chaos for customers.

### 4. The company can get most of the strategic benefit internally without the public split
Netflix does not need two consumer brands to run two operating models. It can separate leadership focus, capital allocation, content investment logic, and operational metrics internally while maintaining a unified consumer experience. That preserves optionality: management can continue shifting the center of gravity toward streaming without detonating the installed base.

## Strongest Counterarguments, and Why They Do Not Win
### Counterargument: Streaming needs freedom from DVD legacy constraints
True. Streaming should not be governed as a DVD adjacency forever. But the immediate question is not whether streaming deserves strategic primacy; it does. The question is whether a public split with duplicate customer friction is the right mechanism in September 2011. It is not. Internal separation captures much of the benefit with far less churn, confusion, and reputational damage.

### Counterargument: DVD decline is inevitable, so Netflix should force the transition now
The decline is real, but forced migration only works when the replacement product is clearly superior. Here it is not: streaming offers roughly 20,000 titles versus about 100,000 on DVD, while content costs are rising and competition is intensifying. Pushing customers off a still-valuable service before the substitute is ready risks losing them altogether.

### Counterargument: Wall Street needs a clean streaming story
Some investors may prefer cleaner optics, but the stock collapse already shows that execution credibility matters more than narrative purity. A reversal may be painful in the short term, yet it is less damaging than persisting with a plan that signals management is ignoring plainly visible customer harm.

## Immediate Actions
1. Publicly cancel the Qwikster separation and state clearly that customers will keep one Netflix relationship.
2. Freeze further customer-facing complexity increases for 2 quarters while trust is rebuilt.
3. Reframe the strategy as: one brand externally, two operating disciplines internally.
4. Protect DVD cash flow while accelerating streaming content depth and product quality.
5. Set explicit transition triggers for any future structural split: stable satisfaction recovery, reduced churn, stronger streaming catalog parity, and evidence that customers will accept unbundling without mass defection.

## Decision Frame
Reverse course on the customer-facing Qwikster split. Keep Netflix as the unified brand and account experience, while separating decision rights and economics internally. The right strategy is not to deny the future of streaming; it is to avoid sabotaging that future by destroying trust, convenience, and funding at the same time.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- Exact contribution margin split between DVD and streaming by cohort.
- Elasticity of churn to additional friction beyond the July 2011 price increase.
- Whether any customer segments would accept a split if the UX were substantially cleaner.
- How quickly streaming catalog quality can improve relative to DVD breadth.
- Investor reaction to a reversal versus persistence over the next 2 quarters.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass for decision: there is enough evidence to reject the announced split now.

Fail for permanent structural separation: Netflix has not shown that streaming can stand alone economically or that customers will tolerate separate service relationships without severe churn and brand damage.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A recovery plan covering 90-day customer trust repair, pricing stabilization, internal operating-model separation, and milestone-based criteria for revisiting any future structural split.
