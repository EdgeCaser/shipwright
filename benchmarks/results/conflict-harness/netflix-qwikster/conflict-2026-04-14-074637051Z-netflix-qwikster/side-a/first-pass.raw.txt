Keep DVD-by-mail and streaming under one Netflix brand, one account, one bill, and one customer relationship. The strategic premise behind separation is directionally valid: streaming is the future and DVD is in secular decline. But the announced form of the split destroys customer experience, accelerates churn at the wrong moment, weakens the cash engine funding streaming, and consumes management attention while competition and content costs are rising.\n\nThe core issue is not whether Netflix should transition from DVDs to streaming. The split compounds an active trust crisis\nNetflix has already triggered severe customer backlash with the effective 60% increase for customers who want both DVDs and streaming. The company then asked those same customers to accept separate websites, separate billing, separate queues, and separate reviews. That is not strategic clarity from the customer perspective; it is cumulative friction layered on top of a price shock.\n\nEvidence of damage is already visible: total subscribers fell from about 24.6M in Q2 2011 to 23.8M in Q3 2011, a loss of 800,000 subscribers, while customer satisfaction collapsed from 79 to 52 in a single quarter. The DVD business is still strategically valuable\nDVD is declining, but it remains a large, high-margin cash flow business with roughly $1B in revenue. Streaming is not ready to stand alone as the full customer promise\nStreaming has strategic momentum, but in 2011 it is still materially inferior to DVD on selection: roughly 20,000 streaming titles versus about 100,000 on DVD. Many customers still need both services to get the experience they associate with Netflix. Separating the brands before streaming reaches stronger catalog parity forces customers to feel the gap more sharply.\n\nA cleaner long-term org chart is not worth exposing a weaker near-term product reality.\n\n### 4. Management focus should be on content, competition, and international execution\nNetflix is entering the most resource-intensive phase of the streaming war. Amazon Prime Video, Hulu Plus, and HBO GO are in market. International expansion is underway in Canada, Latin America, and the UK. Content costs are surging. Those are the real strategic battles.\n\nQwikster creates a self-inflicted execution tax: rebranding, migrating accounts, explaining product confusion, handling billing anger, and repairing reputation damage. Leadership bandwidth is scarce. It should be spent on streaming economics and differentiation, not on administratively separating a still-interdependent customer offer.\n\n## Strongest Counterargument\nThe strongest argument for proceeding is that bundling DVD and streaming traps Netflix between two business models. DVD logistics, merchandising, and queue design can distort decision-making for a streaming-first future. Separation could create sharper accountability, clearer financial visibility, and faster product decisions for each business.\n\nThat logic is real, but it fails the timing and market test. A good internal strategy can still be a bad market move if customers experience it as punishment and confusion. Netflix can preserve internal operating separation without imposing a customer-facing corporate split. The company does not need two brands, two websites, and two bills to create management clarity. It needs clearer internal P&L accountability, distinct operating goals, and a transition plan that does not further damage trust.\n\n## What Netflix Should Do Instead\n1. Cancel Qwikster publicly and keep one Netflix brand.\n2. Preserve one login, one website, one bill, and one account identity.\n3. Reorganize internally into separate DVD and streaming operating units with distinct leaders and metrics.\n4. Stabilize churn by reframing pricing around simplicity and value, not corporate structure.\n5. Use DVD cash flow deliberately to fund streaming content and international ramp, while managing DVD for profitability rather than growth.\n6. Build a staged migration path where streaming earns primacy through better catalog depth and product quality, not forced separation.\n\n## Decision Frame\nReverse course on Qwikster. The separation creates immediate customer harm, threatens the cash flow that funds streaming, and distracts from the actual strategic contest. Internal separation is sufficient; external separation is unnecessary and value-destructive.\n\n## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps\n- The precise churn mix between DVD-only, streaming-only, and combo
