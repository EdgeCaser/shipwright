The platform must enforce mandatory human approval for pricing commitments and use LLMs only behind deterministic policy gates that own final routing, approval, and send/no-send decisions.\n\n### Target Users\n- Event coordinators who manage incoming client requests, event details, timelines, and follow-ups.\n- Sales or account managers who approve commercial commitments.\n- Operations managers who need visibility into event status, unresolved client needs, and handoffs.\n- Clients or prospects who initiate planning and follow-up through WhatsApp.\n\n### Phase 1 Goal\nReduce manual coordination load for inbound event planning while preventing the system from autonomously making commitments about price, availability, contract terms, service guarantees, refunds, cancellations, or client-facing policy positions.\n\n### Non-Goals\n- No autonomous negotiation.\n- No autonomous discounting, final quote issuance, or price guarantee.\n- No LLM-generated pricing logic.\n- No fully automated contract execution.\n- No replacement of the event coordinator for ambiguous or high-stakes client decisions.\n- No generalized workflow builder in Phase 1.\n\n### Core User Problems\n- WhatsApp requests are fragmented, hard to triage, and easy to lose across staff inboxes.\n- Event requirements are often gathered in unstructured messages and must be retyped into planning tools.\n- Clients expect fast responses, but staff cannot safely automate answers involving pricing, availability, or contractual commitments.\n- Managers need a console that separates routine automation from human-required decisions.\n\n### Phase 1 Product Scope\n\n#### 1. WhatsApp Assistant\nThe WhatsApp assistant supports inbound and outbound client messaging for event intake, status updates, and structured follow-up.\n\nPhase 1 capabilities:\n- Receive WhatsApp messages from clients and prospects.\n- Create or update a conversation record linked to a contact and event request.\n- Collect approved intake details such as event date, location, attendee count, event type, food and beverage needs, equipment needs, contact details, and urgency.\n- Confirm receipt using approved non-commercial templates.\n- Send human-approved follow-up messages from the console.\n- Escalate messages that include price, discount, contract, refund, cancellation, guarantee, legal, safety, custom package, or availability commitment language.\n\nThe assistant must not:\n- Commit to pricing, discounts, availability, refunds, substitutions, deadlines, or contract terms.\n- Invent package details or policy interpretations.\n- Continue a conversation when classification is unknown, confidence falls below the configured threshold, validation fails, or any restricted-topic signal is present.\n\n#### 2. Internal Console\nThe internal console is the operational control surface for event staff.\n\nPhase 1 capabilities:\n- Inbox view of WhatsApp conversations with event-linked context.\n- Event request profile with structured fields extracted from messages.\n- Human review queue for pricing, policy, availability, and contract-related messages.\n- Approval workflow for outbound pricing-related replies.\n- Conversation timeline with source messages, extracted fields, assistant actions, human edits, approvals, and sent messages.\n- Filters by event status, assigned owner, escalation reason, pending approval, and last client response.\n- Manual override for classification, extracted fields, and routing.\n- Audit log for all system and human actions.\n\n#### 3. Human Approval for Pricing Commitments\nPricing commitment approval is mandatory in Phase 1.\n\nA pricing commitment includes:\n- Final quote or invoice amount.\n- Discount, promotion, waiver, or concession.\n- Statement that a price is guaranteed or locked.\n- Custom package price.\n- Payment schedule, deposit amount, cancellation fee, refund policy, or minimum spend.\n- Any message that could reasonably be interpreted as a commercial commitment.\n\nRequired behavior:\n- The system may draft a pricing response only as an internal draft.\n- The system must label the draft as unapproved and unsent.\n- A human with pricing approval permission must approve before the message can be sent.\n- The final sent message must preserve approver identity, timestamp, original draft, edited final text, and source conversation context.\n- If no approved price exists in the deterministic pricing system, the assistant must escalate instead of estimating.\n- Pricing approval cannot be bypassed by editing a draft, choosing a template, using a manual send path, or invoking an assistant-generated rewrite.\n\n#### 4. Deterministic LLM Boundary\nLLMs may be used only for bounded assistive tasks where deterministic controls decide whether the output may be used.\n\nAllowed LLM uses:\n- Summarizing conversation history for internal staff.\n- Extracting candidate event details into structured fields, subject to validation and source-message traceability.\n- Drafting internal response suggestions that require human review when they touch restricted topics.\n- Rewriting approved human-authored text for tone only after deterministic semantic-preservation checks and, where required, human approval.\n- Classifying message intent as a candidate signal, not as the final authority for restricted workflows.\n\nForbidden LLM uses:\n- Pricing calculation.\n- Discount approval.\n- Availability confirmation.\n- Contract term generation.\n- Refund, cancellation, or policy adjudication.\n- Payment commitment.\n- Autonomous escalation resolution.\n- Any irreversible external action without deterministic checks.\n\nDeterministic controls:\n- A rules engine owns final routing, permission checks, escalation state, approval requirements, and send/no-send decisions.\n- Pricing values come only from approved structured sources, not generated text.\n- Restricted-topic detection uses deterministic rules plus model-assisted signals, but the deterministic policy gate performs the final state transition.\n- Signal arbitration is deterministic: if any source flags a restricted topic, missing required data, low confidence, semantic drift, policy uncertainty, or unknown intent, the message is routed to human review.\n- If deterministic rules classify a message as safe but the LLM classifier flags it as restricted, the policy gate escalates to human review and records the LLM signal as the escalation reason.\n- If the LLM classifier classifies a message as safe but deterministic rules flag it as restricted, the policy gate escalates to human review and records the rule match as the escalation reason.\n- If both layers classify a message as safe, the pre-send policy gate still validates the outbound text against restricted-topic, pricing-source, role, template, and approval rules before delivery.\n- The LLM cannot overrule deterministic escalation, downgrade a restricted state, approve a send, or resolve a policy conflict.\n- Any failed validation, low confidence, missing required field, restricted-topic match, or classifier/rule conflict defaults to human review.\n\nTone rewrite boundary:\n- Tone rewrites are allowed only for already-approved human-authored text.\n- The system must preserve protected facts exactly, including price, dates, venue, package names, capacity, deadlines, payment terms, cancellation terms, refund terms, and approval language.\n- The rewrite service must return a structured diff against the approved source text.\n- The policy gate blocks the rewrite if protected facts are added, removed, or changed; if restricted-topic terms appear without an existing approved source; if numeric values change; or if the rewrite introduces new commitments.\n- If semantic preservation cannot be deterministically verified, the rewrite becomes an internal draft requiring human approval before send.\n\n### Functional Requirements\n\n| ID | Requirement | Priority | Acceptance Criteria |\n|---|---|---:|---|\n|
