# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The PRD must reject a Q2 production launch and redefine success around the fastest compliant path because the minimum regulatory review time exceeds the time remaining in Q2 and skipping review creates material legal exposure.

## Evidence Or Reason
The claim treats the 14-week minimum as a fixed, unexamined constraint, but the PRD never surfaces whether that figure is the process floor or a historical average with variance. No evidence is offered that a parallel review track, external counsel acceleration, pre-submission engagement with the regulator, or expedited review under exceptional circumstances is unavailable. A decision-quality PRD must distinguish 'current process takes 14 weeks' from 'fastest legally permissible review takes 14 weeks.' Without that distinction, the PRD risks treating a process inefficiency as a legal ceiling, which could cause unnecessary delay and customer harm without corresponding legal benefit. The Unknowns section acknowledges the submission start date is uncertain but does not flag review duration itself as an open question—leaving the foundational constraint unverified.
