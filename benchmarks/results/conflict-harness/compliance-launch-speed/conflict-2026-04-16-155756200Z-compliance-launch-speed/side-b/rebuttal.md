## Claim Under Attack
The 4-week structural gap between the compliance minimum (14 weeks) and the committed date (10 weeks) is not a tradeoff to optimize — it is a binary authorization problem that must be resolved by executives before any engineering work is scoped.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible record supports a schedule conflict and legal risk, but it does not support the stronger conclusion that no engineering scoping should occur before executive authorization. The scenario says the review takes 14 weeks under the current process and that skipping it creates material exposure; it does not show that parallel engineering discovery, feasibility analysis, or compliant scope-shaping work is impossible or wasteful. Claiming this is purely a binary executive authorization problem adds an unsupported process constraint beyond the evidence provided.
