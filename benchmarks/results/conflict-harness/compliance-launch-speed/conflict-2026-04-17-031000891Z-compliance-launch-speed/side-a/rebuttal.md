## Claim Under Attack
The regulated disbursement capability should not launch in Q2 because the required compliance review exceeds the remaining quarter and skipping it creates material legal exposure.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B treats the 14-week review timeline as immovable without investigating whether an expedited or parallel review process exists. The PRD lists 'narrower feature slice that avoids the same regulatory review' as an open question but never closes it — a decision-quality PRD should either confirm no such slice exists (with legal sign-off) or structure the recommendation conditionally on that finding. As written, the no-Q2-launch conclusion is stated with medium confidence but the evidence base is the case packet alone, with no documented inquiry into process acceleration, phased regulatory submissions, or jurisdictional scoping. The claim forecloses options it has not actually ruled out.
