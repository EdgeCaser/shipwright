# Exit Zillow Offers and refocus Zillow on the marketplace

## Recommendation
Zillow should exit iBuying entirely rather than attempt another reform cycle. In October 2021, the evidence indicates Zillow Offers is not suffering from a temporary execution issue; it is failing on the core requirement of the business model: pricing homes accurately enough to earn thin flipping margins at scale. Zillow's algorithmic edge has not materialized in practice, losses are now large enough to obscure the economics of the core marketplace, and the current housing backdrop is turning less forgiving just as Zillow is carrying a large backlog of mispriced inventory.

The board should authorize an orderly wind-down of Zillow Offers, monetize inventory as quickly as value-destructively prudent, stop new home purchases, and reposition Zillow around its higher-margin IMT and adjacent transaction-enablement businesses.

## Why Exit Is Correct

### 1. The core unit economics appear structurally broken, not temporarily impaired
Zillow Offers bought 9,680 homes in Q3 2021 but sold only 3,032, creating a severe inventory imbalance at the exact moment management knows the pricing model has been overpaying. Zillow is now holding about 7,000 homes purchased above current market value, with estimated write-down exposure of $300M to $500M. In home flipping, gross margins are typically only 2% to 5%, which means even modest pricing error destroys profitability. A Zestimate median error rate around 6.9% nationally is simply too wide for this business model, especially at national scale. Two pricing-model pivots in 2021 failed to solve the problem, which suggests the issue is not a tweakable parameter but a mismatch between model capability and operational ambition.

### 2. Zillow is scaling the wrong business while masking the right one
Zillow Offers generated $1.2B of Q3 2021 revenue, about 70% of company revenue, but that scale is misleading because it carries negative EBITDA and worsening losses. The business is revenue-heavy but margin-destructive. By contrast, Zillow's core IMT segment produced about $490M of revenue with roughly 30% EBITDA margins and 20%+ growth. The board's concern is well founded: Offers is making Zillow look like a low-quality, volatile balance-sheet operator instead of a high-margin marketplace and software-enabled housing platform. Exiting Offers would improve strategic clarity, capital efficiency, and investor understanding of the underlying business.

### 3. Macro conditions reduce the odds that a reform plan succeeds
The recent housing market was unusually supportive, with Case-Shiller showing home prices up 19.5% year over year as of August 2021. If Zillow could not make iBuying work in one of the strongest housing environments in recent memory, the odds are worse in a cooling market. When home price appreciation slows or reverses, a backlog of overpriced homes becomes more painful, not less. A reform plan would require Zillow to prove better pricing precision, tighter operations, and more disciplined geography selection in a less favorable market while already absorbing large inventory losses. That is the wrong moment to double down.

### 4. The claimed competitive advantage has not translated into durable operational advantage
The founding thesis was that Zillow's consumer traffic and Zestimate data would produce superior pricing. The evidence now points the other way. Opendoor, despite narrower geography and lower volume, has operated closer to breakeven. That suggests discipline, narrower scope, and operational fit matter more than broad top-of-funnel traffic or a consumer-facing estimate product. Zillow may still have valuable data assets, but not a sufficient edge to run balance-sheet-intensive market making at national scale.

## Strongest Counterarguments and Why They Do Not Change the Decision

### Counterargument 1: Reform with tighter buy-boxes, narrower geographies, and lower volume
This is the strongest argument for staying in the business. In principle, Zillow could retrench to a smaller footprint, impose stricter pricing guardrails, and use humans-in-the-loop to improve acquisition discipline.

That approach is still inferior to exit for three reasons. First, Zillow already pivoted the algorithm twice in 2021 without restoring confidence. Second, shrinking volume does not solve the credibility problem created by the current loss exposure and inventory backlog. Third, a reduced-scale iBuying business still consumes management attention, capital, and organizational oxygen that the core business can deploy at much higher returns.

### Counterargument 2: Zillow needs transaction ownership, not just leads, to win long term
There is logic here: housing profits are larger deeper in the transaction, and Zillow has long wanted to evolve beyond media and leads.

But exiting iBuying does not require abandoning transaction adjacency. Zillow can still pursue asset-light transaction services such as financing, closing, touring, seller services, and partner-enabled cash-offer experiences without taking direct housing inventory risk. The strategic mistake is not trying to move closer to the transaction; it is using principal risk and low-margin home flipping as the vehicle.

### Counterargument 3: Exiting now crystallizes losses and damages credibility
The losses are real whether Zillow exits or not. Continuing to buy homes after discovering model failure risks converting a contained strategic retreat into a compounding capital loss. Credibility with investors is more likely to recover from a decisive, evidence-based exit than from a prolonged attempt to defend a broken thesis.

## Board Actions for the Next 90 Days
1. Formally cease new home purchases and communicate that the pause is strategic, not temporary.
2. Liquidate existing inventory with a speed-versus-loss framework managed to portfolio economics, not property-by-property optimism.
3. Separate and report the wind-down economics clearly so investors can see the earning power of the core business.
4. Reallocate capital and leadership attention to IMT growth and asset-light transaction products.
5. Reduce Offers-related headcount and operating footprint quickly, while retaining selective talent relevant to marketplace and transaction services.
6. Publish a clear post-Offers strategy narrative: Zillow remains committed to digitizing residential real estate, but through software, demand aggregation, and services rather than principal home ownership.

## Decision Frame
Decision: Exit Zillow Offers entirely and refocus Zillow on its high-margin marketplace and asset-light transaction-enablement businesses.

Why this decision wins: The evidence supports that Zillow lacks the pricing accuracy required for profitable large-scale iBuying, current losses are large enough to impair valuation and strategic focus, and the core business already offers a superior growth-plus-margin profile.

What would have to be true for reform to be better: Zillow would need credible proof that it can sustainably achieve sub-2% effective pricing error in a narrower operating model, with positive unit economics after holding, renovation, and disposition costs. The case packet does not provide such evidence.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- We do not have market-by-market performance data; it is possible some local cohorts are viable even if the national model is not.
- We do not have a full contribution-margin bridge separating pricing error from operational cost overruns.
- We do not know the exact liquidation discount required to clear the current 7,000-home backlog.
- We do not have investor-reaction testing on an exit narrative versus a reform narrative.
- We do not have a detailed view of which Offers capabilities can be repurposed into asset-light transaction products.

These unknowns matter for execution, but not enough to reverse the recommendation. They affect how to exit, not whether to exit.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if the board agrees that:
- Zillow Offers' current failure is structural enough that more capital and time are unlikely to produce acceptable returns.
- The value of strategic focus and restored earnings clarity outweighs the option value of one more reform attempt.
- Zillow can preserve its broader housing-platform ambition through asset-light models rather than owned inventory.

Fail if the board believes, based on evidence not in this packet, that:
- A narrow-geo, low-volume iBuying model can reach durable positive unit economics quickly.
- Zillow's pricing capability is materially better in the only markets that matter for a relaunched model.
- Retaining iBuying is essential to defending the core marketplace in the next 12 to 24 months.

Given the evidence provided, the recommendation is ready for decision and favors exit.
