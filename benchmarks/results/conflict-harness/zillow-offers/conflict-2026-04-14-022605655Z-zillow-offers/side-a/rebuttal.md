# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Zillow should exit iBuying because its pricing accuracy is structurally inadequate for a thin-margin home-flipping model.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B treats the 6.9% national Zestimate error rate as proof of structural inadequacy, but this conflates a consumer-facing estimate with the actual Zillow Offers buy-side model, which uses a different, more granular pricing pipeline with local comps, interior condition data, and human overrides. The case states Zillow 'pivoted the algorithm twice in 2021' — evidence of active model iteration, not frozen incompetence. Side B never establishes what the Offers-specific pricing error rate actually is, nor whether targeted geographic tightening (fewer markets, better data density) could bring error below the 2% threshold in select metros. The claim that the gap is 'fatal' rests on a national median statistic applied to a business that operates market-by-market, which is an ecological fallacy. Without cohort-level or market-level accuracy data — which Side B itself lists as an unknown — the 'structural inadequacy' conclusion is asserted rather than demonstrated.
