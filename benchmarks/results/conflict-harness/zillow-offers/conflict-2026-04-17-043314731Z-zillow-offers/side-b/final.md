# Recommendation: Exit Zillow Offers and Refocus on the Marketplace Business

Zillow should exit iBuying rather than attempt another reform of Zillow Offers. The case no longer supports treating this as a recoverable execution problem. The business has already gone through two algorithm pivots in 2021 without solving overpayment, yet Zillow still purchased 9,680 homes in Q3 2021, sold only 3,032, and now holds about 7,000 homes that were acquired above current market value. With estimated write-down exposure of $300M to $500M, widening EBITDA losses, and a pricing engine whose median error rate is too wide for a thin-margin flipping model, the board should conclude that Zillow lacks the operating basis for a credible turnaround.

The strongest argument for reform is that Zillow Offers generated $1.2B in Q3 2021 revenue and represented 70% of total company revenue, which suggests meaningful customer demand and strategic reach. But revenue scale is not the relevant test here. The question is whether Zillow has a durable pricing and operating edge in a business where small valuation errors erase economics. The packet points the other way: losses are worsening quarter over quarter, the inventory backlog is large, the market is showing signs of cooling, and prior remediation attempts did not fix the core failure mode.

The better strategic path is to absorb the loss, unwind the inventory, and refocus management attention and capital on the core marketplace business. That segment produced $490M of revenue, about 30% EBITDA margins, and growth above 20% year over year. The board's concern is that Offers is masking the strength of that core business. Exiting iBuying addresses that directly. It removes a structurally misfit business from the portfolio and restores strategic coherence around the part of Zillow that is both profitable and growing.

## Decision Frame

The decision is whether Zillow should keep investing in a narrower version of Zillow Offers or exit the business entirely. The better choice is exit. The evidence supports the view that the pricing problem is structural enough to overwhelm reform efforts, while the core marketplace business is already strong enough to justify concentration rather than continued experimentation.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

- The packet does not provide market-level performance by geography or home segment, so it is possible some submarkets performed better than others.
- The case does not show whether any subset of transactions improved after the two algorithm pivots.
- The packet does not specify the exact operational cost and timing of a full unwind.
- The long-term strategic cost of abandoning direct transaction participation is not quantified.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

Pass if the board is prepared to treat the current inventory as a containment and liquidation problem, accept the write-down, and re-center the company on the marketplace business.

Fail if management believes another limited reform can succeed without new evidence that pricing accuracy is materially better in a constrained operating model. On the current record, that evidence is absent.

## Recommended Next Artifact

A board-approved exit and unwind memo covering inventory disposition, organizational restructuring for Offers-related headcount, investor communication on the rationale for exit, and a capital-allocation plan focused on expanding the high-margin marketplace and adjacent transaction services without principal home exposure.
