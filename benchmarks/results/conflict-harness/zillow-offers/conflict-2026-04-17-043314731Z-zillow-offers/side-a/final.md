# Strategic Recommendation: Exit Zillow Offers
**To:** Zillow Board of Directors 
**From:** Strategy Advisory 
**Date:** October 2021 
**Re:** Zillow Offers — Exit iBuying and Recommit to Core Marketplace

---

## Decision Frame

**Recommendation: Exit iBuying entirely.**

The case for exit rests on a compounding failure pattern that the available record does not give the board sufficient grounds to expect a reform path to reverse. Zillow's pricing algorithm carries a median error rate of ~6.9% nationally, while profitable home flipping requires sub-2% pricing accuracy. That gap is wide. Whether it is structurally permanent or theoretically closable in some narrow-market configuration is not proven by the evidence — but what is proven is that two algorithm pivots in 2021 failed to close it at the scale and geography Zillow actually operates. The board is not evaluating a fresh hypothesis. It is evaluating a demonstrated pattern of failure across multiple correction attempts.

The critique is correct that the 6.9% figure is a national median, and the case packet does not provide market-level unit economics showing that the accuracy gap persists uniformly in high-liquidity metros where Offers actually operated. That is a genuine evidence gap acknowledged in this analysis. The claim is therefore restated with appropriate precision: the available evidence does not support a credible reform path at national scale, and no evidence in the record shows the gap closes materially in any specific market. The burden of proof to justify continued capital deployment falls on the reform thesis, not the exit thesis — and that proof is absent.

The board should not confuse revenue scale with business viability. Zillow Offers generated $1.2B in Q3 2021 revenue — 70% of total company revenue — while producing negative EBITDA with losses widening quarter-over-quarter. That revenue reflects homes purchased and sold, not margin created. Revenue growth in a money-losing transaction business accelerates exposure to the underlying problem; it does not offset it.

The counterargument most worth engaging: Zillow's proprietary demand-side data (200M+ monthly visitors) gives it a pricing edge competitors lack, and a reformed Offers program at reduced volume in fewer markets might operate closer to the required accuracy band. This is the strongest version of the reform case. It fails for two reasons. First, Opendoor approaches breakeven at narrower geography and lower volume without comparable traffic data — the demand-side data advantage has not manifested as a pricing accuracy advantage in the observable record. Second, the reform path requires a capital commitment against an unquantified probability of success, while the core business being sacrificed to fund that bet is documented, high-quality, and capital-light.

The core IMT marketplace segment generates $490M in quarterly revenue at approximately 30% EBITDA margins, growing over 20% year-over-year. Offers losses are masking that strength — the stock decline from ~$200 to ~$85 reflects investor uncertainty about a business simultaneously operating a strong marketplace and a money-losing transaction unit. Exit clarifies the investment thesis. Zillow can pursue transaction economics through referral fees, mortgage origination, and closing services — asset-light exposure to transaction volume without the balance-sheet risk of holding and pricing residential real estate at scale.

**Decision: Exit Zillow Offers. Wind down home purchases, liquidate existing inventory at best available price, and redeploy headcount and capital to the IMT segment and adjacent transaction services.**

---

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

1. **Market-level pricing accuracy.** The 6.9% error rate is a national median. The record does not provide metro-level unit economics showing algorithm performance in high-liquidity markets where Offers operated most actively. This gap is material to evaluating a narrow-market continuation thesis. The board should commission this analysis before ruling out a geographically constrained middle path — but should not delay an exit decision pending that analysis if the reform path lacks a credible champion and a funded plan.

2. **Inventory liquidation timeline and realized loss.** The $300–500M write-down range is wide. A market-by-market liquidation analysis is required before committing to a public wind-down announcement. The timing and realized loss magnitude affect both P&L impact and communication strategy.

3. **Headcount transition costs and timing.** Zillow grew from 5,400 to 8,000+ employees, mostly for Offers. Severance, retention for wind-down operations, and redeployment capacity are not quantified in the available record and are material to exit economics.

4. **Competitive response.** Exiting cedes transaction inventory to Opendoor and Offerpad. The strategic question is whether that inventory creates durable demand-side advantage for competitors, or whether Zillow's marketplace traffic is sticky enough to maintain demand-side leverage without holding inventory.

5. **Mortgage and closing services capacity.** The transaction-adjacent thesis depends on Zillow Home Loans and closing services absorbing volume that Offers currently captures. Current scale and unit economics of those businesses are not in the available record.

---

## Pass/Fail Readiness

**This recommendation is ready to present to the board with the following qualifications:**

- **Pass:** The core exit recommendation is well-supported by available evidence. The pricing accuracy gap is wide, reform attempts have failed at national scale, and the core business quality is documented. A board can act on this recommendation.

- **Conditional:** The liquidation plan and headcount transition costs must be developed before public announcement. Announcing exit without a credible wind-down timeline risks amplifying market uncertainty and accelerating stock decline beyond what a clean exit narrative would produce.

- **Fail condition:** If market-specific analysis reveals the algorithm performs within required accuracy thresholds in a meaningful subset of high-liquidity metros — and if Zillow can operate a self-funding, geographically contained business in those markets without national Offers overhead — the recommendation should be revisited as a narrow-market continuation. That analysis does not exist in the current record. Its absence is itself informative: two years of operating Offers have not produced evidence the board can point to. The reform thesis has had the opportunity to generate that evidence and has not.

---

## Recommended Next Artifact

**Wind-Down and Capital Redeployment Plan**

The board needs a structured document covering: (1) market-by-market inventory liquidation schedule and projected realized losses within the $300–500M range, (2) headcount transition plan with severance estimates and redeployment targets, (3) capital reallocation model showing how freed resources accelerate IMT segment growth and adjacent transaction services, and (4) investor communication strategy framing exit as a return to a high-quality capital-light business, not a retreat from transaction ambition.

This document should be produced by the CFO and Corporate Development teams within 30 days, prior to any public announcement. Optionally, a parallel market-accuracy analysis should be commissioned to formally close the narrow-market continuation question before the board votes.
