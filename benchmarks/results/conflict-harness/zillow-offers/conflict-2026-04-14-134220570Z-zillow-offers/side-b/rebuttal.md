# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Zillow's Zestimate error rate (~6.9%) is structurally incompatible with the sub-2% accuracy required for profitable home flipping, and two failed algorithm pivots in 2021 demonstrate this is a fundamental capability gap, not a calibration problem.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim overreaches from the visible evidence. The packet gives a national Zestimate median error rate and says Zillow pivoted its pricing algorithm twice, but it does not establish that Zillow Offers was literally pricing homes off the consumer Zestimate, that national median error is the right metric for the narrower geographies Zillow bought in, or that sub-2% is a hard operational threshold for Zillow's full fee-and-services economics. Two failed pivots in one year show current underperformance, not that improvement is structurally impossible. The artifact turns evidence of a serious execution failure into proof of an unfixable capability gap without showing the missing causal link.
