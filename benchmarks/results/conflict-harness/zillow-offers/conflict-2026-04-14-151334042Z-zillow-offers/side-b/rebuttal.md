# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Zillow's pricing capability is too imprecise for a 2%-5% gross-margin business, making reform unlikely to produce durable profitability.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side A conflates the consumer-facing Zestimate's 6.9% national median error rate with Zillow Offers' actual acquisition pricing model. These are different systems: the Zestimate is a broad automated valuation for browsing, while Offers used a separate underwriting algorithm with additional data inputs, local market adjustments, and human review layers. The case packet states Zillow 'pivoted the algorithm twice in 2021' — proving the Offers pricing model was distinct and actively iterated. Side A presents no evidence of what the Offers-specific pricing error rate actually was, yet treats 6.9% as the operative number to declare structural impossibility. Furthermore, Opendoor operates profitably in the same 2-5% margin band, demonstrating that iBuying is not inherently incompatible with algorithmic pricing — the question is execution quality, not category viability. Side A's strongest analytical move (comparing 6.9% error to 2-5% margin) collapses if the actual Offers pricing error was materially different from the Zestimate figure, and no evidence is offered to rule this out.
