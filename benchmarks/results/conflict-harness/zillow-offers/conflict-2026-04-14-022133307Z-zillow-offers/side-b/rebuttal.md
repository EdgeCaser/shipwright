# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The 3.2:1 buy-to-sell ratio in Q3 2021 (9,680 purchased vs 3,032 sold) demonstrates systemic inventory accumulation driven by pricing model failure, not temporary operational constraints.

## Evidence Or Reason
The ratio shows throughput imbalance, but the artifact does not prove causality. A single-quarter buy/sell mismatch could also reflect lag between acquisition and resale, deliberate inventory build, renovation bottlenecks, or labor/supply constraints already acknowledged in the case. No geography mix, home-age cohort, days-to-resale, renovation completion, or contribution analysis is provided to isolate pricing-model failure as the dominant driver rather than an operational bottleneck interacting with rapid volume growth.
