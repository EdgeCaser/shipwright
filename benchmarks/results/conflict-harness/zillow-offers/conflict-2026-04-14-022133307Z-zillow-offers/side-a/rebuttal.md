- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
A reform strategy is less defensible than exit because Zillow has already tried multiple algorithm changes and still expanded losses, undermining confidence in its claimed right to win.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B treats two algorithm pivots in 2021 as sufficient evidence that reform is exhausted, but two iterations is an extremely thin basis for declaring a capability permanently unachievable. The artifact provides no benchmark for how many iterations a pricing model typically requires to reach sub-2% accuracy, nor does it compare Zillow's iteration pace against Opendoor's own learning curve (Opendoor operated for years before approaching breakeven). Side B also conflates volume-scaling losses with model-quality losses: Zillow purchased 9,680 homes in a single quarter while still tuning the algorithm, meaning losses could reflect reckless scaling speed rather than fundamental model inadequacy. A reform path that slows volume to match model maturity — buying hundreds rather than thousands per quarter — was never tested and therefore never falsified. Declaring the right to win 'already falsified' based on two pivots during a period of aggressive scaling is an unsupported inferential leap.
