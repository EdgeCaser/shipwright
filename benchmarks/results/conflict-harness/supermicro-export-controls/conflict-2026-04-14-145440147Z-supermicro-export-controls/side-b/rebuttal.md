# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-4
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
A selective pause is superior to a blanket slowdown because it preserves compliant AI-growth capacity while reducing the probability of compounding downside.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side A asserts that the company can cleanly distinguish 'high-risk' from 'low-risk' revenue streams and pause only the former, but then lists this exact capability as an open unknown in the Unknowns & Evidence Gaps section ('Whether the company can cleanly separate low-risk from high-risk revenue without major operational disruption') and as a fail condition ('Board cannot identify which revenue streams are high-risk'). The selective-pause recommendation therefore depends on an operational capability that Side A itself concedes may not exist. If segmentation proves infeasible or slow, the 90-day controlled-execution posture collapses into either a de facto blanket slowdown (destroying the revenue-preservation benefit Side A claims) or a de facto continuation of full-speed execution (destroying the compliance-reset benefit). No evidence is cited showing Supermicro has the internal data, systems, or organizational capacity to perform transaction-risk segmentation within the proposed 14-day timeline. The recommendation's core mechanism is unvalidated by Side A's own admission.
