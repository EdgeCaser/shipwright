# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The alleged $2.5B violation scale and active criminal indictments create asymmetric downside risk that makes a bounded pause in high-risk channels the more defensible board posture — the cost of underreacting exceeds the cost of a deliberate, scoped slowdown.

## Evidence Or Reason
The claim asserts an asymmetric risk calculus but does not provide visible evidence that a board-imposed pause would materially reduce the specific downside it names. The packet shows the company is not named as a defendant, the implicated individuals are gone, and an independent investigation plus compliance review are already underway. From the visible record alone, it is not established that additional compliant commercial activity would worsen legal exposure, preserve or damage Nvidia supply, or change regulator behavior more than ongoing remediation already does. The argument therefore jumps from seriousness of allegations to necessity of a pause without showing the causal link.
