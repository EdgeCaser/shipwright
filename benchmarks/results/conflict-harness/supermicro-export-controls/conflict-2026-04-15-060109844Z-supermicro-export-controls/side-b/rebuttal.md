# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-4
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Board members face Caremark fiduciary exposure if they vote to accelerate growth and the investigation subsequently reveals systemic oversight failures; a documented pause and reset is the board's best personal protection.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim overreaches the visible record. The packet shows an independent investigation is underway, a compliance-program review has started, and the acting CCO reports to the General Counsel, but none of that establishes the elements needed for a Caremark-style oversight failure or shows that a pause/reset is the board's 'best personal protection.' It also provides no evidence that choosing continued growth while remediation proceeds would itself create materially greater director liability than active investigation oversight, documented controls, and targeted remediation. The artifact turns a legal-risk possibility into a near-determinative governance conclusion without visible support.
