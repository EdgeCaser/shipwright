# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The asymmetric risk profile strongly favors a temporary compliance pause: underreacting risks irreversible consequences (BIS enforcement, Nvidia reallocation, customer defection) while a 1-2 quarter pause in high-risk channels produces bounded, recoverable costs given sustained multi-year AI infrastructure demand.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim overstates both sides of the asymmetry without visible support in the packet. The record shows investor concern that Nvidia relations could be strained and that pausing could protect against deeper fallout, but it does not show any actual Nvidia allocation threat, BIS action, or customer defection underway. On the other side, the artifact asserts that a 1-2 quarter pause is 'bounded' and 'recoverable' because AI demand will persist, yet provides no evidence on Supermicro-specific backlog stickiness, customer switching behavior, or whether hyperscale buyers would wait rather than reallocate orders permanently. The conclusion may be plausible, but the claimed asymmetry is not demonstrated from the visible evidence.
