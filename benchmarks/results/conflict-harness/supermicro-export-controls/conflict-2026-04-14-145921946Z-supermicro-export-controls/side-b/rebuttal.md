# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The reported scale of the alleged export-control conduct and potential Nvidia relationship strain create downside risk that outweighs the near-term upside of preserving maximum growth velocity.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side A treats the Fortune-reported $2.5B figure and investor concern about Nvidia relationship strain as established risk factors sufficient to justify pausing growth. But the artifact's own Unknowns section concedes that 'the precise degree of Nvidia relationship risk is reported as investor concern, not confirmed partner action.' Side A is building its central asymmetry argument — that downside dwarfs upside — on unverified media reporting and market sentiment rather than confirmed regulatory findings, partner statements, or actual supply-chain actions. No evidence in the case packet shows Nvidia has taken or signaled any concrete step to restrict Supermicro's GPU allocation, renegotiate terms, or impose compliance conditions. The $2.5B figure is alleged, not adjudicated, and the indictment names individuals, not the company. Side A acknowledges these gaps but then reasons as though the worst case is the base case, which inflates the perceived downside asymmetry that anchors the entire recommendation. A board memo that recommends material commercial disruption — freezing channels, imposing restart gates, weekly board oversight cadence — should require stronger evidentiary grounding than 'Fortune reported' and 'investors are concerned,' especially when the company is not a defendant and has already separated from the implicated individuals.
