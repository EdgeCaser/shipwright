# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The reported scale and nature of the alleged export-control misconduct create a governance and partner-confidence risk that is more dangerous than the near-term revenue loss from a targeted pause.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B asserts the downside of underreacting is 'more asymmetric' than the downside of a targeted pause, but provides no evidence for the magnitude or probability of the pause-side losses. The case packet notes that hyperscale and enterprise customers may shift orders to rivals and not return quickly — a concrete, near-certain revenue consequence. Side B dismisses this as 'the weaker argument' without quantifying either side of the asymmetry. The $2.5B figure cited from Fortune describes the alleged misconduct's scale, not the expected cost to Supermicro of continued operations; Side B conflates the two. Meanwhile, the company is not named as a defendant, is not accused of wrongdoing, and has already separated the indicted individuals — facts that materially reduce the probability of the governance-crisis scenario Side B treats as the baseline. The claim that partner-confidence risk exceeds revenue-loss risk is asserted, not demonstrated, and rests on a worst-case framing that the available evidence does not yet support.
