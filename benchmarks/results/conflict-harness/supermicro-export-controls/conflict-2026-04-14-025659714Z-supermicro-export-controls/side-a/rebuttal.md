# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Supermicro should pause and narrow high-risk activity rather than continue full-speed AI growth because the cost of underreacting during a live export-controls investigation is greater than the cost of a targeted slowdown.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B asserts that 'the downside of underreacting is structurally larger than the downside of a targeted slowdown' but provides no evidence for the magnitude or reversibility of the slowdown cost. The case packet explicitly warns that 'hyperscale and enterprise customers shift orders to other suppliers and do not return quickly' — a permanent share-loss risk that Side B acknowledges only in passing ('the strongest argument for pressing growth is real') before dismissing it without quantification. Side B cites no precedent, no market data, and no customer-switching analysis to support the claim that a pause is less costly than continued execution. The asymmetry assertion — that underreaction risk exceeds slowdown risk — is the load-bearing claim of the entire memo, yet it rests entirely on hypothetical worst-case regulatory scenarios while treating the concrete, well-documented competitive cost of ceding AI infrastructure orders during peak demand as a secondary concern. Without evidence that paused customers actually return, or that competitors cannot absorb redirected demand permanently, the core cost comparison is unsupported.
