## Product Requirement Direction
Replace the major platform PRD with a narrower decision artifact that separates two decisions:
- whether an immediate response is needed for the two requesting customers
- whether the requested workflow integration should be funded as a reusable platform investment

## Recommendation
Authorize a retention-focused validation and scoping brief rather than a full platform PRD. That brief should evaluate a minimum account-driven response for the requesting customers while explicitly holding open, rather than assuming, a broader platform path.

## Rationale
The current case contains two real signals that must both be respected. First, the requests matter because they come from customers representing 18% of ARR. Second, the evidence does not establish that this is a cross-enterprise pattern because the two customers are from the same niche vertical and there is no broader customer survey. The correct response is not inaction; it is to avoid collapsing an account-risk signal into a generalized platform conclusion without additional validation.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- Whether the need exists outside the requesting niche vertical.
- Whether the right response is a customer-specific delivery path or a reusable platform capability.
- Whether the main objective is retention of the two requesting customers, broader enterprise expansion, or both.
- What minimum scope would address the immediate customer risk without precommitting to full platformization.

Fail for full platform PRD approval now.

Pass conditions for a full platform PRD:
- The team can show this is more than a same-vertical anecdotal pattern.
- The proposed solution is justified as a reusable platform capability rather than a bespoke response.

Pass conditions for an interim plan now:
