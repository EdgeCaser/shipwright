# Platform Workflow Integration PRD Recommendation

## Decision Frame
Do not approve a full 4-month platform engineering commitment on the current business case. The available signal comes from two enterprise customers that together represent 18% of ARR, but both operate in the same niche vertical and there is no broader customer survey. That is enough to justify structured discovery and a narrower validation artifact, not a major platform PRD that assumes general enterprise demand.

## Problem Statement
Two important enterprise customers have requested the same workflow integration. Their revenue concentration makes the request commercially relevant, but the current case does not establish whether the need is specific to their niche vertical or representative of a broader enterprise pattern.

## Goals
- Preserve the opportunity with the requesting customers.
- Avoid locking platform engineering into a major integration without evidence of cross-segment demand.
- Produce a decision-ready follow-up artifact that can support either targeted delivery or broader platform investment.

## Non-Goals
- Treating two same-vertical requests as proof of a general enterprise requirement.
- Committing to a platformized solution scope before validating breadth of need.
- Using a full PRD to stand in for missing market and customer evidence.

## Recommendation
Create a staged validation-and-scope brief instead of a full platform PRD. The brief should separate:
- a customer-specific opportunity for the requesting accounts
- a reusable platform opportunity only if broader demand is demonstrated

## Rationale
The current case mixes account importance with market representativeness. Those are different questions. The revenue exposure makes the request important, but the evidence base is too narrow to support extrapolation because both customers come from the same niche vertical and no broader survey exists.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- Whether customers outside the niche vertical have the same workflow integration need.
- Whether the requested integration requires a broadly reusable platform capability or a narrower account-driven solution.
- Whether the expected business value comes from retention of the two requesting customers, broader expansion, or both.
- What minimum scope would satisfy the immediate customer demand while preserving optionality.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Fail for full PRD approval now.

Pass conditions for a major platform PRD:
- Evidence shows the need extends beyond the two requesting customers.
- The team can distinguish a reusable platform capability from a niche workflow request.
- The business case ties the 4-month investment to validated demand, not anecdotal extrapolation.

Pass conditions for a narrower interim plan:
- The team frames the work explicitly as account-driven rather than market-general.
- Scope is reduced to the minimum needed to address the immediate enterprise risk or opportunity.
- A follow-up decision point is defined before platformizing further.

## Recommended Next Artifact
Produce a validation brief, not a full platform PRD. It should include:
- customer segmentation of the request beyond the niche vertical
- explicit hypotheses about general enterprise demand
- decision criteria for account-specific build versus reusable platform investment
- a reduced-scope option for immediate customer coverage
- a trigger for when a full PRD becomes justified
