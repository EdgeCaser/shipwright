# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Intel should continue structural separation of the foundry business into a ring-fenced subsidiary with outside-capital optionality, rather than revert to tighter full integration.

## Evidence Or Reason
The recommendation treats outside-capital access as a core justification for separation, but provides no evidence that such capital is actually obtainable on non-punitive terms. SIDE_B's own Unknowns section concedes this: 'whether outside investors would fund the foundry subsidiary on acceptable terms in late 2024 or 2025' is explicitly unresolved. A business losing $7B in operating income on $18.9B of sales, with breakeven deferred to sometime before 2030, is a deeply unattractive investment for third-party capital at any moment, let alone during a year when Intel's stock fell ~60%. Structural separation imposes real governance costs and execution friction immediately; the capital benefit it is supposed to unlock is speculative and contingent on market conditions SIDE_B has not modeled. The memo's Pass/Fail section even lists 'outside capital is only available at punitive valuations or with restrictive terms' as a fail condition — which means the primary justification for the recommended path could simultaneously be the trigger for its failure. The claim that separation unlocks a 'credible path to third-party capital' is asserted, not demonstrated, making the core recommendation rest on an unvalidated assumption.
