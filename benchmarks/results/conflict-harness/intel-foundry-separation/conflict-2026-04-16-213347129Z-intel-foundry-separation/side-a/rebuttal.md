# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Intel should avoid pushing further structural separation during a fragile turnaround because additional governance and financing complexity would raise execution risk.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B asserts that governance complexity is the primary execution risk, but never quantifies or substantiates this claim against the known alternative risk: Intel was burning roughly $25B/year on foundry with a $7B operating loss in 2023 and a break-even horizon not until before 2030. The real execution risk during a fragile turnaround is running out of capital and investor confidence — not governance overhead. The $10B cost-reduction plan and European expansion pauses already signal the balance sheet is under severe strain. Deeper separation that unlocks outside capital directly addresses the liquidity constraint that could kill the turnaround. Side B treats 'governance complexity' as a first-order risk without evidence, while ignoring that capital starvation is a more concrete and immediate threat to execution.
