# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-4
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Structural separation via a subsidiary model preserves engineering collaboration with the product business while changing only capital structure and governance.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible record supports only that Intel planned a separate foundry entity with its own operating board and possible outside funding. It does not show that design-manufacturing coordination, transfer-pricing incentives, capacity allocation, roadmap priority, or accountability mechanisms would remain effectively unchanged in practice. Once a unit has separate governance and prospective external investors, those frictions become central rather than incidental, so claiming collaboration is preserved with only capital-structure changes is asserted rather than evidenced.
