# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-4
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Microsoft's desperate need for search scale creates unique leverage for Yahoo to negotiate above the initial $33 offer toward the board's $37 minimum, making a negotiated sale the value-maximizing path.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible evidence shows only that Microsoft needs scale and that Yahoo's board wants $37; it does not show Microsoft's actual willingness or capacity to raise the bid after already withdrawing at $33. The claim jumps from strategic need to assumed pricing leverage without evidence of a competing bidder, financing constraint analysis, or any indication that Ballmer would accept Yahoo's terms rather than walk away again. That makes the $37+ negotiation thesis speculative, not demonstrated.
