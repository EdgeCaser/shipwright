# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Yahoo should reopen negotiations and pursue a sale because the Microsoft bid offers greater realizable shareholder value than Yahoo's lower-confidence standalone path.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B treats the $33/share offer as the baseline for comparison, but concedes in its own Unknowns section that Microsoft's realistic maximum willingness to pay is unknown and that strategic need 'suggests room for improvement.' This is a critical gap: if Microsoft's true ceiling is materially above $33 — even approaching the board's $37 floor — then the relevant comparison is not '$33 vs. standalone' but '$37+ vs. standalone.' Side B never constructs a stand-alone sum-of-the-parts valuation to anchor that comparison. The Yahoo Japan stake (~$8-10B) and Alibaba stake (~$3-4B) alone represent roughly $11-14B of the $47.5B headline offer, meaning the operating business implied by the Microsoft bid is approximately $33-36B. Without a rigorous standalone SoTP, Side B cannot establish that the bid's implied operating value exceeds what Yahoo could realize independently — which is precisely the claim it is making. Asserting the bid 'reflects strategic value that public markets are unlikely to credit' is assertion, not analysis, and Side B acknowledges it lacks the standalone valuation to prove it.
