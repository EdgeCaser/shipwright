# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The post-rejection stock price (~$24) demonstrates the market values Yahoo's independent path at $13/share below Microsoft's offer, confirming the premium is real and substantial.

## Evidence Or Reason
This inference overreads the visible stock-price evidence. A post-rejection trading price does not cleanly equal standalone intrinsic value; it also reflects event-driven factors like residual probability of a renewed Microsoft bid, uncertainty around the Google partnership, and broader market conditions in 2008. The claim supplies no decomposition showing how much of the ~$24 price was true standalone value versus merger-arbitrage expectations, so it cannot support a confident conclusion that the market had marked Yahoo's independent path at exactly $13 below the offer.
