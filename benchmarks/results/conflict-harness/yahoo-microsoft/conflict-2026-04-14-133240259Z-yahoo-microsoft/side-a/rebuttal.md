# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Yahoo's independent strategy is not credible because display leadership does not offset declining search share and inferior search-margin economics versus Google.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim treats search as the only profit pool that matters and dismisses display as structurally inferior, but the evidence provided does not support that conclusion for Yahoo's specific situation. Yahoo was #1 in US display with 15.6% of total digital ad spend — a segment that in 2008 was still larger than search advertising in absolute dollars. The artifact compares Yahoo's total operating income ($695M) to Google's ($5.1B) as proof of structural weakness, but this conflates Google's search dominance with a general verdict on Yahoo's business model. Yahoo's display business had different economics than search and was not in decline in 2008 — it was facing fragmentation risk from Facebook, but that risk was speculative at the time (Facebook had 100-200M users and negligible ad revenue). The artifact provides no evidence that display margins were deteriorating, no revenue trajectory for Yahoo's display segment specifically, and no analysis of whether display plus the 260M+ Mail user base plus Yahoo Finance/Sports engagement constituted a defensible advertising bundle. Declaring independence 'not credible' based on search economics alone ignores that Yahoo's actual revenue center was display, and the artifact offers no data showing that revenue center was failing.
