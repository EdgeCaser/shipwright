# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Search advertising economics are winner-take-all with network effects, and Yahoo's declining 20% share cannot be reversed — the gap with Google (63% share, 3x revenue, 3x margins) will widen, making independent search competition unviable.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible evidence supports that Yahoo is behind in search, but not the stronger conclusion that reversal is impossible or that independent search is unviable. The case packet only shows Yahoo at roughly 20% share, Panama improving revenue-per-search by 15-20%, and Microsoft itself at roughly 10% share despite continued participation. That cuts against a strict winner-take-all framing. No time-series evidence, cost curve, or scenario analysis is provided to prove Yahoo's decline is irreversible rather than difficult, so the claim overstates what the record establishes.
