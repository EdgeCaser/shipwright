# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The market's post-rejection valuation of ~$24/share (vs. rejected $33 offer) signals that investors do not believe independent Yahoo is worth what the board claims, undermining the fiduciary basis for rejection.

## Evidence Or Reason
This inference overreads the stock-price gap. Once the bid became uncertain, Yahoo's trading price would mechanically reflect deal-break probability, timing risk, and merger-arbitrage unwind, not a clean standalone valuation verdict. A ~$24 post-rejection price can be consistent with investors assigning some chance of a renewed bid or a lower takeout, so it does not by itself show that independent Yahoo is worth materially less than $33 or that the board lacks fiduciary basis to reject.
