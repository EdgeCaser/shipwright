# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Yahoo's valuable audience properties and Asian equity stakes strengthen negotiating leverage for a sale more than they justify continued independence.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B's own evidence shows Yahoo Japan at $8-10B and Alibaba at $3-4B, totaling $11-14B of the $47.5B offer. Side B argues these stakes are better used as 'negotiating leverage for sale terms' but never demonstrates that Microsoft would actually pay a premium reflecting their full value. In fact, the artifact acknowledges Microsoft's willingness to pay above $33 'remains untested after withdrawal' — meaning there is zero evidence Microsoft would price these stakes fairly. The logical flaw is severe: if Microsoft won't pay above $33, Yahoo shareholders surrender $11-14B in Asian equity stakes at a deep discount embedded in a blended per-share price. An independent Yahoo retains full optionality on these stakes — including a future Alibaba IPO whose upside Side B completely ignores despite Alibaba's pre-IPO valuation already at $3-4B. Selling to Microsoft crystallizes the value of these stakes at 2008 levels inside someone else's balance sheet, which is the opposite of maximizing value for assets with asymmetric upside.
