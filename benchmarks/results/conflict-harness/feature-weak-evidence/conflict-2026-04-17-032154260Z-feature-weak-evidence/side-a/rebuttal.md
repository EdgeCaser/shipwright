# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The most decision-useful artifact under these conditions is an evidence-gated discovery PRD rather than an implementation commitment.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B asserts that an evidence-gated discovery PRD is the most decision-useful artifact, but provides no criteria for when a conventional PRD would be preferable, nor any acknowledgment that the framing itself is a choice that could be wrong. The claim is self-referentially circular: it cites absent evidence as the justification for producing an artifact type that conveniently requires no evidence to evaluate. A decision-useful artifact should demonstrate that it was chosen over alternatives — Side B never surfaces a competing artifact type, never weighs cost of the discovery phase against urgency or opportunity cost, and never states what conditions would make a different artifact more appropriate. The 'most decision-useful' label is asserted, not argued.
