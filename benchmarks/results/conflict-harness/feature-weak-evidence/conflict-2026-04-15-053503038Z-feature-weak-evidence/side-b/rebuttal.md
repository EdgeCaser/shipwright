## Claim Under Attack
A PRD written under weak-evidence conditions should surface gaps and gate on discovery, not authorize a build — the document's primary value is structuring what must be learned before commitment.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim is presented as the correct handling of the scenario, but the artifact provides no evidence that a weak-evidence PRD must be converted into a discovery-gate document rather than still specifying a tentative feature, scope, assumptions, and decision criteria. The case packet only asks for a PRD under weak supporting evidence; it does not establish that refusing product specification is the appropriate or superior response. Without support from the rubric, validator, or external standard, this is a normative leap rather than an evidence-backed conclusion.
