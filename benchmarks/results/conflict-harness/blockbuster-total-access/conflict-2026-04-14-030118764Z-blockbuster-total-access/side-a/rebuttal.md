# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Total Access's $400M burn is a pricing/policy design flaw (unlimited free exchanges at ~$2 each), not evidence the hybrid strategy is broken — restructuring with exchange caps and tiered pricing can reduce burn by 50-60% while preserving competitive pressure on Netflix.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible evidence only establishes that each in-store return costs about $2 and that the program burns about $400M annually. It does not show the exchange-frequency distribution, subscriber willingness to pay, or churn response to caps/tiering. Without those facts, the memo has not demonstrated that the burn is merely a fixable pricing defect rather than the core economics of the hybrid offer, and the claimed 50-60% cost reduction while preserving competitive pressure is speculative.
