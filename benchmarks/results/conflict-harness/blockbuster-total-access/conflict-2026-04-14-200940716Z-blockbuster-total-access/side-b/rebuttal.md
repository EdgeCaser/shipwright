# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The $400M/year burn is a cost structure problem fixable through caps, tiering, and fulfillment redesign, not evidence the program should be eliminated.

## Evidence Or Reason
The artifact treats a 40-50% cost reduction as achievable, but none of the cited evidence shows Blockbuster can actually impose return caps, raise price, redesign fulfillment, or secure franchisee cooperation without eroding the very consumer value that drove growth. The visible record only establishes that returns cost about $2 each, burn is about $400M/year, debt is $1.1B, and franchisees are already hostile. That supports 'costly and constrained,' not 'fixable at scale.'
