# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The program can be optimized from ~$400M/year to ~$300M/year through exchange caps, tiered pricing, and franchisee revenue-sharing without losing the majority of subscribers.

## Evidence Or Reason
The artifact invents the key economics needed for its recommendation: a $100M annual burn reduction, 30-40% cost cut, and 80%+ subscriber retention. The case packet only establishes the current ~$400M/year burn, ~$2 in-store exchange cost, and franchisee hostility; it provides no evidence that exchange caps or tiering would preserve most subscribers when the generous in-store exchange benefit is the program's differentiator. Without proof that modified Total Access keeps the competitive subscriber gains while materially cutting losses, the recommendation rests on an unvalidated spreadsheet assumption.
