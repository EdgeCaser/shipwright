# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The binary 'continue vs. kill' framing is the strategic error — Total Access should be restructured to ~$200M/year burn (from $400M) by tiering in-store exchanges, with savings redirected to streaming infrastructure.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim smuggles in the memo's core conclusion without visible support. The case packet gives the current ~$400M/year burn and the ~$2 per in-store return cost, but it provides no evidence that capping exchanges would cut burn to ~$200M, no estimate of how many exchanges drive the loss, and no basis for the asserted 40-50% savings. The same gap applies to the proposed streaming reallocation: there is no evidence that Blockbuster could fund a meaningful streaming build with those savings, or that repricing would preserve the subscriber base long enough to realize them. Because the recommendation depends on an unproven numeric bridge from current losses to a sustainable hybrid model, the claim is materially under-evidenced.
