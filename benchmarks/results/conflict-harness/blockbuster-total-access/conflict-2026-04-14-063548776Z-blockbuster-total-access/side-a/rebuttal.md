- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The binary 'continue or kill' framing is a false choice; a 50-60% cost restructuring preserves competitive pressure on Netflix while avoiding insolvency

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim depends on several unproven assumptions that the artifact never substantiates from the visible record: that repricing and return caps would actually cut burn by 50-60%, that subscriber retention would remain high enough to preserve Netflix pressure, and that the board can practically execute a middle path despite franchisee hostility and Icahn-backed pressure for cuts. The case packet gives evidence that Total Access worked when it was generous and expensive, not that a materially weakened version would keep its strategic effect. Without evidence on elasticity, return frequency, contribution margins, or franchisee economics, the proposed 'restructure' outcome is asserted rather than demonstrated.
