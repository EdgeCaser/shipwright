# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Total Access created real competitive pressure on Netflix, but that tactical success does not justify continued funding because it does not create a durable moat in the market's likely shift toward streaming.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side A treats the streaming transition as near-term inevitable, but the evidence in the case packet undermines that timeline. In Q3 2007, Netflix Watch Instantly had only ~1,000 titles, broadband penetration was ~50%, and the DVD-by-mail market was projected to peak in 2010-2012 — meaning 3-5 years of remaining physical-media growth. Side A never quantifies how quickly streaming would become a viable substitute for the full DVD catalog, nor does it address that Netflix itself was still overwhelmingly a disc-by-mail business in 2007. The claim leaps from 'streaming exists' to 'physical-media moats are therefore worthless' without establishing the transition speed. A 3-5 year window of DVD dominance is precisely the period where pressing a competitive advantage that 'scared' Netflix's CEO could permanently alter market share before streaming matures. Side A dismisses this window without evidence that streaming would mature fast enough to make the moat irrelevant within Blockbuster's debt servicing horizon.
