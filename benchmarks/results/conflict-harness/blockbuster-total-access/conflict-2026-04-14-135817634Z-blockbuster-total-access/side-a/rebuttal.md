# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
A controlled drawdown (cap exchanges at 5/month) can retain 70-80% of subscribers while cutting ~60% of program costs, freeing capital for streaming investment that leverages Blockbuster's 47M store-visit customer base as a distribution advantage.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible record does not provide the usage distribution data needed to support either the claimed 70-80% retention or the ~60% cost reduction from a 5-exchange cap. The memo itself lists subscriber elasticity to exchange caps as an unresolved unknown, which directly undercuts presenting these economics as if they were established. Without actual exchange-frequency cohorts, churn sensitivity, and contribution-margin modeling, this is speculative math rather than evidence-backed board guidance.
