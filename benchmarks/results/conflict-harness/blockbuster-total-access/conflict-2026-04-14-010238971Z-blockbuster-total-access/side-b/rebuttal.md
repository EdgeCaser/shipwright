## Claim Under Attack
The current $400M/year burn is unsustainable but can be reduced ~40% through tiered return limits, franchisee revenue sharing, store closures, and selective price increases while preserving competitive pressure.

## Evidence Or Reason
The artifact asserts $150-180M/year in savings from specific reforms, but the visible case packet only establishes the $400M burn, debt, Q2 loss, and store count. It provides no cost breakdown by return volume, subscriber tier behavior, store-level economics, franchise contract terms, or churn impact from limiting returns and raising prices. Without that supporting evidence, the claimed ~40% burn reduction and continued competitive pressure are speculative and central to the recommendation's feasibility.
