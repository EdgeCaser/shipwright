- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The right board action is a time-bounded continuation with tighter unit economics, franchisee alignment, and a required streaming roadmap, not unconditional continuation at the current $400M annual burn.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim presents a redesigned Total Access as a feasible middle path but provides no evidence that Blockbuster had the organizational capacity to execute a simultaneous four-front restructuring (subsidy caps, cohort segmentation, franchisee realignment, and streaming roadmap) within two quarters. The artifact's own Unknowns section admits cohort economics, franchise cannibalization data, streaming capability options, and liquidity headroom are all unquantified — yet the recommendation depends on all four being solvable on a compressed timeline. A company that just replaced its CEO, has an activist investor controlling three board seats, faces franchisee revolt, and is losing $85M per quarter is precisely the kind of organization least equipped to run a complex operational redesign under time pressure. The 'continue but fix everything' framing assumes execution capacity that the case evidence actively contradicts, making this effectively an unfunded mandate dressed as disciplined strategy.
