# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The continue-or-kill framing is a false binary; Blockbuster should restructure Total Access to ~$150-180M/year (50-60% reduction) by capping in-store returns and tiering pricing, preserving competitive pressure while avoiding solvency risk.

## Evidence Or Reason
The visible evidence supports that Total Access is expensive and that in-store returns drive cost, but it does not support the core quantitative leap that simple caps and tiering can cut burn by 50-60% while preserving the subscriber base. The artifact itself admits the key unknown is churn sensitivity to cap changes, which means the recommendation depends on an unvalidated assumption about customer behavior. It also adds franchisee revenue sharing, but that is a new cost line, not proven savings, and the artifact concedes franchise agreements may block it. Without evidence on return-volume concentration, willingness to pay for a premium tier, or churn impact from removing the headline benefit, the proposed middle path is asserted rather than demonstrated.
