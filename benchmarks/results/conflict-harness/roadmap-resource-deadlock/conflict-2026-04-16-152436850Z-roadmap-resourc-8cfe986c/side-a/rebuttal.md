# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The shared engineering team should be assigned to one initiative for the quarter through a forced-ranking process, while the other initiative moves to a defined holding plan rather than receiving split staffing.

## Evidence Or Reason
The forced-ranking recommendation assumes an operating committee with authority to impose a binding decision on two SVP sponsors — but the scenario explicitly states neither sponsor yields and CEO escalation is unavailable. Side B never identifies who holds the authority to enforce the forced ranking or accept the arbitration memo. Without a named decision owner who outranks both SVPs, the 'transparent portfolio decision' collapses back into the same deadlock it purports to resolve. The holding plan also assumes the deferred SVP will accept demotion without political retaliation or covert resource diversion — an assumption with no supporting mechanism.
