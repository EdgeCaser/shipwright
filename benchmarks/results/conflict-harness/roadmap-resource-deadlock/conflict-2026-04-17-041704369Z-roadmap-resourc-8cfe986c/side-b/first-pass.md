# Your artifact

## Decision Frame
Recommend against trying to split the same engineering team across both SVP-sponsored initiatives at the same time. Instead, force a single-quarter sequencing decision at the sponsor level, using an agreed decision rule and a written commitment on what slips, what ships, and when the second initiative gets access to the team. Since CEO escalation is unavailable this quarter, the practical path is a jointly signed operating agreement between the two sponsors and the team leader with one designated first mover and one explicitly scheduled follower.

This is the most decision-useful option under the stated constraints because the deadlock is not a capacity-planning problem alone; it is a governance problem. A shared team cannot serve two initiatives that each claim top priority at the same time without creating ambiguity, unmanaged tradeoffs, and continued conflict.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The packet does not provide evidence about strategic impact, deadlines, contractual obligations, dependencies, regulatory exposure, customer commitments, or reversibility for either initiative. Without that, there is not enough evidence to name which initiative should go first on business merits alone.

The most important missing inputs are:
- what business consequence occurs if each initiative is delayed this quarter
- whether either initiative has an immovable external deadline
- whether one initiative materially unlocks or de-risks the other
- whether either initiative can be reduced to a smaller first tranche that preserves the option value of the other

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if:
- both sponsors accept that one team cannot carry two top-priority efforts simultaneously without an explicit sequence
- the organization agrees on a decision rule for first position this quarter
- the second initiative receives a dated, written re-entry point rather than an indefinite deferment
- the engineering lead confirms the chosen sequence is operationally executable

Fail if:
- both initiatives remain labeled highest priority without a forced tradeoff
- the team is asked to pursue both in parallel without narrowing scope
- no written consequence management exists for what slips as a result of the choice
- the sponsors refuse a shared operating agreement and continue to relitigate priority informally

## Recommended Next Artifact
Produce a one-page resource allocation memo signed by both sponsors and the engineering lead. It should state:
- the initiative sequenced first this quarter
- the explicit reason it was chosen under the agreed decision rule
- the start condition and target handoff point for the second initiative
- the scope reductions or deferrals required to make the sequence real
- the owner responsible for enforcing the agreement and handling disputes below CEO level
