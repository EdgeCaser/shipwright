# Revised artifact

## Decision Frame
Do not treat full-quarter sequencing as the automatic answer. The practical recommendation is to force an explicit sponsor-level allocation decision between two bounded options: (1) full-quarter sequencing with a designated first mover and dated handoff, or (2) parallel partial staffing only if both sponsors accept explicit scope reduction, written tradeoffs, and a single enforcement owner. Since CEO escalation is unavailable this quarter and the packet provides no comparative business evidence for either initiative, the immediate decision should be about operating mode and governance discipline, not an unsupported claim that one initiative must fully displace the other.

The core issue is still governance rather than informal priority claims. A shared engineering team cannot be left serving two initiatives that each assert top priority without a written rule, explicit concessions, and named ownership for enforcement. But the available packet does not justify ruling out constrained parallelism in all cases, so the recommendation should preserve both viable structures and require the sponsors to choose one explicitly.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The packet does not provide evidence about strategic impact, deadlines, contractual obligations, dependencies, customer commitments, reversibility, or the practical divisibility of the engineering work. Because of that, there is not enough evidence to determine whether full sequencing or constrained parallelism is less harmful this quarter, and there is also not enough evidence to identify which initiative should go first if sequencing is chosen.

The highest-value unknowns are:
- whether either initiative has an immovable deadline or delivery commitment this quarter
- whether the engineering work can be split without creating operational failure or constant reprioritization
- whether a reduced tranche for each initiative would preserve acceptable progress on both
- who below the CEO has authority to enforce the chosen operating agreement when disputes recur

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if:
- both sponsors accept that the current deadlock must be converted into a written allocation agreement
- the organization chooses one explicit mode for this quarter: sequencing or constrained parallelism
- the chosen mode includes defined scope boundaries, dated checkpoints, and named enforcement ownership
- the engineering lead confirms the plan is operationally executable for the shared team

Fail if:
- both initiatives continue to be treated as simultaneous top priority without explicit concessions
- the team is asked to absorb both initiatives with no written scope reduction or handoff rules
- sequencing is imposed without stating what slips and why, or parallelism is attempted without narrowing scope
- disputes remain dependent on informal sponsor pressure rather than a signed operating rule

## Recommended Next Artifact
Produce a one-page resource allocation memo signed by both sponsors and the engineering lead. It should state:
- which operating mode is approved for this quarter: sequencing or constrained parallelism
- the decision rule used to choose that mode
- the scope limits, tradeoffs, and consequences accepted by each initiative under the chosen mode
- the dated review point, handoff condition, or checkpoint for revisiting the allocation
- the single owner responsible for enforcement and dispute handling below CEO level
