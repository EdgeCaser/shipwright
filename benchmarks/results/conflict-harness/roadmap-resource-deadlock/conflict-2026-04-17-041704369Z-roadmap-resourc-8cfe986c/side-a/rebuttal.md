# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The recommended action is to sequence the initiatives rather than split the shared engineering team across both at the same time.

## Evidence Or Reason
The artifact asserts sequencing as the superior default without establishing that splitting is always inferior. In some scenarios — particularly when both initiatives have immovable external deadlines acknowledged in the artifact's own open questions — parallel partial staffing with explicit scope reduction per initiative may be less harmful than fully deferring one. The claim treats sequencing as categorically correct, but the artifact simultaneously admits it lacks comparative business evidence (claim 3). A recommendation that concedes insufficient evidence to identify the first-mover on business merits has equally insufficient evidence to rule out partial parallelism. The sequencing recommendation is presented as a governance solution but functions as a content decision: one initiative loses a full quarter. That consequence requires business justification the artifact explicitly says it cannot provide.
