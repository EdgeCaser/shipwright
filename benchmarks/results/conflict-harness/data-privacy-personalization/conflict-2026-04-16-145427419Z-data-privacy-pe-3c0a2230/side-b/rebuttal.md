# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Federated/on-device learning (Option C) may eliminate the privacy conflict entirely and should be evaluated in a short feasibility spike running in parallel with the accuracy window analysis, before any option selection is made.

## Evidence Or Reason
The claim overstates both the likely benefit and the sufficiency of a 2-3 day spike without any visible support. 'Eliminate the privacy conflict entirely' is not established, because on-device personalization can still involve collection, synchronization, telemetry, model updates, or derived-profile handling that may remain subject to the same policy and legal constraints. The artifact also provides no evidence that the current recommendation approach is technically compatible with federated/on-device methods, so treating this as a near-term option-selection input is speculative rather than decision-useful.
