# SMB Market Entry PRD Assessment

## Decision Frame
The requesting team should not treat the current business case as sufficient foundation for an SMB market-entry PRD. The available inputs are enterprise sales data and an intuition-based downward adjustment, but there is no direct SMB evidence to support buyer behavior, conversion, contract value, churn, or overall viability assumptions. The useful decision at this stage is not whether to launch, but whether to authorize targeted validation work before writing a full execution PRD.

## Product Requirement
Before an execution-grade SMB PRD is approved, the team must replace proxy-only assumptions with direct SMB evidence. The immediate objective is to validate whether SMB demand, buying motion, pricing tolerance, and retention dynamics are materially different from enterprise patterns in ways that would change the product, go-to-market plan, or business case.

## Scope
In scope:
- Validate core SMB demand and purchase criteria.
- Test whether enterprise funnel assumptions transfer at all.
- Establish whether contract value and churn assumptions are directionally credible.
- Identify the minimum product, packaging, and onboarding differences required for SMB.

Out of scope:
- Full launch planning.
- Revenue commitments based on current proxy model.
- Final roadmap prioritization for SMB-specific features.

## Success Criteria
A full SMB PRD can proceed only after the team has:
- Direct SMB customer evidence rather than enterprise-only behavioral history.
- At least one validated basis for conversion, value, and retention assumptions in the target segment.
- Clear articulation of where the SMB motion differs from the enterprise motion.
- A business case grounded in observed evidence rather than intuition-adjusted proxy transfer.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The current packet leaves the following unresolved:
- Whether SMB buyers have the same core problem severity as enterprise buyers.
- Whether SMB purchase process, decision-maker profile, and sales cycle resemble enterprise patterns.
- Whether enterprise contract values are directionally usable in SMB.
- Whether churn behavior in SMB invalidates the business case even if acquisition works.
- Whether the product and onboarding experience are appropriate for SMB without major changes.
- Whether the 40% downward adjustment is conservative, insufficient, or irrelevant.

## Risks
The main risks of proceeding with the current PRD are:
- False precision in forecasts derived from a non-validated proxy model.
- Building the wrong product or packaging for the target segment.
- Underestimating churn or overestimating conversion due to segment mismatch.
- Locking leadership into a plan that looks structured but is not decision-ready.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Fail for execution PRD readiness.

Reasoning:
- There is no direct SMB research.
- The business case depends on enterprise metrics used as proxies.
- The key adjustment is intuition-based rather than evidence-based.
- No pilots, interviews, or comparable benchmarks have been conducted.

Pass would require:
- Direct SMB discovery evidence.
- A validated replacement for proxy-only funnel and value assumptions.
- A revised business case that explicitly separates observed facts from remaining assumptions.

## Recommended Next Artifact
Create a pre-PRD SMB validation brief rather than a launch PRD.

That artifact should contain:
- Target SMB segment definition.
- Core assumptions to test.
- Direct customer research plan.
- Pilot or lightweight validation design.
- Decision thresholds for advancing to a full PRD.
- Updated business case structure with assumptions clearly labeled.

## Recommendation
Do not approve a full SMB market-entry PRD on the current basis. Approve a validation-stage artifact whose purpose is to determine whether SMB entry is credible enough to justify a later PRD.
