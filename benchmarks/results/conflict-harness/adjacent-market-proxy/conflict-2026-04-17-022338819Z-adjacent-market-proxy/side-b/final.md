# Revised artifact

## Decision Frame
The team should not approve an execution-grade SMB market-entry PRD on the current basis. The packet supports a narrower decision: authorize a validation-stage work product that is explicitly designed to test whether SMB demand, buying motion, value, and retention differ materially from the enterprise patterns currently being used as proxies.

## Product Requirement
Before a full SMB launch PRD is approved, the team must replace proxy-only assumptions with direct SMB validation. The current business case depends on enterprise conversion, contract value, and churn figures that were adjusted downward by intuition, with no direct SMB interviews, pilots, or comparable benchmarks.

## Scope
In scope:
- Test whether enterprise assumptions transfer to SMB at all.
- Validate SMB buyer needs, buying process, and likely retention dynamics.
- Identify whether product, packaging, onboarding, or go-to-market changes are required for SMB.
- Determine what minimum evidence would justify a later execution PRD.

Out of scope:
- Final launch commitments.
- Revenue planning based on the current proxy model.
- Treating the intuition-based adjustment as validated forecasting logic.

## Success Criteria
A full SMB PRD can proceed only after the team has:
- Direct SMB evidence relevant to buyer behavior and demand.
- A validated basis for key business-case assumptions rather than enterprise proxy transfer alone.
- Clear documentation of which assumptions remain open and which have been tested.
- Enough evidence to justify a concrete launch plan rather than a validation-stage plan.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- Whether SMB buyers have the same problem intensity as enterprise buyers.
- Whether SMB conversion patterns resemble enterprise conversion patterns.
- Whether enterprise contract values are directionally usable for SMB.
- Whether churn in SMB would materially alter the business case.
- Whether the product and onboarding model fit SMB without significant adaptation.
- Whether any subset of existing enterprise data is sufficiently analogous to SMB to justify partial transfer.
- Whether the fastest validation path is discovery, pilot work, or a staged PRD with explicit assumptions.

## Risks
- False precision from unvalidated proxy assumptions.
- Mis-scoping the SMB opportunity because enterprise behavior is treated as transferable without proof.
- Approving an execution plan before the target segment is evidenced.
- Choosing the wrong validation path because the packet does not establish which route is best.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Fail for execution PRD readiness.

Current fail reasons:
- No direct SMB research is present.
- No pilots have been conducted.
- No comparable benchmarks have been conducted.
- Core business-case inputs are enterprise proxies with an intuition-based adjustment.

Pass for a later execution PRD would require:
- Direct SMB validation evidence.
- A revised business case that separates observed evidence from assumptions.
- An explicit record of which validation path was used and what it proved.

## Recommended Next Artifact
Create a validation-stage market-entry brief, not a full launch PRD. That brief should define the assumptions to test, the evidence gaps to close, and the decision rule for choosing among targeted discovery, pilot work, or a staged PRD with explicit assumption flags. The packet supports the need for validation before execution planning, but it does not support naming one validation path as categorically correct.

## Recommendation
Do not approve a full SMB market-entry PRD on the current record. Approve a validation-stage artifact whose purpose is to determine whether SMB entry is credible and which validation path should be used to reach an execution-grade PRD.
