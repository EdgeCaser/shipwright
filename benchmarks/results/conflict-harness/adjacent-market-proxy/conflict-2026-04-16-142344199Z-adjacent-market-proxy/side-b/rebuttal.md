# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
A structured 8-week validation sprint (interviews, pricing experiment, soft pilot, external benchmarks) can resolve all blocking unknowns at minimal cost before any engineering or GTM resources are committed.

## Evidence Or Reason
The claim overstates what the proposed sprint can prove. An 8-week plan cannot credibly resolve churn or unit-economics unknowns when the artifact itself says an ideal churn input is a 6-month pilot cohort, yet the proposed sprint uses only 60-day retention. It also labels the effort 'minimal cost' while assigning AE time, running paid acquisition or landing-page experiments, and recruiting SMB interviews without any cost basis in the case packet. Those specifics may be reasonable hypotheses, but the visible evidence does not support the stronger claim that this sprint will resolve all blockers at minimal cost.
