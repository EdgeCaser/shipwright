# Revised artifact

## Summary
Do not approve an SMB launch PRD yet. The existing enterprise dataset is usable as a bounded prior for hypothesis formation, but not as a decision-grade forecast for SMB entry because the transferability of key metrics is unvalidated and the 40% adjustment is intuition-based. The immediate need is a validation PRD that preserves the proxy data where it is directionally informative, explicitly quarantines where it is weak, and sequences research to reduce the highest-value uncertainties first.

## Problem Statement
The team wants to enter SMB using three years of enterprise sales data as a proxy for SMB demand, conversion, ACV, and churn. No SMB interviews, pilots, or external comparables have been collected, so the current case can frame hypotheses but cannot justify launch commitments.

## Goals
- Determine whether SMB demand, buying motion, price tolerance, and retention are strong enough to support entry.
- Separate transferable enterprise signals from non-transferable ones.
- Reduce uncertainty on the few metrics that most affect go/no-go economics.

## Non-Goals
- Approving full SMB launch scope.
- Locking final pricing, packaging, or GTM motion.
- Treating the current proxy model as a forecast baseline for revenue commitment.

## Working Assumptions and Evidence Discipline
- Enterprise history is a bounded prior, not zero evidence.
- Most likely to transfer directionally: whether the product solves a real workflow problem, broad retention direction if the same core job-to-be-done exists, and likely implementation/support complexity categories.
- Least likely to transfer: acquisition motion, conversion rate, sales cycle length, ACV, packaging fit, and churn magnitude.
- The current 40% downward adjustment is not decision-grade because no uncertainty interval or benchmark supports it.

## Requirements
- Build an SMB validation PRD rather than a launch PRD.
- Use enterprise data only to set initial hypotheses and ceiling/floor ranges, not committed forecasts.
- Run SMB interviews across the intended beachhead segment and recent no-decisions.
- Define a pilot with explicit pricing, packaging, channel, and onboarding assumptions.
- Measure actual SMB lead-to-paid conversion, time to first value, early retention, support load, and gross margin.
- Predefine go/no-go thresholds by metric and confidence level.
- Sequence work by uncertainty reduction value: acquisition/conversion first, ACV and support burden second, early retention third.

## Success Metrics
- Interview completion and pattern saturation in target SMB segment.
- Pilot conversion by channel and motion.
- Observed ACV or monthly price realization versus assumption range.
- Time to first value and onboarding completion.
- 30/60/90-day retention indicators.
- Support cost and margin viability at SMB price points.

## Risks
- Enterprise demand may overstate SMB urgency.
- Low ACV may not support the assumed acquisition or support model.
- SMB churn may be structurally higher even if enterprise retention has been strong.
- A sales-assisted enterprise motion may not translate to SMB efficiently.

## Decision Frame
Recommendation: reject approval of an SMB launch PRD now; approve a validation PRD that uses enterprise data as a bounded prior only. The launch decision should wait until the team validates the highest-sensitivity SMB metrics directly.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- Which SMB sub-segment is the actual beachhead.
- Real SMB acquisition motion and conversion rate.
- Actual price tolerance, packaging preference, and ACV range.
- Early retention and churn shape in the first 90-180 days.
- Support burden and onboarding effort at SMB scale.
- External comparable benchmarks to bound the current 40% adjustment.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass for pilot authorization if the team has a clear beachhead segment, explicit hypothesis ranges derived from enterprise priors, and predefined success thresholds. Pass for launch only if direct SMB evidence shows acceptable conversion, viable realized pricing, manageable support load, and early retention consistent with target unit economics. Fail if launch approval still depends mainly on enterprise proxy rates with unsupported intuition adjustments.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A validation PRD for SMB discovery and pilot execution that includes: segmented hypotheses, a transferability map for each enterprise metric, benchmark-backed assumption ranges, interview plan, pilot design, decision thresholds, and a go/no-go review memo.
