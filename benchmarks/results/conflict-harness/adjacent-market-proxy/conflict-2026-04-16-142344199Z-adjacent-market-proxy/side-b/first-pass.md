# SMB Entry PRD

## Summary
This document should not authorize market entry yet. The current business case relies on enterprise buyer behavior, conversion, contract value, and churn as stand-ins for SMB outcomes, with a 40% intuition-based adjustment and no direct SMB validation. The useful output at this stage is a gated validation PRD for SMB discovery and pilot execution, not a launch PRD.

## Problem Statement
The company wants to assess whether and how to enter the SMB segment. Current evidence is derived from three years of enterprise sales experience, but there is no direct SMB customer evidence, no pilot, and no benchmark set establishing that enterprise economics transfer to SMB.

## Goals
- Determine whether SMB demand, buying behavior, and willingness to pay are materially different from enterprise assumptions.
- Validate whether acquisition, conversion, retention, and support economics can support entry.
- Produce enough direct evidence to decide whether to proceed, delay, or abandon entry.

## Non-Goals
- Full revenue commitment for SMB entry.
- Final pricing and packaging lock.
- Headcount or GTM scale-up beyond a pilot.

## Users and Buyers
Hypothesized SMB buyers and admins are not yet validated. Their decision-maker profile, purchase urgency, evaluation process, and implementation constraints remain unknown.

## Requirements
- Conduct direct SMB research before committing launch scope.
- Run interviews with target SMB prospects and recent non-buyers.
- Define a pilot offer with explicit pricing, packaging, and onboarding assumptions.
- Measure actual SMB funnel metrics rather than proxying enterprise conversion.
- Measure early retention, expansion potential, support burden, and implementation effort.
- Compare pilot results against pre-set thresholds before authorizing broader entry.

## Success Metrics
- Interview completion against target sample.
- Pilot conversion from qualified lead to paid account.
- Time to first value and onboarding completion.
- Early retention and churn indicators.
- Gross margin and support-cost viability at SMB price points.

## Risks
- Enterprise sales behavior may not transfer to SMB self-serve or low-touch motion.
- Contract value may be too low to absorb acquisition and support costs.
- Churn may be materially higher than enterprise baselines.
- The 40% adjustment may understate or overstate market differences without evidence.

## Decision Frame
Recommendation: do not approve an SMB entry PRD based on the current model. Approve only a validation-stage PRD that gates any launch decision on direct SMB evidence from research and pilot results.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- Actual SMB buyer, user, and budget-holder behavior.
- Real SMB pricing tolerance and preferred packaging.
- Conversion rates by channel and motion.
- Retention and churn behavior in the first 90-180 days.
- Support, onboarding, and product-fit differences versus enterprise.
- Comparable benchmarks for similar products in SMB.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass only if the team can show direct SMB evidence from interviews, pilot conversion data, early retention data, and explicit entry thresholds tied to unit economics. Fail if the business case continues to rely primarily on enterprise proxies with intuition-based adjustments.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A validation PRD for SMB discovery and pilot execution, including target segments, interview plan, pilot design, measurable hypotheses, decision thresholds, and a go/no-go review checkpoint.
