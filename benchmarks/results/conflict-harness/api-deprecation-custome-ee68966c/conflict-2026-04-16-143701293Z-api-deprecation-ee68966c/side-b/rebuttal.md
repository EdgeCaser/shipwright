# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
A tiered or conditional extension approach (e.g., Month 12 with incentivized migration) dominates a hard Month 6 sunset on both churn risk and customer feasibility, and should be the default posture pending contract review.

## Evidence Or Reason
The artifact does not provide evidence that a 12-month or tiered approach actually improves outcomes versus a 6-month sunset. There is no contract analysis showing customers would accept an extension structure, no maintenance-cost data showing partial support is affordable, and no migration-readiness evidence showing phased deprecation is technically feasible. Given the memo itself identifies major unknowns on contract terms, functional parity, and cost burden, claiming this posture 'dominates' overstates what the visible record supports.
