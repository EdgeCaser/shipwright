- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-2, side_b-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Because Muse Spark is competitive but not clearly best-in-class, Meta's defensible near-term edge is product distribution and integration, and immediate reopening would leak value before Meta converts its reset into adoption and monetization.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B asserts that a competitive-but-not-superior model gains more value from closed distribution control than from open release. But the artifact provides zero evidence that closed distribution of a non-leading model actually produces durable monetization or retention advantages. The historical precedent cuts the other way: Meta's own experience with Llama showed that open release of a non-leading model generated outsized ecosystem leverage precisely because the model was good-enough, not best-in-class. A merely competitive closed model faces the worst positioning: it cannot win on quality against OpenAI/Anthropic/Google's closed flagships, and it simultaneously forfeits the differentiation and adoption flywheel that openness provided. Side B's own Unknowns section admits Meta does not know how much value Llama openness actually created or what Muse Spark's real competitive position is on high-value workloads — yet the core recommendation depends entirely on the assumption that closed distribution of a roughly-parity model will outperform the demonstrated open strategy. That is a load-bearing claim resting on an acknowledged evidence gap.
