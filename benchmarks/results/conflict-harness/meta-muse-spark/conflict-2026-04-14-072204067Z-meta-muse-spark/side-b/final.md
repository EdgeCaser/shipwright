# Revised artifact

## Recommendation
Meta should keep frontier Muse models closed for now, while committing in advance to open-weight downstream releases only after the frontier has moved ahead. The core reason is narrower than the first draft: closure is not required for Meta to integrate AI into its own products, but it is the cleaner way to protect frontier know-how, preserve selective partner leverage, and capture commercial value while Muse Spark is still only competitive rather than decisively superior.

## Why This Position Holds

### 1. Meta's distribution is an advantage, but not the reason to stay closed
Meta can integrate models across Meta AI, WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, Messenger, and AI glasses whether weights are open or closed. The strategic question is what Meta gains by withholding the newest frontier weights while it is rebuilding credibility. On the current facts, the gain is not basic product control alone; it is keeping the most valuable frontier asset scarce while Meta uses its distribution to generate product learning, partner demand, and monetization before rivals and fast followers can benefit from a fresh open-weight release.

### 2. Muse Spark is competitive, not clearly dominant, so Meta should not commoditize its frontier asset yet
The case packet says Fortune characterized Meta's published benchmarks as competitive with leading models, but not clearly best-in-class across the board. That means Meta is back in contention, not safely ahead. In that position, releasing frontier weights quickly would weaken exclusivity without clear evidence that the ecosystem upside would outweigh the loss of secrecy, partner leverage, and pricing power.

### 3. Closed frontier plus open downstream is the highest-coherence strategy
A full return to immediate openness would restore developer goodwill, but it would also blur the purpose of the expensive superintelligence reset that produced Muse Spark and the larger Muse-family roadmap now in development. A permanently closed posture would also be a mistake because it abandons the ecosystem advantage Meta built with Llama. The disciplined middle path is to keep the frontier closed, then release older, smaller, or distilled descendants once Meta has already captured the first-order product and commercial gains.

## Strongest Counterargument
The strongest case for reopening is that Meta's clearest AI differentiation came from open weights, not from acting like another closed frontier lab. That is real. Open releases improved goodwill, adoption, and strategic distinctiveness.

It is still the wrong near-term move because April 2026 is a recovery-and-proof phase. Meta has just launched the first model from MSL after a costly reset, said larger Muse models are already in development, and is rolling Spark across its product estate while offering only selected partners private-preview API access. In that context, the immediate objective is to prove Meta can turn frontier capability into proprietary product and commercial gains. Reopening too early would spend scarcity before those gains are secured.

## Leadership Action
Keep frontier Muse models closed through the next major generation, expand product rollout quickly, keep API access selective, and publish explicit criteria for when a Muse-family descendant becomes eligible for open release. That preserves optionality without forcing Meta into an ideological swing back to full openness before the new stack has paid off.

## Decision Frame
Choose `Closed Frontier / Open Downstream Later`. The decision turns on sequencing: capture value first from the newest frontier model, then reopen selectively once Meta has moved the frontier forward again.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- We do not have quantified evidence on how much selective closure improves partner economics or monetization versus a simultaneous open release.
- We do not have actual post-launch KPI lift from Meta AI app and website usage.
- We do not have measured developer backlash or ecosystem attrition from the shift away from immediate openness.
- We do not have cost-per-inference or margin data for Muse Spark at Meta scale.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if leadership accepts that the near-term goal is value capture from a newly credible frontier asset, and formalizes a staged roadmap rather than a permanent ideological shift.

Fail if new evidence shows open release would materially expand adoption, talent, and ecosystem leverage without meaningfully weakening commercialization, secrecy, or partner leverage.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A trigger-based operating memo defining when a Muse-family model moves from `frontier closed` to `downstream open`, with thresholds for benchmark distance from frontier, partner revenue, product KPI lift, and ecosystem strategy.
