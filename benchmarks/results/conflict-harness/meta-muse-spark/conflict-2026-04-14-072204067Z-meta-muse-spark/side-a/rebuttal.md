# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Meta should keep frontier Muse models closed in the near term because its strongest advantage is product distribution and integration across owned surfaces, not open-weight ecosystem reach.

## Evidence Or Reason
The claim that distribution is Meta's 'strongest near-term edge' is asserted without evidence that closed-model control is necessary to exploit that distribution. Llama models were open-weight yet Meta still deployed them across its own products with full control over product integration, latency, safety, and personalization. The case packet provides no evidence that open weights ever impaired Meta's ability to integrate models into its owned surfaces. Side B conflates two independent variables — product integration quality and weight openness — without demonstrating a causal link. Meta can ship a tightly integrated product experience AND release weights simultaneously, as it did throughout the Llama era. The argument that 'closed preserves tight control' needs to show what specific product control is lost when weights are published, and it does not.
