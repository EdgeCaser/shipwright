- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Meta can preserve the benefits of openness later through a sequenced hybrid strategy that keeps frontier models closed while opening lagged or smaller Muse-family variants once product and business thresholds are met.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim assumes developer goodwill and ecosystem momentum are recoverable after a 12-18 month closed period, but provides zero evidence for that assumption. The artifact's own Unknowns section lists 'whether developers would materially punish Meta for delaying openness' as an unresolved question — yet the recommendation treats the answer as benign. Historical precedent runs the other way: developer ecosystems that lose trust in a platform's openness commitments (e.g., Oracle/Java, Twitter API) do not simply snap back when the platform later re-opens. The Llama ecosystem took years to build; dismissing its fragility as a recoverable variable while simultaneously acknowledging it as an unknown is internally contradictory. The 'open lagged variants later' path also lacks any specified timeline, threshold, or commitment mechanism — making it an unfalsifiable promise rather than a credible strategic hedge. Without evidence that delayed openness actually preserves ecosystem value, this claim is load-bearing but unsupported.
