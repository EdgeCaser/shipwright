# Revised artifact

## Recommendation
Meta should reopen the frontier roadmap after Muse Spark rather than make closed frontier models the enduring default. Muse Spark can remain closed as a near-term bridge for product integration, partner learning, and launch control, but the company should not convert one controlled launch into a permanent strategic identity.

The revised reason is narrower and more defensible than the first pass: the case packet does not prove that Meta cannot become best-in-class on a closed path, but it also does not prove that it will. What the packet does show is that Muse Spark is competitive rather than clearly superior today, that larger Muse-family models are already in development, and that Meta's prior differentiation came from the open Llama strategy rather than from leading a closed frontier stack. That means leadership is deciding under uncertainty. In that setting, the safer strategic choice is to preserve the temporary benefits of closure without surrendering the differentiator that previously gave Meta ecosystem reach and developer goodwill.

A pure stay-closed strategy asks Meta to compete more directly on product execution, monetization speed, secrecy, and capital intensity against rivals already established in that lane. The packet identifies real benefits to that approach, and the improving trajectory at MSL is a legitimate counterargument. But trajectory is not the same as evidence of durable dominance. Until Meta has stronger proof that closed-model leadership itself is the source of a durable advantage, it should avoid locking the company into a strategy that blurs its identity and weakens the ecosystem position that was previously distinctive.

The best course is sequencing, not reversal for its own sake: keep Muse Spark closed long enough to finish rollout across Meta surfaces and learn from selected partners, then publicly commit that frontier successors will move back toward open-weight release once the immediate product-first phase is complete. That preserves control where the packet says control matters, while keeping Meta aligned with the strategic posture that most clearly differentiated it.

## Strongest Counterarguments
The strongest counterargument is that Meta's trajectory may now be sharply improving. MSL rebuilt its stack in nine months, Muse Spark is already in market, and larger Muse-family models are in development. On that reading, a closed strategy could be the right way to buy time, protect progress, and convert improving capability into leadership.

That argument is credible, but still insufficient to justify a permanent closed default. The packet supports momentum, not proof that momentum will translate into a durable closed-model edge. In the absence of that proof, leadership should treat closure as a tactical phase rather than as the long-run strategy.

## Why Reopening Is Still Correct
Reopening remains the better strategic recommendation because it matches the evidence actually available. The packet gives direct support for open-weight releases strengthening developer trust, ecosystem adoption, and Meta's strategic differentiation. It also makes clear that staying closed pushes Meta into more direct competition with closed rivals. Since the packet does not establish decisive superiority for Muse Spark, the burden of proof should be on the strategy that requires Meta to abandon its historical differentiator, not on the strategy that preserves it.

## Decision Frame
Choose a time-bounded closed launch strategy for Muse Spark, followed by a return toward open-weight frontier successors. The key judgment is not whether temporary closure is useful. It is whether current momentum is enough to justify replacing Meta's open strategic identity with a permanently closed frontier posture. The case packet does not support that stronger conclusion.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The biggest unknown is whether Meta's current trajectory will become durable frontier dominance or remain broad competitiveness without clear separation. The packet also does not show how much monetization, partner leverage, and product control a sustained closed strategy would create relative to the ecosystem and identity costs of abandoning openness. A further gap is how much developer trust would erode if multiple successive frontier models remain closed.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if leadership accepts a sequencing approach: closed for the current launch phase, open as the directional roadmap once rollout learning is captured. Fail if leadership treats positive trajectory alone as sufficient proof that Meta should permanently compete as a conventional closed frontier lab.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A decision memo comparing two forward paths for the next Muse-family release: remain closed by default versus reopen with open-weight successors, using explicit criteria for product control, ecosystem leverage, strategic differentiation, and evidence of durable model leadership.
