# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Meta should not make a closed frontier roadmap its default because the case packet shows Muse Spark is competitive but not clearly superior, making direct imitation of closed rivals strategically weak.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B treats 'competitive but not clearly best-in-class' as a stable condition, but this is a snapshot from launch day. The packet states MSL rebuilt its stack in nine months and that larger Muse-family models are already in development, suggesting the competitive gap is a temporary state, not a structural ceiling. If Meta is on an improving trajectory, the correct inference is that a closed strategy buys time to reach dominance — not that the current parity proves dominance is unachievable. Side B never engages with this trajectory argument, leaving its central premise — that Meta cannot win on closed-model merit — unsubstantiated rather than demonstrated.
