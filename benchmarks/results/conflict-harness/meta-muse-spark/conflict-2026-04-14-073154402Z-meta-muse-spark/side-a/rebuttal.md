- Attack Type: evidence_gap

## Claim Under Attack
Meta can manage the downside of closing Muse by preserving a credible future reopen path through lagged open releases or smaller open models rather than reopening the frontier roadmap immediately.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim assumes Meta can credibly promise a future reopen without providing any evidence that such promises retain developer trust. The case packet contains no data on developer sentiment response to the closed launch, no precedent showing that 'we hope to open-source future versions' statements sustain ecosystem engagement, and no mechanism forcing Meta to follow through. In practice, once a company shifts to closed-model economics and builds monetization around API gating and product integration, the internal incentives to reopen diminish with each quarter of closed-model revenue. The artifact identifies this exact gap in its own Unknowns section ('no evidence on whether developer sentiment has already shifted materially') yet still treats the reopen option as a reliable hedge. A hedge you cannot verify is not a hedge — it is an aspiration. Without concrete trigger conditions, committed timelines, or evidence that lagged-open strategies have preserved ecosystem trust elsewhere in the industry, this claim is structurally unsupported.
