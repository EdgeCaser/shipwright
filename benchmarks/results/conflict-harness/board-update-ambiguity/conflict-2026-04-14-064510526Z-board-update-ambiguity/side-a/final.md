# Strategy Update: Board Briefing Under Ambiguity

## Decision Frame

**Core question:** Should the board authorize continued investment in the current strategic direction given material uncertainty in three key areas, or should we pivot resources toward a hedged portfolio approach?

**Why this decision matters now:** The current planning cycle closes in Q3. Committing resources past this window locks capital allocation for 12–18 months. Delaying the decision is itself a decision — it preserves optionality but foregoes first-mover positioning in segments where early commitment correlates with durable advantage.

**Decision type:** Resource allocation under ambiguity — not a go/no-go gate, but a calibration of bet size and reversibility.

**Stakeholder alignment status:** Executive team is aligned on the problem definition but split on response magnitude. Two camps exist: (1) maintain current trajectory with tighter monitoring triggers, (2) redistribute 30% of committed capital toward adjacent opportunities with shorter feedback loops.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

| Unknown | Impact if resolved favorably | Impact if resolved unfavorably | Current evidence quality | Expected resolution timeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Market demand elasticity** in target segment | Validates 3-year revenue model; justifies full commitment | Requires 40%+ downward revision to base case | Weak — based on analog markets, no direct signal | 6–9 months via pilot data |
| **Regulatory trajectory** in primary geography | Clears path for planned expansion phases 2–3 | Delays or blocks expansion; strands phase-1 investment | Moderate — legislative signals directionally positive but non-binding | 3–6 months; key committee vote scheduled Q3 |
| **Competitive response timing** | If slow, we capture share; validates premium positioning | If fast, margin compression accelerates; payback extends 2+ years | Weak — competitor signals are ambiguous; one filed patents, another cut headcount | Uncertain; observable only after our market entry |

**What we know with confidence:**
- Unit economics at current scale are positive (contribution margin 34%, validated over 4 quarters).
- Team capability is sufficient for Phase 1 execution; Phase 2 requires 2–3 senior hires not yet in pipeline.
- Customer retention in pilot cohort exceeds plan (88% vs. 75% target).

**What we do not know and cannot cheaply learn:**
- Whether the regulatory environment will remain permissive past the current legislative session.
- Whether competitor X's patent filing signals market entry or defensive positioning.
- Whether demand elasticity observed in pilot (small N, self-selected) generalizes to broader market.

**Evidence we are choosing not to collect and why:**
- Full-scale market study (~$400K, 4-month timeline) — cost exceeds information value given that pilot expansion delivers directional signal at lower cost and faster.
- Competitive intelligence engagement — risk of signaling our intentions outweighs incremental insight.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

**Are we ready to make this decision today?** No — but we are ready to define the *structure* of the decision triggers. The specific thresholds require calibration before they become operative.

**Readiness assessment by dimension:**

| Dimension | Ready? | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Financial model stress-tested | Partial | Downside case uses assumed elasticity, not observed |
| Team and execution capacity | Yes for Phase 1 | Phase 2 hiring pipeline not yet started |
| Regulatory risk characterized | Partial | Directional signals only; no binding commitments |
| Board information sufficiency | No | This briefing is the first structured presentation of unknowns |
| Reversibility if wrong | Yes | Phase 1 spend is recoverable (infrastructure has resale/reuse value); Phase 2 commitment is not |

**Proposed trigger framework (structure approved today; thresholds calibrated before Q3):**

The triggers below use illustrative thresholds to convey the framework's logic. **These are not yet decision-grade.** Before the Q3 gate, finance must back-solve each threshold from the board's stated risk tolerance and the stress-tested financial model. The board's action today is to approve the trigger *structure* and direct the CFO to return with calibrated cutoffs.

- **Proceed signal:** Pilot cohort 2 retention ≥ [threshold derived from breakeven LTV/CAC at downside elasticity] AND regulatory committee does not table the bill → authorize Phase 2 hiring and capital.
- **Pause signal:** Pilot cohort 2 retention < [threshold below which 3-year NPV turns negative under base case] OR regulatory committee tables/amends bill with restrictive provisions → hold Phase 1 only, reassess in Q4.
- **Abort signal:** Competitor launches comparable product at ≥ [price discount that compresses margin below contribution breakeven] AND pilot retention below pause threshold → wind down Phase 1 with asset recovery plan.

**Calibration method:** The CFO should derive each threshold by running the financial model backward from the board's minimum acceptable IRR under the downside scenario. This grounds the triggers in the company's actual economics rather than in round-number heuristics.

## Recommended Next Artifact

**What the board should request next:** A *Decision Gate Package* for the Q3 board meeting, containing:

1. **Calibrated trigger thresholds** — CFO-owned, derived from stress-tested financial model against stated risk tolerance, replacing the illustrative thresholds in this briefing with decision-grade cutoffs.
2. **Pilot Cohort 2 results** — structured as a pass/fail readout against the calibrated trigger thresholds, not as a narrative progress report.

4. **Phase 2 hiring pipeline status** — so the board can assess whether execution capacity will be ready if the proceed signal fires.
5. **Capital allocation options memo** — presenting the full-commitment vs. hedged-portfolio approaches with explicit trade-offs, IRR ranges, and reversibility characteristics for each.

**Format:** Written pre-read (max 5 pages) circulated 7 days before Q3 meeting, with 15-minute live briefing focused on the trigger readout and board discussion of allocation preference.
