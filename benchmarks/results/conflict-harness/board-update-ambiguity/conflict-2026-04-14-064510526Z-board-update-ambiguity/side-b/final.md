# Revised artifact

## Strategy Update

### Executive Summary
Management should not ask the board to bless a single forecast while core facts remain unresolved. The board should approve a 90-day ambiguity-reduction plan: keep funding low-regret work that increases learning, block irreversible scale commitments, and require a return review against three explicit gates.

### Current Situation
The opportunity may still be attractive, but the case packet contains no underlying evidence pack, so the update must treat uncertainty as a first-class operating condition rather than bury it in narrative. The board decision is therefore a governance choice: whether to preserve option value while forcing fast validation, or to let the company drift into implied commitment without proof.

## Decision Frame
Approve a staged plan for the next operating window with three concrete board controls:
1. Continue reversible investments only: customer discovery, limited pilots, and milestone delivery needed to generate evidence.
2. Prohibit irreversible commitments before gate clearance: no scaled hiring plan, no broad market launch commitment, and no long-dated spend justified by unproven demand.
3. Require a formal gate review at the end of the window, even if evidence is mixed.

The three load-bearing assumptions that management must now test are:
1. Customer urgency: target customers will convert stated interest into a real commitment.
2. Economic viability: the offering can progress without requiring structurally uneconomic delivery.
3. Execution reliability: the team can hit the next milestone without hidden dependency risk breaking timing.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
No source evidence was provided, so the update should name the gaps directly.

Evidence required against each assumption:
- Customer urgency: at least one observable commitment signal from the target segment, such as a signed pilot, paid design-partner agreement, or equivalent production-use commitment. If none appears by the next checkpoint, demand remains unproven.
- Economic viability: pilot delivery must show a repeatable path that does not depend on exceptional manual effort or one-off concessions. If early wins require bespoke support that cannot plausibly scale, economics remain unproven.
- Execution reliability: the next milestone must ship without repeated slip or a newly discovered critical dependency. If the milestone slips twice or becomes blocked by an unowned dependency, execution confidence is unproven.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass at the next board checkpoint if all of the following are true:
- Management presents evidence for each of the three named assumptions, not just activity completed.
- At least one real customer commitment signal exists.
- The next product milestone is delivered, or any deviation is contained and explained without invalidating the plan.
- No request is made for irreversible scale spend ahead of evidence.

Fail at the next board checkpoint if any of the following are true:
- There is still no observable customer commitment.
- Early traction depends on bespoke economics that do not generalize.
- Execution slips materially enough to undermine the timing premise.
- Management asks for hiring or launch expansion before the gates are cleared.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A decision-gated board memo with an assumption register, evidence table, and explicit action by scenario:
- If customer commitment and execution evidence are positive, authorize the next tranche of investment.
- If signals are mixed, extend the test window with narrowed scope.
- If customer commitment is absent or economics fail, stop scale investment and revisit strategy.
