## Claim Under Attack
The decision should not be made today; instead the board should adopt an explicit trigger framework (proceed/pause/abort signals tied to observable metrics) that converts ambiguity into a structured decision gate at Q3.

## Evidence Or Reason
The trigger framework is presented as decision-useful, but the artifact itself admits the thresholds are illustrative and unsupported by case-specific evidence or a calibrated financial model. Without grounded justification for why 80% retention, 65% retention, or a 20% price discount are the right cutoffs, the framework risks giving false precision rather than actually converting ambiguity into a defensible board decision gate.
