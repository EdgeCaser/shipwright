# Strategy Update

## Executive Summary
The business is operating under material uncertainty, so the board decision is not whether to fully commit to a single outcome now, but whether to authorize a staged plan that preserves option value while forcing fast evidence collection. The recommended posture is controlled advancement: continue funding the highest-confidence near-term actions, defer irreversible bets, and require explicit decision gates tied to a short list of unknowns.

## Current Situation
We have enough signal to say the opportunity remains plausible, but not enough verified evidence to claim forecast precision. In this context, the main strategic risk is false certainty: either overcommitting before the facts are mature or underinvesting and losing time on a potentially viable path. The right board-level response is to narrow uncertainty quickly rather than to debate ungrounded point estimates.

## Implications for Board Oversight
Board attention should center on three questions:
1. Which assumptions are load-bearing for the plan?
2. Which decisions are reversible versus irreversible?
3. What evidence must be obtained before capital, hiring, or go-to-market commitments expand?

A useful board update under ambiguity should therefore frame progress in terms of assumptions retired, risks reduced, and gates cleared, not just activity completed.

## Decision Frame
Approve a staged strategy for the next operating window with three rules:
1. Fund execution against the core plan only where actions are low-regret and increase learning.
2. Delay high-cost or difficult-to-reverse commitments until defined evidence thresholds are met.
3. Require management to return with a gate review once the top uncertainties are resolved or a predefined timebox expires.

This decision preserves momentum while maintaining discipline. It is the most decision-useful approach when directional confidence is higher than forecast confidence.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The update cannot responsibly claim certainty on market response, timing, or resource efficiency because no supporting evidence pack is provided in the case packet. The key gaps are:
- Demand certainty: whether the target problem is urgent enough to support sustained adoption.
- Economic certainty: whether the path can produce acceptable unit economics or strategic leverage.
- Execution certainty: whether the team can deliver the next milestone on the required timeline and quality bar.
- Risk concentration: whether one hidden dependency could invalidate the near-term plan.

Until these are evidenced, management should present ranges, scenarios, and trigger conditions rather than single-case forecasts.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass conditions for the next board checkpoint:
- The top strategic assumptions are explicitly named and ranked.
- Each assumption has an owner, evidence method, and target date.
- Reversible and irreversible decisions are clearly separated.
- A scenario-based plan shows what management will do under positive, mixed, or negative signal.

Fail conditions:
- Management continues to present confidence without evidence.
- Material spending or hiring is requested before the decision gates are met.
- Progress is reported as activity volume rather than uncertainty reduction.
- The board cannot tell what would cause the strategy to change.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A decision-gated strategy memo with scenario ranges, assumption register, and explicit go/no-go thresholds. That artifact should be the next board-facing document because it converts ambiguity into a concrete governance mechanism rather than a narrative update alone.
