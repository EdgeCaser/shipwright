## Evidence Or Reason

The artifact asserts 'insufficient signal,' 'observed interest,' 'acceptable unit economics,' and 'current capacity' without citing any actual data, metrics, or source materials. The five enumerated evidence gaps (demand certainty, economic quality, execution feasibility, timing sensitivity, risk exposure) are presented as open questions rather than assessed against available information, making it impossible for a board to evaluate whether the 'conditional pass' verdict is calibrated or arbitrary. A strategy update that recommends deferring commitment due to ambiguity must demonstrate it has exhausted available evidence before concluding the evidence is insufficient — otherwise the ambiguity may be an artifact of incomplete analysis rather than genuine market uncertainty. The pass/fail thresholds are qualitative ('enough signal,' 'too high relative to confidence level') with no quantified criteria, rendering the staged-vs-full distinction unfalsifiable.
