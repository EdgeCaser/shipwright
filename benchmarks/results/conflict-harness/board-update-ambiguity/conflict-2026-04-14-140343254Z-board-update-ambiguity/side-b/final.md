# Strategy Update: Board Briefing Under Ambiguity

## Decision Frame

The board faces a strategic inflection point where key market signals are contradictory and incomplete. This update does not recommend a final commitment. Instead, it frames the decision the board must make, surfaces what we know and don't know, and proposes the minimum next step to reduce uncertainty before capital is committed.

**Core question:** Should we accelerate investment in the current growth vector, pivot toward an adjacent opportunity, or hold position until signal quality improves?

**Why now:** Three converging pressures force this onto the agenda: (1) a competitive move that may reshape channel economics within two quarters, (2) a customer concentration risk surfaced in the latest retention cohort, and (3) a pending budget cycle that locks resource allocation for 6+ months.

**What this update is not:** This is not a recommendation to act. It is a structured account of what we know, what we don't, and what it would take to know enough.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

| Unknown | Impact if Wrong | Current Evidence Quality | Gap-Closing Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Competitor's channel pricing durability | High — could erode 15-20% of margin in affected segments | Low — based on two distributor reports, no direct confirmation | Commission competitive intelligence sweep; 3-week turnaround |
| Customer concentration trajectory | Medium — top-5 accounts represent 38% of ARR, trending upward | Medium — 4 quarters of data, but no churn-intent signal | Conduct structured account health interviews with CS team |
| Adjacent market willingness-to-pay | High — determines whether pivot option is viable at all | Very low — one exploratory conversation, no quantitative WTP data | Run Van Westendorp or Gabor-Granger on 30+ prospects; 6-week turnaround |
| Internal capacity to execute pivot | Medium — engineering team has not scoped the adjacent product | Low — verbal estimates only, no spike or design review | Request 2-week engineering spike with written estimate |

**Key observation:** No single unknown is dispositive on its own, but the combination means any major commitment made today rests on assumptions we have not validated. The risk is not that we lack a strategy — it is that we lack the evidence to distinguish between two plausible strategies.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

**Readiness verdict: NOT READY for a major resource commitment.**

The criteria below are proposed by management as minimum evidence thresholds for a sound board-level commitment. They are not externally validated benchmarks; they represent our best judgment about what the board would need to see before allocating capital with confidence. The board should challenge whether these are the right gates and whether the thresholds are appropriately calibrated.

| Criterion | Status | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Competitive threat validated by ≥2 independent sources | ❌ Fail | Only distributor hearsay; no pricing documentation or customer confirmation |
| Customer concentration risk bounded with retention confidence | ❌ Fail | Quantitative trend exists but no leading indicators of churn intent |
| Adjacent market WTP validated quantitatively | ❌ Fail | Single anecdotal data point is insufficient for a go/no-go |
| Engineering capacity estimated with written spike | ❌ Fail | Verbal estimates are unreliable for board-level resource decisions |
| Financial model stress-tested under bear case | ⚠️ Partial | Model exists but uses optimistic assumptions on channel stability |

**Current standing: 0 of 5 criteria fully met, 1 partial.** This means no gating criterion clears its evidence bar outright, though the financial model is closest. A board that accepts higher risk tolerance could treat the partial criterion as sufficient and lower the bar on one or two others — but that is a deliberate risk acceptance decision, not evidence-based confidence.

**What "ready" looks like:** At least 3 of 5 criteria at Pass, with the remaining gaps explicitly accepted as risks with documented mitigation plans.

## Recommended Next Artifact

**Recommendation: Commission a 6-week Evidence Sprint, not a strategy revision.**

The gap is not in strategic thinking — it is in evidence quality. Producing another strategy deck on the same information base will not reduce the board's risk.

**Proposed deliverable:** A *Decision-Ready Brief* in 6 weeks that includes:
1. Competitive intelligence package with source triangulation
2. Account health scorecard with churn-risk indicators
3. Adjacent market WTP analysis (n≥30)
4. Engineering spike write-up with effort/risk assessment
5. Updated financial model with scenario bands (base/bear/bull)

**Cost of the sprint:** Approximately $40-60K in research costs plus 2 weeks of engineering time for the spike. This is small relative to the capital at risk in a premature commitment.

**Cost of skipping the sprint:** The board commits resources against assumptions. If the competitive threat is overstated, we over-invest defensively. If the adjacent market lacks WTP, we burn a quarter on a pivot that stalls. Either error is significantly more expensive than the sprint.

**Board action requested:** Approve the 6-week evidence sprint and schedule a follow-up board session to review the Decision-Ready Brief before any resource reallocation.
