# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Current ambiguity is concentrated in market, economics, execution, and timing, and any one of these gaps could materially change the right decision.

## Evidence Or Reason
The four named uncertainty categories (market, economics, execution, timing) are generic taxonomy labels that apply to virtually any strategic decision in any industry. The artifact provides no evidence that these are the *actual* uncertainties governing this specific situation rather than a boilerplate framework pasted over the prompt. A board-ready strategy update must identify the concrete unknowns that matter here — e.g., a specific customer segment whose adoption rate is unvalidated, a specific cost line whose scaling behavior is unproven. By listing every possible category of uncertainty without prioritizing or specifying any, the claim creates an illusion of analytical rigor while actually telling the board nothing about what they don't know. The claim that 'each one can independently change the correct board posture' further undermines decision usefulness: if all four gaps are equally material and none is prioritized, the implicit recommendation is permanent deferral, since resolving all four simultaneously before acting is operationally implausible. This is not evidence discipline — it is ambiguity laundering.
