# Strategy Update: Board Decision Under Ambiguity

## Current Situation
We are operating in a high-ambiguity environment with insufficient evidence to support an irreversible strategic commitment. The immediate objective is not to force precision we do not have, but to preserve decision quality while accelerating learning on the few variables that will determine whether we should scale, hold, or narrow scope.

## What We Believe
1. The core opportunity appears directionally credible, but confidence is not yet high enough for full-scale expansion.
2. The main risk is not only choosing the wrong strategy, but locking into cost, sequencing, or narrative commitments before the evidence base is mature.
3. The highest-value move in the next period is a staged strategy: protect optionality, concentrate resources on a small set of proof points, and define explicit triggers for escalation or retrenchment.

## Recommended Near-Term Direction
We should continue forward, but in a gated mode rather than as a broad commitment. Management should focus the next cycle on:
- validating the few assumptions that most affect upside and downside
- limiting fixed-cost exposure until those assumptions are tested
- preparing a sharper decision memo once the uncertainty has narrowed

## Decision Frame
Board decision requested: endorse a staged execution posture rather than a full commitment or a full stop.

This means:
- approve continued investment only at a level necessary to resolve the highest-value uncertainties
- require management to return with explicit decision thresholds tied to observed evidence
- avoid expanding scope, headcount, or external promises until those thresholds are met

The practical choice is not "go or no-go." It is "how much conviction do we require before scaling?" The recommended answer is: more than we currently have.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The decision remains constrained by four evidence gaps:
- market certainty: we do not yet know whether demand is broad, urgent, and repeatable versus episodic or segment-specific
- economic certainty: unit economics, payback, or resource efficiency are not yet proven at the level needed for scale confidence
- execution certainty: we have not yet demonstrated that the team can deliver the strategy reliably without disproportionate coordination or operational drag
- timing certainty: it is unclear whether acting now creates durable advantage or merely accelerates spend ahead of proof

These gaps are material because each one can independently change the correct board posture.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if, by next review, management can show:
- a small set of validated assumptions tied to adoption, economics, and execution feasibility
- clear thresholds for scaling, holding, or narrowing the strategy
- evidence that the opportunity remains attractive after accounting for delivery cost and timing risk

Fail if, by next review, we still see:
- narrative confidence without decision-grade evidence
- expanding scope before core assumptions are resolved
- inability to specify what evidence would change the recommendation

Current readiness: conditional pass for continued staged execution; not ready for full-scale commitment.

## Recommended Next Artifact
The next artifact should be a decision memo that converts ambiguity into a threshold-based board choice.

It should include:
- the 3-5 critical assumptions that govern the strategy
- the evidence collected against each assumption
- explicit scale/hold/stop triggers
- the capital and organizational exposure under each path
- a recommendation updated against those triggers

That artifact is the right bridge from directional strategy to board-actionable commitment.
