- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-1, side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The board should adopt a staged preservation-and-proof posture instead of approving irreversible expansion under current ambiguity, because the packet provides no operating facts to justify a stronger directional commitment.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side A treats the absence of evidence in the case packet as evidence for a specific strategic posture (preservation-and-proof / defensive hold). This is a category error: 'no data was provided' is a constraint on the exercise, not a business signal. A board update written under ambiguity should acknowledge that ambiguity cuts in both directions — the same information vacuum that argues against irreversible expansion equally argues against defaulting to cost containment and defensive posture. Side A's artifact repeatedly converts 'we don't know' into 'therefore hold,' which is itself an unsupported directional commitment dressed as prudence. The Pass/Fail Readiness section reinforces this bias by defining failure as scope expansion but never defining failure as missed opportunity from excessive caution. A genuinely evidence-disciplined artifact would present the preservation posture and an opportunistic posture as co-equal options gated by the same unknowns, rather than privileging one as the 'most defensible stance' without any operating data to support that framing.
