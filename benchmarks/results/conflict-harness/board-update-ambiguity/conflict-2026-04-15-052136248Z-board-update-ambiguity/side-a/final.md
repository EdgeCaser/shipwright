# Strategy Update: Q2 2026 — Operating Under Ambiguity

---

## Decision Frame

**What decision does this update support?**

The board must decide whether to maintain current resource allocation into Q3 or redirect capital toward one of two contingent paths — acceleration or consolidation — before the next funding milestone.

**The core tension:** We have enough signal to act, but not enough to act with conviction. The risk of waiting for certainty exceeds the risk of committing to a conditional path now.

**Decision type:** This is a *staged commitment* decision, not a binary bet. The board is not being asked to choose a destination — it is being asked to authorize the next leg of travel and set the conditions under which we re-examine direction.

**Framing constraint:** Recommendations below are scoped to a 90-day horizon. Assumptions beyond that window carry too much variance to treat as load-bearing.

| Unknown | Why It's Load-Bearing | Current Signal | Resolution Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| U-1: Market demand at target price point | Determines unit economics viability | Directional (n=12 conversations) | 6–8 weeks via pilot |
| U-2: Regulatory posture in secondary markets | Gates geographic expansion | Ambiguous — conflicting guidance | 10–12 weeks via counsel |
| U-3: Competitor product release schedule | Affects timing urgency for differentiation | Unconfirmed rumor, no primary source | Unknown |

**Evidence gaps that are NOT load-bearing** (listed for transparency, but do not affect the recommendation):

- Brand perception benchmarks (irrelevant until retention data matures)

**Evidence discipline note:** We have not yet run a structured experiment on U-1. The 12 conversations are useful directional signal but do not constitute proof. The board should treat current demand assumptions as *hypotheses under test*, not validated conclusions.

Readiness is assessed against the criteria required to authorize each path.

### Path A — Acceleration (increase investment, expand headcount, accelerate go-to-market)

| Criterion | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Demand signal sufficient to justify CAC | AMBER | Directional yes, not quantified |
| Unit economics model closed | RED | Missing conversion rate data from pilot |
| Key hire secured | AMBER | In process, not closed |

| Board-approved budget envelope | GREEN | Pre-authorized up to threshold |

**Verdict: NOT READY.** Two RED/AMBER blockers are resolvable within 6 weeks. Premature acceleration without closing U-1 and U-4 risks capital burn against an unvalidated demand curve.

### Path B — Consolidation (hold headcount, focus on retention, extend runway)

| Criterion | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Current cohort retention defensible | GREEN | 78% 30-day retention, above benchmark |
| Operating runway sufficient to reach next gate | GREEN | 14 months at current burn |
| Team stability under hold posture | AMBER | Two retention risks identified |
| Customer base sufficient for learning | GREEN | Adequate for structured experimentation |

**Verdict: READY — but not optimal.** Consolidation is executable now and preserves optionality. The cost is speed: if U-3 (competitor release) resolves adversely, a delayed acceleration decision becomes more expensive.

### Recommended Posture

**Conditional hold with acceleration trigger.** Execute Path B immediately. Define explicit criteria that, if met within 60 days, auto-authorize a shift to Path A without requiring a full board session:

- U-1 resolves: pilot conversion rate ≥ X%
- U-4 resolves: Head of Growth offer accepted
- U-3 resolves: no confirmed competitor release within 90-day window

**Why conditional hold rather than committing to a path now:**

This is a comparative judgment, not a default. Three factors favor deferral over immediate commitment:

1. **Cost asymmetry.** Accelerating today without demand validation exposes approximately [N months] of incremental burn at Path A's higher run rate before the pilot would have returned results anyway. The pilot costs roughly [X] to run and returns a board-grade signal. The cost of waiting is bounded; the cost of accelerating into an unvalidated demand curve is not.

2. **Resolution feasibility.** The 6-week window for U-1 and U-4 is not assumed — it is structured. The pilot can be designed and launched within 10 business days (see Recommended Next Artifact). The two Head of Growth candidates are in final-round interviews; 3–4 weeks to close is the recruiting team's stated estimate, not a projection. If either timeline slips materially, the conditional hold posture should be revisited at that point.

3. **Why auto-triggers rather than a second board meeting.** A direct board choice in 60 days is an alternative, but it introduces scheduling latency and creates pressure to decide before results are in. Pre-authorizing the acceleration trigger now, against specific observable criteria, removes ambiguity about what constitutes sufficient evidence and reduces the risk that the board reconvenes before signal is available. If conditions are not met by the trigger deadline, the board convenes normally — the auto-trigger is a conditional fast path, not a bypass.

**Acknowledged risk of this posture:** If U-3 resolves adversely (competitor releases in <90 days), the hold posture will have cost timing. That risk is real and is the primary argument for acceleration now. The board should weigh whether competitive timing risk, currently based on unconfirmed rumor with no primary source, justifies accelerating ahead of demand validation. Management's current assessment is that it does not — but this is the crux of the decision, and reasonable board members may disagree.

## Recommended Next Artifact

**Artifact:** Pilot Design & Measurement Plan

**Why this, not something else:**
The primary blocker to acceleration (U-1 — demand at price point) is resolvable via a structured 6-week pilot. The next artifact should be a concrete pilot design with hypothesis statement, success metrics, sample size rationale, and decision rules — not another strategy document.

**Owner:** Product + Revenue leads, jointly

**Deadline:** Draft to board within 10 business days

**What this artifact should answer:**
1. What specific behavior change in customers constitutes demand confirmation?
2. What sample size is required for the signal to be board-grade?
3. What is the kill criterion — the result that confirms we should stay on Path B permanently?

**Secondary artifact (parallel):** Regulatory Memo from outside counsel on secondary market posture. This unblocks geographic optionality independent of the pilot outcome.
