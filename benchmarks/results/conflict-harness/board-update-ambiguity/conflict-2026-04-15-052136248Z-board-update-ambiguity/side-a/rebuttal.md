## Claim Under Attack
The board should avoid broad acceleration and instead authorize a time-boxed validation phase with explicit scale-or-stop gates.

## Evidence Or Reason
The artifact recommends a time-boxed validation phase but never specifies the duration of that time-box, the capital envelope authorized for it, or the minimum evidence threshold that would trigger a scale recommendation. 'Explicit exit criteria' are named as the mechanism but no criteria are defined — the Pass/Fail section lists directional descriptions (e.g., 'repeatable customer pull,' 'economics meet a pre-agreed minimum threshold') rather than measurable gates. A board cannot authorize a validation phase without knowing how long it runs, what it costs, and what specific numeric outcome constitutes a pass. The recommendation therefore preserves optionality in rhetoric while leaving the three most operationally load-bearing decisions — timeline, budget, and threshold — unresolved. This is not a decision frame; it is a deferral dressed as a decision.
