# Revised artifact

## Strategy Update

The board should not approve broad acceleration at this stage. It should approve a bounded validation phase with explicit operating limits and measurable graduation criteria so management can return with a real scale recommendation rather than another ambiguity update.

## Decision Frame
The decision is whether to fund broad acceleration now or authorize one final proof window with predefined stop/go rules.

Recommendation: authorize a 75-day validation phase, capped at no more than 15% above the current operating run-rate for this initiative, with no irreversible headcount or market-expansion commitments during the window.

Management should return at the end of the 75 days with one of three recommendations only:
1. Scale, if all gating criteria are met.
2. Continue testing in narrowed scope, if signal improves but one gate remains below threshold.
3. Stop or reposition, if two or more gates are missed.

## What Has Not Changed
- The opportunity remains credible enough to justify bounded investment.
- The downside of premature scaling is still larger than the downside of a short delay.
- The board needs decision-grade evidence, not directional optimism.

## What Must Be Proven In This Window
- Demand is repeatable, not founder-led or anecdotal.
- A single target segment shows materially better pull than the rest.
- Unit economics remain viable on a fully-loaded basis.
- Delivery quality holds under a larger but still controlled cohort.

## Gating Criteria For A Scale Recommendation
A scale recommendation is authorized only if all four conditions are met by the end of the 75-day window:

- Demand: at least 3 consecutive wins from the same target segment through a repeatable motion, with no more than 1 requiring exceptional executive intervention.
- Segment clarity: one segment represents at least 60% of qualified pipeline or active usage in the validation cohort.
- Economics: projected 12-month gross margin after implementation and support costs is positive and within 10% of the company's minimum threshold for new investments.
- Delivery readiness: implementation or service delivery for the validation cohort completes on time in at least 80% of cases, with no severe quality failure.

If exactly one gate is missed but trendlines improve materially, management may request a narrowed follow-on test. If two or more gates are missed, the default board posture should be stop, reposition, or harvest learning rather than continue funding.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- The current record does not establish what the company-wide minimum investment threshold is; the economics gate therefore must be benchmarked against the existing internal standard before launch.
- The present update does not quantify current pipeline quality, so the demand gate should be treated as a board authorization condition, not as already achieved.
- Delivery-readiness assumptions may vary by segment; if implementation burden differs materially across segments, the cohort mix must be disclosed in the next memo.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass:
- The 75-day window is accepted as the final bounded proof period.
- The spend cap is acceptable within current capital posture.
- Management agrees to report against the four numeric gates above.
- The board is willing to default to stop/reposition if two or more gates are missed.

Fail:
- Management cannot operate within a capped budget and fixed timeline.
- The company lacks an agreed internal economic threshold to judge the initiative.
- The next update would still rely on narrative signal instead of measured gates.
- The board is unwilling to enforce a stop decision if evidence remains weak.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A board decision memo at day 75 with four exhibits:
1. A scorecard against each gating criterion.
2. Actual spend versus the authorized cap.
3. Cohort-level demand, economics, and delivery performance by segment.
4. A single recommendation: scale, narrow-and-retest, or stop/reposition.
