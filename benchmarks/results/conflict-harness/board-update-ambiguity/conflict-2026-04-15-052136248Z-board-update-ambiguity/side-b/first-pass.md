# Strategy Update

## Current View
We are operating with insufficient signal to support a high-confidence scale decision. The right board-level posture is to preserve strategic optionality, continue bounded investment, and force the next 60-90 days to answer the few questions that would materially change capital allocation.

Management is not asking for approval to accelerate broadly. Management is asking to continue a controlled prove-or-stop phase with explicit decision gates.

## What Has Not Changed
- The core opportunity still appears directionally credible.
- The cost of waiting is lower than the cost of scaling the wrong motion.
- A disciplined evidence standard is more valuable than narrative certainty at this stage.

## What Is Unclear
- Whether current demand signals represent repeatable market pull or isolated early interest.
- Whether the delivery model can support expansion without disproportionate operational drag.
- Whether the economics can clear our threshold once fully-loaded costs are included.
- Which customer segment, if any, should anchor the next phase.

## Recommended Stance
Proceed with a narrow execution plan rather than a broad growth plan. Concentrate resources on a small set of falsifiable assumptions, reduce parallel bets, and defer irreversible commitments until the next board check-in.

This approach protects downside while preserving upside if evidence strengthens.

## Decision Frame
The decision is not whether the strategy is promising in theory. The decision is whether we have enough evidence now to justify acceleration.

Recommendation: do not scale yet. Authorize a time-boxed validation phase with explicit exit criteria. If those criteria are met, management should return with a scale recommendation. If they are not met, management should narrow scope, reposition, or stop.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
- Reliable demand evidence: repeated conversion, not anecdotal interest.
- Segment clarity: identifiable customer profile with better-than-average pull and retention signals.
- Unit economics: credible contribution profile after implementation and support costs.
- Operational readiness: proof that delivery quality holds under higher volume.
- Strategic alternatives: whether adjacent uses or packaging produce stronger signal than the current path.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass:
- We can show repeatable customer pull from a defined segment.
- Economics meet a pre-agreed minimum threshold on a fully-loaded basis.
- Delivery performance remains stable through a larger but still bounded cohort.
- The next scale motion is clear enough to fund with confidence.

Fail:
- Demand remains episodic or founder-led.
- Economics only work under optimistic assumptions.
- Operational burden rises faster than revenue quality.
- The team cannot name a focused segment and a credible go-to-market motion.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A board decision memo for the next meeting with three components:
1. Validation scorecard against the four gating assumptions.
2. Capital-allocation recommendation across scale, continue-testing, or stop/narrow options.
3. One-page scenario view showing base, upside, and downside implications of each option.
