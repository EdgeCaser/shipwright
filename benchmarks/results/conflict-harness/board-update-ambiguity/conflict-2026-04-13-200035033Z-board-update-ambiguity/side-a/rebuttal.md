# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Under ambiguity and with no company-specific evidence supplied, the board should authorize bounded strategy validation rather than a full scale commitment.

## Evidence Or Reason
The claim that 'bounded validation' is the correct board posture is presented as self-evident but is itself an unsupported strategic recommendation. The artifact acknowledges zero company-specific evidence yet still prescribes a specific course of action (continue pursuing the direction via validation) over equally valid alternatives such as pausing entirely, divesting, or accelerating based on time-sensitive competitive dynamics. No evidence or framework is offered for why validation is superior to these alternatives under the stated ambiguity. A truly evidence-disciplined artifact would acknowledge that without knowing the company's cash position, competitive clock speed, or opportunity cost of attention, even the recommendation to 'validate' carries implicit assumptions about resource availability and strategic patience that are not defended. The artifact treats 'proceed cautiously' as a neutral default when it is in fact a directional bet that consumes board attention, management bandwidth, and capital—all of which have opportunity costs that remain completely unaddressed.
