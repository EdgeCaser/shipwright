# Strategy Update: Board Briefing Under Ambiguity

## Decision Frame

**Core question:** Should the board authorize continued investment in the current strategic direction given incomplete market signals, or pivot resource allocation toward emerging alternatives?

**Why this decision now:** Three converging pressures force a decision within this quarter:
1. Current runway supports the existing strategy for approximately two more quarters at present burn rate.
2. Two key market indicators have diverged from our planning assumptions (customer acquisition cost trending 18-25% above model; retention in cohort 3+ showing early softening).
3. A credible adjacent opportunity has surfaced that would require reallocation of 30-40% of current engineering capacity to pursue.

**What this update is NOT:** This is not a recommendation to abandon the current strategy. It is a request to establish decision criteria now so that the board can act decisively when ambiguity resolves — rather than reacting under pressure.

| Competitive response | Two funded competitors entered the space in the last two quarters | Their go-to-market velocity, pricing strategy, and whether they are buying market share at a loss | If they are buying share: our CAC increase is partially explained and may normalize when their funding pressure hits. **Key epistemic risk:** Multiple unknowns are entangled. CAC increases could be driven by competition, market saturation, or our own product issues — and the correct strategic response differs for each root cause. We should resist the temptation to pick a single narrative prematurely.

Readiness criteria and current status:

| Retention signal disambiguated (PMF vs. UX) | ❌ Fail | Churn interviews in progress, 3 weeks out |
| Adjacent opportunity validated with at least 1 paying design partner | ❌ Fail | Discovery not yet started |
| Competitive threat level assessed with data (not narrative) | ⚠️ Partial | Win/loss data available but sample size insufficient (n=12) |
| Financial model updated with scenario ranges | ✅ Pass | Three-scenario model (base/bull/bear) completed, shared in board packet appendix |

**What IS ready:** The financial model and scenario analysis. The board can review the range of outcomes and the trigger points for each strategic path. What is missing is the diagnostic evidence to determine which scenario we are actually in.

**Risk of premature commitment:** Committing to a pivot now without diagnostic evidence risks abandoning a viable strategy based on ambiguous signals. Committing to stay the course risks burning runway on a strategy whose assumptions may have broken. The asymmetry favors a bounded diagnostic period over an immediate directional bet.

## Recommended Next Artifact

**Deliverable:** A time-boxed Decision Package due to the board in 8 weeks, containing:

1. **Diagnostic results** from the three evidence-gathering workstreams (channel mix A/B, churn interviews, competitive intelligence).
2. **Two discovery sprint reports** from the adjacent opportunity — including willingness-to-pay signals and technical feasibility assessment.
3. **Updated scenario model** incorporating diagnostic findings, with a clear recommendation for one of three paths:
   - **Stay:** Double down on current strategy with tactical fixes (if CAC is cyclical and retention is UX-driven).
   - **Expand:** Pursue adjacent opportunity as a second revenue line (if discovery validates repeatable demand).
   - **Retrench:** Reduce burn rate and narrow focus to highest-performing segment (if CAC is structural and adjacent opportunity is unvalidated).

**Basis for the 8-week window and $85K budget:**
The 8-week timeline is set by the longest-lead evidence dependency: the competitive pricing industry report (6-8 weeks), which cannot be accelerated. The other workstreams resolve sooner — churn interviews in 3 weeks, channel mix A/B in 4 weeks — and discovery sprints are scoped to fit within the same window. The $85K budget is decomposed as follows: $25K for third-party research subscriptions and churn interview incentives, $40K for two 4-week discovery sprints (1 PM + 1 engineer per sprint at loaded cost), and $20K for competitive intelligence tooling and win/loss interview facilitation. These are estimates, not precision figures — the board should treat them as a ceiling for planning purposes.

**Acknowledged risk to timeline:** This window assumes all three workstreams execute concurrently without material delays. The competitive intelligence workstream in particular depends on a third-party report whose delivery date is estimated, not confirmed. If that report slips, the board should expect a partial Decision Package at week 8 with a competitive assessment gap flagged, rather than an extension of the full diagnostic period.

**Escalation trigger:** If any workstream produces a strongly directional signal before the 8-week window closes (e.g., churn interviews at week 3 reveal unambiguous PMF erosion), management will escalate to the designated board sounding-board member immediately rather than waiting for the full package.

**Board ask for today:**
- Approve the 8-week diagnostic period and associated budget (estimated ceiling: $85K incremental).
- Agree on the decision criteria above so that the next board conversation is a decision meeting, not another ambiguity discussion.
- Designate a board member to serve as sounding board during the diagnostic period for any time-sensitive signals that emerge before the 8-week window closes.
