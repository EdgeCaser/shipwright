# Strategy Update

## Decision Frame
The prudent near-term decision is to avoid presenting ambiguous signals as settled direction and instead give the board a decision-oriented update centered on what is known, what is not yet known, and which choices will require board attention next. Under the current packet, the most defensible strategy is disciplined ambiguity management rather than a directional commitment.

## Current Assessment
The requesting team has been asked to provide a strategy update, but the packet contains no underlying operating evidence, market evidence, financial evidence, customer evidence, or execution data. That means the board update should optimize for decision usefulness through clarity of assumptions, explicit uncertainty, and a concrete plan for resolving the highest-value unknowns.

A strong board-facing posture in this situation is:
- state the decision areas that matter
- separate confirmed facts from unresolved assumptions
- identify what management can decide now versus what should wait
- define the next artifact needed to convert ambiguity into a board-actionable recommendation

## Recommended Board Message
Management is operating in an information-constrained environment and should not overstate confidence. At this stage, the objective is to preserve strategic flexibility, narrow the highest-impact uncertainties, and return with a decision-ready recommendation once the evidence gap is materially reduced.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The packet does not establish:
- the strategic decision currently in front of the board
- the business context or operating baseline
- the relevant risks, alternatives, or tradeoffs
- the timeline for decision or consequences of delay
- the evidence required to distinguish between viable paths

Because those elements are absent, any more specific strategy claim would exceed the available record.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass:
- the board update clearly distinguishes knowns, unknowns, and pending decisions
- management avoids unsupported specificity
- the next decision gate and evidence collection plan are explicit
- the board can see what will be brought back for approval or guidance

Fail:
- the update implies confidence not supported by the packet
- unresolved assumptions are framed as conclusions
- no clear path is offered for converting uncertainty into a decision
- the board is asked to react without a defined choice architecture

## Recommended Next Artifact
Produce a decision-ready briefing that defines the specific strategic question, the main alternatives under consideration, the evidence available for each, the key uncertainties, and the recommended trigger for board action. That artifact should be the basis for the next board discussion because the current record is insufficient for a sharper recommendation.
