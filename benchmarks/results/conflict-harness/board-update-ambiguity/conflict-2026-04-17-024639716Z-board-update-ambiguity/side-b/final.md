# Revised artifact

## Decision Frame
This board update should avoid making a directional strategy recommendation because the packet provides no underlying business evidence to support one. The useful decision for this update is procedural: clarify what is known, identify the missing evidence that blocks a recommendation, and define the next decision the board should expect.

## Current Assessment
The case packet specifies that this is a strategy update for a board audience and requires a structured artifact, but it includes no substantive operating, market, financial, customer, or execution evidence. Under that constraint, the update should not imply that management has earned confidence in any specific path.

## Board Update
Management does not yet have an evidence base that supports a recommendation among strategic alternatives. The appropriate update is therefore to make the uncertainty legible, explain which questions remain unresolved, and state what additional work is needed before a board-actionable recommendation can be made.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The packet does not establish:
- the specific decision the board may need to make
- the alternatives under consideration
- the factual basis for preferring one path over another
- the timing, risks, or consequences of delay
- the evidence required to convert the update into a recommendation

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass:
- the update distinguishes absent evidence from actual conclusions
- the board is told which questions remain unresolved
- the next decision point is identified clearly
- management does not imply support for a path that the packet does not justify

Fail:
- the update presents a directional strategy without supporting evidence
- uncertainty is turned into confidence by rhetoric alone
- the board is asked to infer the decision from an incomplete record
- no next artifact or decision path is defined

## Recommended Next Artifact
Produce a decision-ready board briefing that names the strategic question, lays out the alternatives under consideration, identifies the evidence available for each, and states what remaining gaps must be closed before asking the board to weigh in.
