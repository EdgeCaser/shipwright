# Strategy Update: Operating Under Ambiguity

**Prepared for:** Board of Directors  
**Status:** Final revision — decision frame updated to reflect critique on bounded-uncertainty claim

---

## Decision Frame

The board is being asked to evaluate whether conditions are sufficient to advance to the next strategic phase, or whether the current ambiguity warrants a structured hold pending additional evidence.

This document originally framed the board's decision as a narrower resource authorization question, distinct from a binary judgment on strategic direction. That framing requires qualification: it holds only if the regulatory pathway unknown resolves as viable or workable. If it resolves as blocked with no mitigation, the board decision escalates from interim resource authorization to strategic viability — the two questions cannot be treated as separate under that outcome.

The correct framing is therefore **conditional**:

- **If regulatory pathway is viable or in-progress:** The decision is a resource authorization question — approve continued burn rate during the evidence window or require a defined milestone before next spend.
- **If regulatory pathway is blocked:** The decision becomes a strategic direction question — this artifact does not provide sufficient basis for that judgment, and the board should require an out-of-cycle briefing before authorizing further commitment.

**Decision type:** Conditional proceed vs. structured hold, with escalation trigger defined  
**Decision owner:** Board, with management executing  
**Time horizon:** This decision gates the next 60–90 day operating window, subject to the regulatory escalation trigger

**What this document does not claim:** It does not assert that all unknowns are bounded. The regulatory pathway unknown is explicitly not bounded — resolution timeline is uncertain and a negative resolution would invalidate the narrow resource-authorization framing entirely.

**What remains stable:** Strategic rationale, target customer segment, and resource model are stable. Execution-condition unknowns (capacity, pilot signal, market sizing) are bounded and resolvable within the evidence window. The regulatory unknown sits in a different category and is treated as such in the pass/fail criteria below.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

The following criteria define whether the strategy is ready to advance at the next review gate. Criteria are proposed by the requesting team and subject to board modification.

**Pass conditions (all must be met):**

1. Regulatory pathway confirmed as viable or workable with defined mitigation — no ambiguity on this point is acceptable at next gate
2. Operational capacity assessment complete with a staffing plan attached
3. Pilot cohort produces at least directional signal on conversion behavior (not a target hit, but signal present)

**Fail conditions (any one triggers structured hold or escalation):**

1. Regulatory pathway confirmed blocked with no viable workaround — triggers strategic direction review, not merely a hold
2. Operational assessment reveals capacity gap that cannot be closed within the current funding envelope
3. Pilot cohort produces contradictory or null signal that undermines core demand assumption

**Escalation trigger:** If the regulatory pathway resolves negatively before the 45-day evidence window closes, management is directed to request an out-of-cycle board briefing rather than hold until the scheduled touchpoint. This trigger should be ratified explicitly by the board at this meeting.

**Current status:** 0 of 3 pass conditions confirmed. 0 of 3 fail conditions triggered. The strategy is in an indeterminate state. The board is being asked to authorize the work that produces the evidence, and to ratify the escalation trigger, not to approve a strategy already supported by evidence.

## Recommended Next Artifact

The next artifact this board should receive is a **Pass/Fail Evidence Package** — a structured summary produced at the 45-day mark that maps observed evidence against the pass/fail criteria above.

That artifact should:
- Make a binary readiness call (advance or hold) against each criterion

This update is a frame-setting document, not a basis for a final resource decision. The board's action at this stage is to ratify or modify the pass/fail criteria, ratify the regulatory escalation trigger, confirm the 45-day timeline is acceptable, and authorize continued work at the current run rate pending that evidence package.

**Proposed board action:** Approve criteria as written, ratify the regulatory escalation trigger, authorize 45-day evidence window, schedule pass/fail evidence package review at next scheduled meeting.
