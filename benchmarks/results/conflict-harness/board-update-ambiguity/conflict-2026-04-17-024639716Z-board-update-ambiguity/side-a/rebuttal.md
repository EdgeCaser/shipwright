## Claim Under Attack
The most defensible board update under this packet is an ambiguity-managed strategy update that explicitly separates knowns, unknowns, and next decisions rather than asserting a directional strategy.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B treats absence of evidence in the packet as justification for producing a meta-strategy about ambiguity management, but this conflates the scenario's evidence gap with a prescriptive structural recommendation. The claim that 'ambiguity management' is the 'most defensible' posture is itself an unsupported directional assertion — it assumes the board's primary need is epistemic humility rather than, for example, a constrained set of plausible options with risk-weighted paths. No evidence is cited that boards in information-constrained environments respond better to ambiguity-framing than to scenario-based alternatives. The claim imports a consulting-style doctrine without validating it against the actual scenario context, making it a self-referential assertion dressed as a defensible strategy choice.
