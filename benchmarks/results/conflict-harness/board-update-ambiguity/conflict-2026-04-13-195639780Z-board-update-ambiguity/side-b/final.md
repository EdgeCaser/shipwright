# Revised artifact

## Decision Frame

The board decision should be framed as a staged capital-allocation decision under incomplete information, not as a binary approval or cancellation. The available case packet establishes the need for a strategy update under ambiguity, but it does not provide initiative-specific market, financial, customer, competitive, or execution facts. That means the update should separate three categories clearly: what management already knows, what remains uncertain, and what decision the board is being asked to make now.

Recommended board posture: authorize the next stage only if management can present enough current evidence to justify continued investment, while requiring a bounded decision gate for unresolved assumptions before any scaled commitment. This is not a claim that the organization has no evidence; it is a governance recommendation for handling ambiguity without overstating confidence.

The board should ask management to anchor the discussion around the highest-leverage unknowns: customer urgency, economic viability, competitive right to win, execution burden, and fit against other uses of capital. If existing internal data already supports any of these areas, it should be surfaced explicitly. If not, those gaps should become conditions for the next decision gate.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

Known from the case packet:

- The audience is the board.
- The artifact is a strategy update.
- The strategic context is ambiguous.
- No initiative-specific evidence is included in the packet provided for this exercise.

Evidence that should be surfaced before a scaled commitment:

- Customer demand: which segment has the clearest urgency, what behavior proves that urgency, and whether buyers have budget and authority.
- Current traction or internal evidence: prior pilots, sales conversations, usage data, customer commitments, churn signals, support data, or strategic customer requests that management already has.
- Competitive position: the alternatives customers use today, why those alternatives are insufficient, and whether the company has a durable reason to win.
- Business model: pricing evidence, expected gross margin, acquisition path, payback period, retention assumptions, and downside cases.
- Execution feasibility: delivery complexity, staffing needs, dependency risk, operational burden, and opportunity cost against existing priorities.
- Strategic fit: why this initiative strengthens the company’s core position rather than distracting from a higher-confidence path.

The board should not treat missing evidence in the packet as proof that evidence does not exist. It should treat the absence of presented evidence as a disclosure gap that management must close in the board narrative.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

Ready to continue if:

- Management identifies the specific strategic decision required now and the investment exposure attached to it.
- Existing internal evidence is summarized with source, recency, and relevance.
- At least one target segment has credible proof of urgency or a clear plan to validate it quickly.
- The economic case includes explicit assumptions for pricing, margin, acquisition cost, retention, and payback.
- The competitive case names the real alternatives and explains the company’s right to win.
- Execution risk is bounded with owners, milestones, and a clear view of what work would be displaced.

Not ready for scaled investment if:

- The board is asked to approve major capital before the core customer, economic, and execution assumptions are evidenced.
- The update relies on broad market logic without segment-level proof.
- Differentiation is asserted but not tested against specific competitors or substitutes.
- The plan does not show what existing evidence is known versus what remains assumed.
- The opportunity cannot be compared against alternative uses of capital.

Current readiness: ready for a structured board discussion and possibly a bounded next stage, but not ready for scaled commitment unless management can supply initiative-specific evidence that is absent from the case packet.

## Recommended Next Artifact

Prepare a board validation memo that converts ambiguity into a decision-ready evidence map. It should include:

- The decision requested now, the capital at risk, and the decision that will come next.
- A concise inventory of existing evidence: source, date, owner, and implication.
- The top assumptions that still determine success or failure.
- A validation plan for unresolved assumptions, including method, owner, deadline, and pass/fail threshold.
- A comparison against alternative uses of capital.
- A stop/continue/scale rubric tied to observed evidence rather than narrative confidence.

The next artifact should help the board distinguish between uncertainty that is acceptable for this stage and uncertainty that must be resolved before larger investment.
