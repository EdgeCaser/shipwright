# Strategy Update Under Ambiguity

## Decision Frame

The board decision should not be framed as a full-scale approval or cancellation. Given the absence of validated market, financial, and execution evidence in the current packet, the defensible recommendation is a gated continuation: approve a limited next phase designed to reduce decision risk before committing major capital.

Recommended board ask: authorize a time-boxed 60-90 day validation phase with explicit evidence gates, a capped budget, and a clear decision point at the end of the phase.

The strategy is to preserve option value while preventing premature scale-up. This keeps momentum where learning is still valuable, but makes the next investment conditional on proof rather than narrative confidence.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

Key unknowns:

- Customer demand: no evidence yet on urgency, willingness to pay, buying process, or target segment priority.
- Competitive position: no evidence yet on alternatives, differentiation, switching costs, or defensibility.
- Business model: no evidence yet on expected pricing, gross margin, sales efficiency, retention, or payback period.
- Execution feasibility: no evidence yet on delivery complexity, operational burden, technical risk, or dependency risk.
- Strategic fit: no evidence yet on whether this opportunity strengthens the company’s core position or distracts from higher-confidence priorities.

The board should treat the current state as pre-commitment ambiguity, not as a forecastable plan. Any revenue, margin, or adoption targets presented now should be labeled as assumptions until supported by primary evidence.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

Pass criteria for moving from validation to scaled investment:

- At least one clearly defined customer segment shows repeated, high-urgency demand.
- Buyers can articulate the current pain, existing workaround, and reason to switch.
- Early pricing evidence supports a viable gross margin and acquisition motion.
- Competitive review shows a credible reason to win beyond feature parity.
- Delivery plan identifies the top execution risks and shows they are manageable within current team capacity.
- The opportunity ranks above alternative uses of capital on strategic importance and expected return.

Fail or pause criteria:

- Demand is broad but shallow, with no segment showing urgent need.
- Willingness to pay does not support the required economics.
- Differentiation depends on claims that competitors can quickly copy.
- Execution cost grows faster than the expected customer or revenue impact.
- The initiative requires board-level capital before the team can validate basic adoption and economics.

Current readiness: not ready for scaled investment; ready for bounded validation if the board accepts the learning objective and capped exposure.

## Recommended Next Artifact

Prepare a board validation memo before the next decision meeting. It should include:

- The target customer segment and why it is the first segment to test.
- The top five assumptions that must be true for the strategy to work.
- The evidence plan for each assumption, including source, owner, and deadline.
- A capped budget and staffing request for the validation phase.
- A stop/continue/scale decision rubric tied to observed evidence.

The next artifact should make the board decision easier by separating what is known, what is assumed, and what must be proven before larger investment.
