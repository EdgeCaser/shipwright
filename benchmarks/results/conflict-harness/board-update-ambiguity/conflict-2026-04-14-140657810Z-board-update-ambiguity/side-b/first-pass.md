# Strategy Update: Board Briefing Under Ambiguity

## Decision Frame

**Core question:** Should the board authorize continued investment in the current strategic direction given incomplete market signals, or trigger a formal strategy review?

**Context:** We are operating in an environment where key leading indicators are ambiguous — some metrics trend positively while others contradict. The board must decide how to act without waiting for clarity that may not arrive on a useful timeline.

**Stakes:** Premature pivot risks abandoning momentum in a strategy that may be working. Delayed pivot risks compounding losses if the strategy is failing. The asymmetry of these risks is itself uncertain.

**Decision type:** This is a *continue/pause/redirect* decision, not a *go/no-go* on a new initiative. The default path (continue as-is) carries its own risks and should not be treated as the "safe" option.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

| Unknown | What we know | What we don't know | Impact on decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| Market demand trajectory | Current quarter pipeline is stable | Whether pipeline stability reflects genuine demand or lagging contracts from prior momentum | High — determines if current trajectory is sustainable |
| Competitive response timing | Two competitors have announced adjacent products | Whether announcements reflect shipped capability or vaporware positioning | Medium — affects urgency of response |
| Unit economics at scale | Margins hold at current volume | Whether margins compress or expand at 2-3x volume due to unknown cost curves | High — determines viability of growth investment |
| Customer retention drivers | NRR is above target | Whether retention is driven by product value or switching costs that competitors could erode | Medium — affects long-term defensibility |
| Regulatory environment | No current action against us | Whether pending regulatory proposals would materially change our operating model | Low-to-medium — timeline is 12-18 months but impact could be severe |

**Evidence we need but do not have:**
- Customer cohort analysis beyond 12 months (we have 8 months of data)
- Competitive product teardown on announced features
- Stress-tested unit economics model at 3x current volume

**Evidence we will not get before this decision:**
- Regulatory outcome (18-month horizon)
- Full competitive response (depends on their execution)

## Pass/Fail Readiness

**Are we ready to make a high-conviction strategic commitment today?** No.

**Assessment against readiness criteria:**

| Criterion | Status | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Sufficient evidence to commit | ⚠️ Partial | Pipeline data is encouraging but tenure is too short to distinguish signal from noise |
| Downside bounded | ⚠️ Partial | Current burn rate is sustainable for 14 months; a failed bet does not threaten survival but does close adjacent options |
| Reversibility if wrong | ✅ Adequate | Core investments (team, infrastructure) retain value across plausible pivots |
| Team alignment | ⚠️ Partial | Leadership team is aligned on direction but not on pace of investment — this gap will widen without a decision |
| Board information parity | ❌ Insufficient | Board members have uneven access to context; this briefing is a corrective step |

**Bottom line:** We are *not* at a point where inaction is neutral. The default path consumes resources and narrows future options. But the evidence does not support a high-conviction acceleration either. The appropriate posture is **structured optionality** — bounded commitments that preserve the ability to accelerate or redirect within 90 days.

**Specific recommendation for this meeting:**
1. Authorize continuation of current strategy at current investment level (no acceleration)
2. Fund three specific evidence-gathering workstreams (customer cohort study, competitive teardown, unit economics stress test) with 60-day delivery targets
3. Schedule a follow-up board session in 75 days to make the acceleration/redirect decision with better evidence
4. Define now the specific metrics that would trigger an emergency session before the 75-day window

## Recommended Next Artifact

**Artifact:** Evidence-gathering workplan with decision triggers

**Purpose:** Convert today's ambiguity into a structured 60-day learning sprint with pre-committed decision criteria, so the next board conversation is a decision meeting rather than another discussion meeting.

**Contents should include:**
- Three workstream charters (owner, budget, timeline, deliverable format)
- Quantitative thresholds for accelerate/continue/redirect (agreed by board before the data arrives, to prevent post-hoc rationalization)
- Emergency trigger criteria and escalation protocol
- Board pre-read timeline so members arrive at the follow-up session with information parity

**Why this artifact and not others:** A revised strategy deck would be premature — we lack the evidence to write one credibly. A financial model update would give false precision. The evidence-gathering workplan is the honest next step: it acknowledges what we don't know while committing to a timeline for knowing it.
