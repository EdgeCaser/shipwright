# Revised artifact

## Strategy Update
The board should authorize a staged commitment plan, but only with explicit interim thresholds because the current packet contains no operating evidence and the decision is therefore a governance problem under ambiguity rather than a scale decision under confidence.

## Decision Frame
Approve a two-step posture for the next review window:
1. Protect liquidity and core execution capacity.
2. Release incremental growth investment only if management clears predefined gates.

Use the following provisional gates for the next board cycle:
- Demand gate: at least two consecutive measurement periods showing either pipeline conversion or retained revenue within 10-15% of plan, with no single segment contributing more than 50% of the observed improvement.
- Economics gate: contribution margin or payback trend improves by at least 15% from the current baseline, or management shows a credible path to that level without assuming price, mix, or efficiency gains that have not yet appeared in live data.
- Execution gate: at least 80% of committed milestone deliverables ship on time in the limited-scope phase, with no critical dependency remaining ownerless.

If fewer than two of the three gates are met, do not authorize broad expansion. If all three are met, the board may approve a measured scale-up rather than a full acceleration.

## Board-Level Interpretation
The implication is not "wait for certainty." It is "buy information cheaply before buying scale expensively." A staged strategy is justified only because the case presents unresolved ambiguity and no supporting evidence pack; without quantified gates, the recommendation would be too subjective for board use.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The missing evidence remains material:
- Current baseline demand durability by segment.
- Unit economics baseline and variance range.
- Delivery capacity against the proposed roadmap.
- Cash exposure if learning takes one review cycle longer than expected.

Because those inputs are absent, the thresholds above should be treated as interim board controls, not claims that the business is already performing at those levels.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if management returns in the next review window with a baseline dashboard and clears all three gates, or clears two gates plus a credible remediation plan on the third with limited downside exposure.

Fail if management cannot establish the baseline, misses more than one gate, or requests additional scale investment before the board can observe real movement in demand, economics, and execution reliability.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A board decision memo with current-baseline metrics, threshold owners, review cadence, downside case cash exposure, and the exact trigger for moving from limited-scope investment to measured scale-up.
