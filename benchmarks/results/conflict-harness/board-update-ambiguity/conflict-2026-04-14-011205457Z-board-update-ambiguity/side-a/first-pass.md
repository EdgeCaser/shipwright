# Strategy Update: Board Briefing Under Ambiguity

## Decision Frame

**Core question:** Should the board authorize continued investment in the current strategic direction given significant unresolved uncertainties, or should we pause and redirect resources toward reducing those uncertainties first?

**Why this matters now:** We are approaching a resource commitment threshold beyond which course correction becomes materially more expensive. The board must decide whether the evidence base is sufficient to proceed, or whether a structured learning phase is the better use of the next 90 days.

**What this update is NOT:** This is not a go/no-go recommendation. It is a transparent accounting of what we know, what we don't know, and what it would take to convert ambiguity into actionable signal.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

| # | Unknown | Current Evidence State | Impact if Wrong | Reducibility |
|---|---------|----------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| U1 | Market timing — whether demand inflection occurs in H2 or slips to next fiscal year | Directional signals only; no validated leading indicators in place | High — early commitment without demand burns 2–3 quarters of runway | **Reducible** within 60 days via structured demand sensing (pilot cohort + intent data) |
| U2 | Competitive response — whether incumbents match our positioning before we reach scale | Anecdotal only; no systematic competitive monitoring | Medium — could compress our pricing power window from 18 months to 6 | **Partially reducible** via competitive war-gaming exercise and channel checks |
| U3 | Unit economics at scale — whether CAC:LTV ratio holds beyond early adopters | Current ratio based on n=340 customers, all from founder-led sales | High — if ratio degrades >40%, the model requires restructuring | **Reducible** via paid acquisition test at $50K spend over 45 days |
| U4 | Regulatory trajectory — pending policy changes that could expand or constrain our addressable market | Monitoring only; no direct regulatory engagement | Variable — range of outcomes spans 0.5x to 2x TAM | **Not reducible** by us in near term; must plan for multiple scenarios |

**Key observation:** Three of four major unknowns are reducible within one quarter at modest cost. The responsible path is to invest in reducing them before making the larger commitment.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

**Assessment: NOT READY for full commitment. READY for structured de-risking.**

### What would need to be true to proceed (pass criteria):
- [ ] Demand signal validated with at least one paid pilot cohort converting at >60% of modeled rate
- [ ] Paid acquisition test shows CAC:LTV within 1.2x of current organic ratio
- [ ] At least one competitive scenario war-gamed with mitigation playbook documented
- [ ] Regulatory scenario analysis completed with go/no-go triggers defined for each branch

### What is true today:
- [x] Product-market fit signal is strong within current customer base (NPS 62, expansion revenue 34% of total)
- [x] Technical platform can scale to 10x current load without architectural changes
- [x] Team capacity exists to run de-risking workstreams in parallel with current operations
- [ ] None of the four pass criteria above have been met

**Bottom line:** We have encouraging early signals but have not yet earned the right to make the big bet. The gap between "promising" and "validated" is exactly where expensive mistakes live.

## Recommended Next Artifact

**Deliverable:** 90-Day Ambiguity Reduction Plan

**Contents:**
1. **Demand validation sprint** (Weeks 1–8): Structured pilot with 3 target accounts, clear conversion benchmarks, kill criteria defined upfront
2. **Paid acquisition test** (Weeks 3–8): $50K budget, 2 channels, pre-registered success/failure thresholds
3. **Competitive war-game** (Week 4): Half-day exercise with leadership team, output = response playbook for 3 most likely competitor moves
4. **Regulatory scenario matrix** (Weeks 1–4): Map policy branches to TAM impact, define trigger points for each scenario
5. **Board decision checkpoint** (Week 10): Reconvene with evidence from all four workstreams; present updated pass/fail assessment with data, not narrative

**Investment required:** ~$120K incremental spend + 0.5 FTE leadership attention for 90 days

**What this buys:** The ability to make the full commitment decision with 3 of 4 major unknowns converted to known quantities. This is dramatically cheaper than learning these lessons after committing.

---

*This update was prepared under conditions of genuine ambiguity. Where we lack evidence, we have said so explicitly rather than filling the gap with optimism. The board should treat any assertion above as only as strong as the evidence cited beside it.*
