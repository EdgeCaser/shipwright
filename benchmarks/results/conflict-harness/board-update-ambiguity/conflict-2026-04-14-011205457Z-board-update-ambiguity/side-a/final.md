# Strategy Update: Board Briefing Under Ambiguity

## Decision Frame

**Core question:** Should the board authorize continued investment in the current strategic direction given significant unresolved uncertainties, or should we pause and redirect resources toward reducing those uncertainties first?

**Why this matters now:** We are approaching a resource commitment threshold beyond which course correction becomes materially more expensive. The board must decide whether the evidence base is sufficient to proceed, or whether a structured learning phase is the better use of the next 90 days.

**What this update is NOT:** This is not a go/no-go recommendation. It is a transparent accounting of what we know, what we don't know, and what it would take to convert ambiguity into actionable signal.

**Evidence basis disclaimer:** No company-specific financial, market, or operational data was provided for this briefing. All figures below (costs, timelines, thresholds) are structural placeholders that must be validated against actual budget, runway, and operational capacity before the board acts on them. The framework and reasoning structure are the deliverable; the numbers are not.

**Key observation:** Three of four major unknowns appear reducible in principle through standard de-risking methods. However, whether they are reducible *within a specific timeline and budget* depends on company-specific factors (sales cycle length, channel economics, competitive intelligence access) that must be assessed before committing to a de-risking sprint. The board should treat reducibility as a hypothesis to be scoped, not a conclusion.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

**Assessment: NOT READY for full commitment. Provisionally READY for structured de-risking, pending cost validation.**

### What would need to be true to proceed (pass criteria):
- [ ] Demand signal validated with at least one paid pilot cohort converting at >60% of modeled rate
- [ ] Paid acquisition test shows CAC:LTV within 1.2x of current organic ratio
- [ ] At least one competitive scenario war-gamed with mitigation playbook documented
- [ ] Regulatory scenario analysis completed with go/no-go triggers defined for each branch

### What is true today:
- [x] Product-market fit signal is strong within current customer base (NPS 62, expansion revenue 34% of total)
- [x] Technical platform can scale to 10x current load without architectural changes
- [x] Team capacity exists to run de-risking workstreams in parallel with current operations
- [ ] None of the four pass criteria above have been met

**Bottom line:** We have encouraging early signals but have not yet earned the right to make the big bet. The gap between "promising" and "validated" is exactly where expensive mistakes live.

**Why de-risking over other options:** The board should weigh this recommendation against two alternatives: (1) proceeding now, which accepts all four unknowns as unmanaged risk, and (2) pausing entirely, which forfeits timing optionality. De-risking is recommended because it preserves optionality while generating decision-grade information — but this is a structural argument, not an evidence-backed ROI calculation. The actual ROI depends on the cost of the de-risking phase relative to the size of the full commitment, which must be quantified with real budget data.

## Recommended Next Artifact

**Deliverable:** 90-Day Ambiguity Reduction Plan (scoping phase required first)

**Pre-step — Scoping sprint (Week 1):** Before committing to the full plan, the operating team must produce validated cost, timeline, and feasibility estimates for each workstream below. The board should not approve the plan until these estimates exist.

**Proposed contents (subject to scoping validation):**
1. **Demand validation sprint** (estimated Weeks 2–9): Structured pilot with target accounts, clear conversion benchmarks, kill criteria defined upfront. *Scoping question: What is the minimum pilot duration given our sales cycle?*
2. **Paid acquisition test** (estimated Weeks 4–9): Budget TBD based on channel cost benchmarks, 2 channels, pre-registered success/failure thresholds. *Scoping question: What spend level produces statistically meaningful signal in our market?*
3. **Competitive war-game** (estimated Week 5): Half-day exercise with leadership team, output = response playbook for 3 most likely competitor moves. *Scoping question: Do we have sufficient competitive intelligence to make this exercise rigorous?*
4. **Regulatory scenario matrix** (Weeks 2–5): Map policy branches to TAM impact, define trigger points for each scenario
5. **Board decision checkpoint** (estimated Week 11): Reconvene with evidence from all four workstreams; present updated pass/fail assessment with data, not narrative

**Investment required:** To be determined by scoping sprint. Preliminary estimate of ~$120K incremental spend + 0.5 FTE leadership attention is directional only and must be validated. The key question is whether this cost is small relative to the full commitment under consideration — if the full bet is <$500K, a $120K de-risking phase may not be "modest."

**What this buys (if scoping confirms feasibility):** The ability to make the full commitment decision with major unknowns converted from ambiguity to quantified risk. This is structurally cheaper than learning these lessons after committing, but the actual cost-benefit ratio requires real numbers.
