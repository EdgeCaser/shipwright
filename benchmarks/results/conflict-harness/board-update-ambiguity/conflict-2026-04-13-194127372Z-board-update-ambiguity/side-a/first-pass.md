# Strategy Update: Board Briefing Under Ambiguity

## Decision Frame

**Core question:** Given significant uncertainty in our operating environment, what strategic posture should the board endorse for the next 90 days?

**Context:** This update is prepared under conditions where key inputs — market trajectory, competitive response, and regulatory timeline — remain unresolved. Rather than present false precision, this document names what we know, what we don't, and what decisions are time-sensitive versus deferrable.

**Framing principle:** Under ambiguity, the goal is not to pick the "right" strategy but to preserve optionality on irreversible choices while committing decisively where delay itself is costly.

**Key tensions:**
- Committing resources to growth initiatives before demand signals clarify risks capital inefficiency.
- Deferring investment risks ceding positioning to competitors who move earlier.
- Board expectations for a clear direction must be balanced against honest acknowledgment that some inputs are not yet available.

**Decision posture recommended:** Staged commitment — authorize Phase 1 spend on the two highest-conviction bets, gate Phase 2 on explicit evidence milestones, and define a decision checkpoint at 60 days.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

| Unknown | Impact if Resolved Favorably | Impact if Resolved Unfavorably | Expected Resolution Timeline | Current Evidence Quality |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Customer demand elasticity for new pricing tier | Unlocks 15-20% revenue expansion | Forces margin compression or feature unbundling | 30-45 days (pilot data) | Low — survey-based only, no transactional signal |
| Competitor product launch timing | Defines urgency of our GTM acceleration | May require defensive repositioning | 60-90 days (public signals) | Moderate — analyst reports and job postings suggest Q3 |
| Regulatory clarity on data residency requirements | Enables EU expansion roadmap | Requires architecture rework before expansion | 90-120 days (draft regulation expected) | Low — lobbying intel only |
| Key hire pipeline (VP Engineering) | Unblocks technical roadmap execution | Forces reprioritization or interim leadership | 30 days (final-round candidates) | Moderate — two finalists in process |

**What we are NOT uncertain about:**
- Core product retention remains strong (NRR 115%, stable 3 quarters).
- Cash position supports 18+ months of current burn without raising.
- Team capacity is the binding constraint, not capital.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

**Are we ready to commit to a full strategic plan today?** No.

**Rationale:** Two of the four critical unknowns (demand elasticity, regulatory clarity) have evidence quality too low to support irreversible resource allocation. Committing now would be a confidence performance, not a confidence-warranted decision.

**What we ARE ready to do:**
- **PASS — Authorize Phase 1 pilots:** Pricing tier pilot (n=200 customers, 30-day window) and competitive positioning refresh. Cost: <$150K. Reversible.
- **PASS — Set decision gate:** Board reconvenes at Day 60 with pilot data, competitive intel update, and regulatory read. At that point, either commit to Phase 2 (full GTM push + EU prep) or pivot to a defensive consolidation posture.
- **FAIL — Full EU expansion commitment:** Premature without regulatory clarity. Architectural investment is $2M+ and partially irreversible.
- **FAIL — Aggressive hiring beyond approved headcount:** Until VP Engineering hire closes, adding IC headcount increases management debt without strategic leverage.

**Risk of this posture:** If a competitor moves aggressively in the next 60 days, our staged approach may feel slow. Mitigation: the competitive positioning refresh in Phase 1 includes a rapid-response playbook that can accelerate GTM by 2-3 weeks if triggered.
