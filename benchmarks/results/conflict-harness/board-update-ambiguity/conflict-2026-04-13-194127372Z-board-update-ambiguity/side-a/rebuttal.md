- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The board should approve a bounded validation phase rather than an irreversible scale-up decision because no company-specific evidence was supplied.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim conflates the absence of evidence in the case packet with the absence of evidence available to the board. A real board already possesses extensive company-specific data — financials, pipeline, competitive intelligence, operational metrics — that informed the strategy under discussion. The artifact treats the case packet's sparse inputs as proof that the organization lacks evidence, when it actually proves only that the exercise was conducted without providing that context. Recommending a 'bounded validation phase' as the default response to any ambiguity is generic risk-aversion dressed as strategic discipline; it offers no criteria for when a board *should* act decisively under uncertainty (e.g., closing competitive windows, locking distribution partners, or capitalizing on regulatory shifts). The claim therefore lacks an evidence-based threshold for distinguishing productive caution from costly delay, making it unfalsifiable — any amount of existing evidence could be dismissed as insufficient without the artifact specifying what 'enough' looks like in concrete, measurable terms tied to the company's actual situation.
