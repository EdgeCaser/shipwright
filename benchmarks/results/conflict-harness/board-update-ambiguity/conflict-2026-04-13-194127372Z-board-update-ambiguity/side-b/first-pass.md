# Board Strategy Update: Operating Under Ambiguity

## Decision Frame

The board should not approve an irreversible scale-up decision yet. The appropriate decision is to approve a bounded learning phase with explicit gates, because the case packet provides no company-specific evidence, customer data, financial model, market proof, or operational constraints.

The board decision requested now is:

1. Approve a time-bounded strategy validation phase rather than a full commitment.
2. Define the minimum evidence required to move from exploration to execution.
3. Align on stop, continue, and escalate thresholds before additional capital or organizational focus is committed.

Recommended posture: maintain strategic optionality, protect runway, and force ambiguity into testable assumptions. The operating question is not whether the strategy is definitively right; it is whether the next increment of investment can produce decision-grade evidence at acceptable cost and speed.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

Current evidence gap: no validated evidence was supplied with the case packet. That means any board update must treat market attractiveness, customer urgency, competitive differentiation, business model quality, execution capacity, and financial return as unresolved.

Highest-priority unknowns:

1. Customer pull: whether the target customer has an urgent problem and a willingness to adopt a new solution.
2. Economic quality: whether the opportunity can support attractive gross margin, payback period, retention, and expansion potential.
3. Competitive durability: whether the company has a credible advantage that can survive incumbent response or fast-follow competition.
4. Execution feasibility: whether the organization can pursue the opportunity without materially weakening current commitments.
5. Capital intensity: whether the next phase requires contained validation spend or creates hidden long-term obligations.

Evidence required before a scale-up decision:

1. Customer evidence from qualified prospects or existing customers.
2. A quantified opportunity model with explicit assumptions and sensitivity ranges.
3. Competitive analysis tied to specific buying criteria, not generic feature comparison.
4. A resourcing plan that identifies tradeoffs against the current roadmap and operating plan.
5. A downside case showing what is lost if the initiative fails after the next funding gate.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

This strategy is not ready for full board approval as a scaled initiative. It is ready for approval as a structured validation program.

Pass criteria for moving forward after validation:

1. At least one clearly defined customer segment shows repeated, high-urgency demand.
2. The economic model remains attractive under conservative assumptions.
3. The company can name a specific advantage that matters in the purchase decision.
4. Required execution capacity is available or can be created through an explicit tradeoff.
5. The next investment tranche has a clear success threshold and an agreed kill threshold.

Fail criteria:

1. Customer interest is broad but shallow, with no evidence of urgency or willingness to pay.
2. The business case only works under best-case adoption, pricing, or retention assumptions.
3. Differentiation depends on claims the company cannot defend or operationalize.
4. The initiative materially distracts from higher-confidence priorities without a compensating upside.
5. The team cannot produce decision-grade evidence within the validation window.

Board readiness assessment: proceed only if the board is comfortable authorizing a limited validation phase and withholding scale-up approval until the evidence thresholds are met.

## Recommended Next Artifact

The next artifact should be a board-ready Strategy Validation Memo.

It should include:

1. A one-page decision summary with the specific board ask.
2. The top five assumptions that must be true for the strategy to work.
3. A 30-60-90 day validation plan with owners, cost, and evidence outputs.
4. A lightweight financial model with base, downside, and upside cases.
5. A decision gate defining continue, pivot, or stop conditions.

Recommended board ask: approve the validation plan, not the scaled strategy. Require management to return with evidence against the agreed thresholds before requesting additional capital, headcount, or strategic commitment.
