## Claim Under Attack
Under current ambiguity, the board should prefer a bounded validation phase with explicit decision gates over aggressive scaling, organized around critical assumptions and thresholds rather than narrative confidence.

## Evidence Or Reason
The artifact asserts that ambiguity is high and evidence is insufficient, but provides zero concrete evidence for the claim that ambiguity actually exists at the level described. No metrics, market signals, or internal data are cited — the entire framework rests on an unsubstantiated premise that conditions are too uncertain for commitment. The artifact also fails to name a single specific assumption, threshold value, or business metric, making its pass/fail criteria untestable. A board receiving this update cannot distinguish between genuine strategic uncertainty and management's lack of preparedness. The recommendation to 'run a bounded validation phase' is unfalsifiable as presented: without specifying what would constitute sufficient evidence to exit the validation phase, the framework defaults to perpetual deferral. This is precisely the kind of narrative confidence disguised as rigor that the artifact claims to avoid.
