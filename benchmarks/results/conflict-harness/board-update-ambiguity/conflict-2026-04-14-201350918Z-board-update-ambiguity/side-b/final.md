# Revised artifact

## Current Assessment
The packet provides no operating metrics, market data, trend evidence, or quantified assumptions. That absence is itself the relevant fact for the board: management has not yet supplied decision-grade support for a full-scale expansion, major repositioning, or material cost reset.

## Decision Frame
The board should not treat the current update as evidence that the strategy is wrong or that aggressive scaling is justified. The actionable decision is narrower: approve only a bounded next interval of work and require management to return with a quantified decision memo before any harder commitment.

Recommended board posture for the next operating interval:
1. Continue only the initiatives necessary to resolve the most consequential uncertainties.
2. Defer irreversible expansion or reset decisions until management produces threshold-based evidence.
3. Time-box this posture so it does not become open-ended deferral.

This recommendation is grounded in the record provided here: the case packet contains no concrete evidence sufficient to support a stronger commitment either way.

## What The Board Can Responsibly Conclude Now
Based on the current record, the board can conclude:
1. The update is decision-incomplete, not decision-neutral.
2. Management has not yet translated strategic ambiguity into testable assumptions and measurable gates.
3. Any request for major commitment before that translation would rely on narrative rather than evidence.

The board therefore should convert ambiguity into a governance requirement, not a confidence statement.

## Management Recommendation
Management should return with a board-ready memo that names the critical assumptions and ties each one to a metric, threshold, owner, and deadline. At minimum, the memo should cover:
1. Demand: what metric proves real pull rather than anecdotal interest.
2. Retention or usage durability: what metric shows signals are persistent rather than noisy.
3. Unit economics or efficiency: what metric shows the model improves with scale rather than deteriorates.
4. Delivery feasibility: what metric shows the organization can execute without hidden capacity risk.

Until that memo exists, the board should authorize only limited spend tied directly to resolving those uncertainties.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The most important gaps are not subtle; they are missing from the packet:
1. No specific demand metric or customer behavior signal is provided.
2. No retention, usage, or durability evidence is provided.
3. No unit-economic or go-to-market efficiency evidence is provided.
4. No operating-capacity evidence is provided.
5. No threshold values or decision deadlines are provided.

Because these items are absent, the board cannot distinguish between genuine market ambiguity and internal lack of preparation.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass readiness for a larger commitment requires all of the following in the next update:
1. The top 2-3 strategic assumptions are explicitly named.
2. Each assumption has one primary metric, one threshold, one owner, and one decision date.
3. The board can see a clear rule for scale, hold, or stop.
4. The proposed capital exposure before the next gate is explicit and bounded.

Fail readiness is present if any of the following remain true in the next update:
1. Assumptions are still described narratively rather than operationally.
2. Metrics are listed without threshold values or decision consequences.
3. Spend continues before the board can evaluate evidence quality.
4. The validation period has no end date or no exit criteria.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A board decision memo for the next 60-90 days with this minimum structure:
1. Assumption register: assumption, why it matters, current evidence, missing evidence.
2. Gating table: metric, threshold value, owner, date, and board action if threshold is met or missed.
3. Capital-at-risk summary: spend authorized before the next decision gate.
4. Decision request: exactly what management wants approved now versus deferred until evidence arrives.

That artifact would make the strategy update testable and would prevent ambiguity from becoming perpetual deferral.
