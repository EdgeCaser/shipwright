# Strategy Update: Board Briefing Under Ambiguity

## Decision Frame

**Core question:** Should the board authorize continued investment in the current strategic direction given incomplete market signals, or pivot resources toward validated alternatives?

**Why this decision matters now:** Three converging pressures force a decision this quarter rather than deferring:
1. **Capital allocation window** — Next fiscal year's budget locks in 60 days. Deferral is itself a decision (to maintain status quo allocation).
2. **Competitive signal ambiguity** — Two competitors have made moves that could indicate either market validation of our thesis or a shift toward a segment we've deprioritized. The data supports both interpretations.
3. **Organizational momentum** — Teams are executing against the current plan. Changing direction has increasing switching costs with each passing month.

**What this update does NOT do:** This is not a recommendation to commit. It frames what we know, what we don't, and what would need to be true for each path to succeed.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

| Unknown | What we have | What we need | Estimated cost to close | Timeline to close |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Customer willingness-to-pay at proposed price point | Qualitative signal from 12 discovery interviews (positive but non-binding) | Conjoint study or binding LOIs from 3+ design partners | $25K–$40K (conjoint) or 4–6 weeks of BD effort (LOIs) | 6–8 weeks |
| Competitor intent behind recent product launch | Public announcements, two analyst reports with conflicting interpretations | Direct competitive intelligence (win/loss interviews from shared prospects) | Internal effort, 3–5 interviews | 4–6 weeks |
| Unit economics at scale | Modeled estimates with ±30% variance on COGS | Pilot cohort data from first 50 customers at production pricing | Requires pilot launch | 10–14 weeks |
| Regulatory trajectory in target segment | Draft guidance published; final rule timeline uncertain | Legal analysis of draft rule + scenario modeling for 2 outcomes | $15K external counsel | 3–4 weeks |

**Prioritization of unknowns:** Not all four gaps carry equal decision weight. WTP validation and unit economics are **primary gates** — a fail on either would independently block a go decision regardless of the other signals. Competitive position and regulatory trajectory are **secondary modulators** — they shape the attractiveness and timing of a go decision but would not alone trigger a pivot. This prioritization reflects standard capital allocation logic: demand and margin viability must be established before market-positioning and regulatory questions become load-bearing.

**Key observation:** The two primary gates (WTP and unit economics) are resolvable within one quarter if we commit resources now. The risk of waiting is not that conditions worsen — it's that we lose the option to learn before the budget locks.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

**Are we ready to make a go/no-go decision today?** No — specifically because the two primary gating unknowns (WTP and unit economics) remain at directional-confidence level, not evidentiary-confidence level.

**Basis for this assessment:** The 12 discovery interviews provide directional signal but lack binding commitment or quantitative rigor. Industry benchmarks for B2B SaaS suggest that qualitative interview enthusiasm converts to purchase intent at roughly 30–50%, meaning our current positive signals could represent anywhere from 4 to 6 genuinely convertible prospects out of 12 — insufficient to underwrite a capital commitment. The unit economics model's ±30% COGS variance spans the difference between a viable business (55%+ gross margin) and a marginal one (<40%), making it decision-useless in its current form.

**What would make us ready:**

| Criterion | Current status | Pass threshold | Fail threshold | Threshold rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Demand validation (PRIMARY GATE) | Directional (interviews only) | ≥3 signed LOIs or conjoint WTP within 15% of target price | <1 LOI after 6-week push, or conjoint WTP >30% below target | 3 LOIs represents minimum viable pipeline for pilot; 15% WTP tolerance reflects typical negotiation discount range |
| Unit economics (PRIMARY GATE) | Modeled with high variance | Pilot cohort gross margin ≥55% at production pricing | Pilot gross margin <40% or negative contribution margin | 55% threshold reflects minimum for reinvestment in growth; <40% historically correlates with unrecoverable margin compression at scale |
| Competitive position (SECONDARY) | Ambiguous — plausible advantage, plausible parity | Win/loss data shows ≥2:1 win rate against primary competitor in target segment | Win rate <1:1, or competitor demonstrates feature parity on core differentiator | Informs go-to-market urgency, not go/no-go |
| Regulatory risk (SECONDARY) | Uncertain | Legal opinion: compliant under ≥1 plausible final rule scenario | Legal opinion: non-compliant under all plausible scenarios | Binary compliance gate; one viable path is sufficient to proceed |

**Current honest assessment:** We have directional confidence but not evidentiary confidence on the two variables that matter most. The gap is closable but requires deliberate allocation of resources to learning over execution for the next 6–8 weeks.

## Recommended Next Artifact

**Recommendation:** Authorize a structured 8-week learning sprint before the capital allocation deadline, with priority sequencing that front-loads the primary gates.

**Deliverables from the sprint (in priority order):**
1. **Demand validation memo** (weeks 1–6) — Conjoint results or signed LOI portfolio, presented as pass/fail against thresholds above. This is the single highest-value learning activity.
2. **Preliminary unit economics report** (weeks 4–10) — From pilot cohort if launched, or refined model with sensitivity analysis if pilot is deferred. Overlaps with demand validation to compress timeline.
3. **Competitive positioning update** (weeks 3–6) — Win/loss analysis with recommendation on whether our differentiation thesis holds.
4. **Regulatory scenario brief** (weeks 2–4) — External counsel opinion mapped to the two most likely final rule outcomes.

**Decision protocol at sprint end:**
- If both primary gates pass → present commit recommendation with resource plan
- If one primary gate fails → present pivot options with cost-of-delay analysis
- If both primary gates fail → present orderly wind-down or radical pivot recommendation
- If evidence is mixed on primary gates → present the two strongest options with explicit trade-offs and a recommended path, not a request for more time

**Resource ask:** $40K–$55K in external spend (conjoint + legal counsel), plus reallocation of 2 BD team members to LOI pursuit for 6 weeks. No headcount additions. No changes to current product roadmap during the sprint.

**Risk of this approach:** 8 weeks of learning delays 8 weeks of execution if the answer turns out to be "proceed as planned." This is the cost of reducing uncertainty. The alternative — committing now — risks a larger misallocation if the evidence would have shown a fail signal.
