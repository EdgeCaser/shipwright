# Revised Strategy Update Under Ambiguity

## Decision Frame

The board should not treat the current ambiguity as a reason to pause strategy work. It should treat it as a reason to make the strategy more explicit, staged, and falsifiable.

Recommended directional bet: pursue a narrow wedge strategy rather than a broad market or platform strategy. The working hypothesis is that the highest-value path is to concentrate on one customer segment, one urgent job, and one measurable business outcome before expanding scope. This is not presented as a proven fact; it is the most responsible strategic posture given that the case packet provides no substantive market, customer, financial, or competitive evidence. [ctx-1]

The decision requested from the board is to approve a bounded strategy-validation phase, not scaled execution. Management should be authorized to test the narrow-wedge thesis while explicitly deferring large fixed investment, broad hiring, major channel commitments, or irreversible product bets.

The board should weigh three options:

1. Proceed broadly: highest upside if the opportunity is already validated, but unjustified under the current evidence gap.
2. Pause entirely: lowest near-term risk, but risks losing learning velocity and strategic momentum.
3. Stage-gate a narrow wedge: best fit for the current ambiguity because it forces a real strategic choice while preserving reversibility.

Recommendation: choose option 3.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

The directional bet depends on assumptions that are currently unproven:

- Customer assumption: there is a specific buyer or user segment with a painful enough job to justify action now.
- Value assumption: solving that job creates a measurable business outcome, not just feature-level interest.
- Differentiation assumption: the company can offer something meaningfully better than the customer's current alternative.
- Economic assumption: the wedge can support a credible monetization path, margin profile, and payback logic.
- Timing assumption: acting now is preferable to waiting because there is a market, customer, competitive, or capability-based reason for urgency.
- Execution assumption: the company can validate the wedge without distracting materially from core operating priorities.

The evidence gaps remain material: no target segment, buyer, use case, willingness-to-pay signal, competitive alternative, financial model, execution constraint, or strategic-fit argument is supplied in the case packet. [ctx-1]

## Pass/Fail Readiness

The strategy should advance only if the next validation phase produces evidence against clear thresholds.

Pass criteria:

- A narrow target segment is named and can be reached directly.
- At least one urgent customer job is validated through direct customer or buyer evidence.
- The team can name the incumbent alternative and explain why the wedge is meaningfully better.
- There is a credible path to revenue, retention, or strategic leverage from the wedge.
- The required next investment remains staged and reversible.
- Management can state what it will stop doing or deprioritize to pursue the wedge.

Fail criteria:

- The opportunity remains framed at category level rather than customer-job level.
- Customer conversations show interest but no urgency, budget, workflow pain, or switching trigger.
- The economics depend on scale before there is evidence of demand.
- Differentiation cannot be defended against current alternatives.
- The team cannot identify a stop condition or continues expanding scope to avoid a hard choice.

Current readiness assessment: not ready for a full go decision. Ready for a board-approved wedge-validation phase with explicit kill criteria.

## Recommended Next Artifact

Prepare a board-facing Wedge Strategy Memo for the next review. It should include:

- The proposed target segment and why that segment is the first wedge.
- The customer job and business outcome the company is betting on.
- The current alternative customers use today.
- The strategic rationale for acting now.
- The top assumptions that must be true.
- The validation plan for the next 30 to 45 days.
- Go, revise, and stop thresholds.
- The resource request and what larger commitments remain deferred.

The board ask should be: approve a staged narrow-wedge validation plan, require management to return with evidence against the thresholds, and withhold approval for scaled execution until the wedge thesis is either validated or rejected.
