## Claim Under Attack
Because the case packet contains no substantive evidence, the board update should recommend a staged evidence sprint rather than a full strategic commitment.

## Evidence Or Reason
SIDE_B conflates the absence of evidence in the case packet with the absence of evidence in the organization. The scenario prompt asks for a 'strategy update for a board audience under ambiguity' — meaning the strategist must demonstrate judgment under incomplete information, not refuse to exercise judgment until information is complete. SIDE_B's artifact is entirely procedural: it lists what evidence is missing and proposes gathering it, but offers zero strategic direction, no hypothesis about where value might lie, and no framework for how the board should weigh tradeoffs while waiting. A board receiving this update would learn nothing about management's strategic thinking — only that management has none yet. The rubric dimension 'decision usefulness' requires the artifact to help the board act; a comprehensive inventory of unknowns paired with a request for more time is the least decision-useful output possible. Real strategy under ambiguity requires stating a directional bet, naming the assumptions it depends on, and defining kill criteria — SIDE_B does only the last of these while avoiding the first two entirely.
