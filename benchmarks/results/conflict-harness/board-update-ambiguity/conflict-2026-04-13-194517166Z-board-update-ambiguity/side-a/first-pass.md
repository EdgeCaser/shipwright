# Strategy Update: Board Briefing Under Ambiguity

## Decision Frame

**Core question:** Given material uncertainty in our operating environment, what commitments can the board ratify today, and which decisions must be deferred until specific evidence thresholds are met?

**Context:** This update is written under conditions where key inputs to our strategic plan remain unresolved. Rather than present false precision, this document separates what we know from what we don't, identifies the decisions that are robust across scenarios, and flags the decisions that depend on information we do not yet have.

**Framing principle:** The board's role in this cycle is not to approve a plan but to approve a decision framework — the criteria under which management will act when ambiguity resolves.

### What is decidable now
- **Resource allocation floor:** Minimum investment levels required to maintain optionality across all plausible scenarios. These commitments are regret-minimizing regardless of how uncertainty resolves.
- **Kill criteria:** Conditions under which we discontinue current initiatives, preventing drift into sunk-cost continuation.
- **Monitoring cadence:** Agreement on the review triggers (not just calendar dates) that bring decisions back to the board.

### What is not decidable now
- Full-year capital allocation above the floor, pending resolution of unknowns listed below.
- Market entry timing for expansion initiatives, pending competitive and regulatory signal clarity.
- Organizational restructuring scope, pending demand trajectory confirmation.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

|---|---------|---------------|----------------------|----------------|-------------|
| U1 | Demand trajectory in core segment | Determines whether we scale current operations or pivot investment to adjacencies | Conflicting signals: leading indicators positive, lagging indicators flat | Commission independent demand study; instrument product telemetry for forward-looking signals | 60 days |

**Evidence discipline note:** Each unknown above has a defined resolution path with a concrete deliverable, not an open-ended "monitor the situation." If the resolution path fails to produce clarity by the target date, that failure itself is a decision input — we default to the conservative scenario.

## Pass/Fail Readiness

**Assessment: NOT READY for full strategic commitment. READY for conditional framework approval.**

### Readiness criteria and status

| Criterion | Status | Implication |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Core demand signal validated | ⬜ Fail — conflicting data | Cannot commit to scale-up capital |
| Regulatory path clear | ⬜ Fail — pending | Cannot commit to expansion geography |
| Competitive landscape stable | ⬜ Fail — insufficient observation window | Cannot finalize pricing strategy |
| Internal capacity validated | ⬜ Fail — audit not complete | Cannot commit to parallel execution |
| Financial reserves adequate for optionality period | ✅ Pass | Can sustain current operations + investigation costs through resolution period |
| Management alignment on decision criteria | ✅ Pass | Team agrees on the framework; disagreement is on inputs, not logic |

**Bottom line:** We have alignment on *how* to decide but not *what* to decide. The responsible action is to approve the decision framework, fund the evidence-gathering workstreams, and reconvene when triggers are met — not before, not after.

### Risk of premature commitment
Committing to a full plan now would require assuming answers to U1–U4. The expected cost of being wrong on any single assumption exceeds the carrying cost of maintaining optionality for 60–90 days. This is not indecision; it is capital-efficient patience.

### Risk of excessive delay
Beyond the 90-day window, optionality cost increases as competitors may establish positions and internal talent retention becomes harder without clear direction. The framework includes hard deadlines that force a decision even under residual ambiguity.

## Recommended Next Artifact

**Deliverable:** Scenario Decision Matrix

**Description:** A structured document mapping each combination of resolved unknowns (U1–U4) to a specific strategic action, pre-approved by the board. When evidence arrives, management executes the corresponding row without requiring a new board cycle.

**Contents:**
1. 2×2 scenario grid on the two highest-impact unknowns (U1 × U2)
2. Pre-committed resource allocation for each quadrant
3. Trigger definitions: what specific evidence moves us from one scenario to another
4. Escalation criteria: which scenario combinations require emergency board review vs. management discretion
5. Timeline: 30/60/90-day checkpoints with specific deliverables at each

**Owner:** CEO + CFO, with strategy team support

**Board action requested:** Approve this framework approach, authorize investigation budgets for U1–U4 resolution paths, and delegate scenario-conditional execution authority to management within pre-agreed parameters.
