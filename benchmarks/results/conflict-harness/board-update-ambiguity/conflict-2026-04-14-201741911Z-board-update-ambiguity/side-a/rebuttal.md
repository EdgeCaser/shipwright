# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1, side_b-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Under current ambiguity, the best board-level recommendation is a bounded validation phase rather than immediate acceleration or retrenchment, because the highest-risk failure mode is making irreversible investment decisions before validation.

## Evidence Or Reason
The artifact asserts that a bounded validation phase is the optimal response and that false precision is the highest-risk failure mode, but provides no evidence for either claim. No data on current burn rate, runway, competitive dynamics, or opportunity cost of delay is presented. A board cannot evaluate whether 60-90 days of validation is prudent or catastrophic without knowing how fast the window of opportunity is closing. If a competitor is scaling now, or if the company has 18 months of runway versus 6, the calculus changes entirely. The artifact also never considers that delayed action under ambiguity can itself be an irreversible decision — market windows close, talent disperses, and first-mover advantages erode. By framing only acceleration as risky and treating the validation phase as inherently conservative, the analysis contains a systematic bias toward inaction that it never acknowledges or stress-tests. The pass/fail criteria are described in qualitative terms ('directionally positive,' 'credible explanation') with no quantitative thresholds, making the gates unfalsifiable and the entire framework vulnerable to confirmation bias at the next review.
