# Strategy Update: Board Briefing Under Ambiguity

## Decision Frame

The board faces a strategic inflection point where key market signals are contradictory and the data needed to resolve them will not arrive within this quarter's decision window. Rather than defaulting to either aggressive commitment or protective stalling, we recommend a **structured optionality** approach: preserve the ability to move decisively once ambiguity resolves, while taking low-regret actions now that improve our position regardless of which scenario materializes.

**Core tension:** We have strong leading indicators on customer demand (pipeline up 22% QoQ) but lagging confirmation on unit economics sustainability at scale. Committing full resources to expansion before validating margin durability risks overextension; waiting for perfect data risks ceding first-mover positioning to competitors who are already scaling.

**Decision required:** Approve a phased resource allocation that commits 60% of proposed expansion budget now (focused on reversible infrastructure and talent pipeline) while gating the remaining 40% on margin validation milestones expected by end of Q3.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

| Unknown | Impact if Resolved Favorably | Impact if Resolved Unfavorably | Expected Resolution Timeline |
|---------|------------------------------|-------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| Unit margin at 2x current volume | Unlocks full expansion case | Requires pivot to margin-first strategy | Q3 cohort data (8-10 weeks) |
| Competitor pricing response | Validates current positioning | May compress margins further | Observable within 6 weeks |
| Regulatory environment shift | Opens adjacent market segment | Constrains TAM by ~15% | Legislative session ends Q4 |
| Key talent availability in target markets | Enables rapid scaling | Slows expansion by 1-2 quarters | Recruiting pipeline data in 4 weeks |

**What we know with confidence:**
- Customer acquisition cost has remained stable through three consecutive quarters of growth
- Product-market fit signals (NPS 62, retention at 94%) are robust across segments
- Balance sheet supports the proposed phased allocation without covenant risk

**What we do not know and cannot yet resolve:**
- Whether current margins hold at significantly higher volume (no historical analog in our data)
- How competitors will respond to our expansion — two have raised recent rounds suggesting capacity to engage in price competition
- Whether pending regulatory changes will expand or constrain our addressable market

## Pass/Fail Readiness

**Assessment: CONDITIONAL PASS — ready to proceed with phased approach, not ready for full commitment.**

| Readiness Criterion | Status | Notes |
|---------------------|--------|-------|
| Financial capacity | ✅ Pass | Balance sheet supports phased allocation; full commitment would require debt facility |
| Operational capacity | ⚠️ Conditional | Current team can execute Phase 1; Phase 2 requires 3 senior hires not yet closed |
| Market validation | ⚠️ Conditional | Demand signals strong but margin durability at scale unproven |
| Risk containment | ✅ Pass | Phase 1 investments are >80% reversible within 2 quarters if needed |
| Board alignment | ❓ Unknown | This briefing is the alignment mechanism; prior informal signals suggest support for measured approach |

**Go/no-go gates for Phase 2 release:**
1. Q3 cohort margin data confirms gross margin ≥ 58% at projected volume (current: 63% at current volume)
2. At least 2 of 3 target senior hires accepted and started
3. No material adverse regulatory development

All three gates must clear for Phase 2 funds release. Any single gate failure triggers a strategy review session with the board before proceeding.
