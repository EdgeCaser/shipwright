## Claim Under Attack
Under current evidence conditions, a hold-and-probe posture is superior to premature commitment because two of four key unknowns have weak evidence bases, making the downside risk of committing now disproportionate to the low cost of a 60-day delay.

## Evidence Or Reason
The claim asserts a comparative decision case without any underlying business data to support either side of the tradeoff. The artifact itself admits there are no evidence files and flags the 2-4 week cost-of-delay estimate as asserted rather than derived. Without concrete financial exposure, pipeline size, burn implications, or quantified downside from delay versus action, the conclusion that hold is 'superior' is not evidence-backed; it is a plausible posture framed as a justified recommendation.
