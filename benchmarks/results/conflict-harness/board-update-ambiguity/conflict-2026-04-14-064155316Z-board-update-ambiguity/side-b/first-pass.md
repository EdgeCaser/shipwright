# Strategy Update: Navigating Ambiguity

**Prepared for: Board of Directors**
**Date: Q2 2026**
**Classification: Board Confidential**

---

## Decision Frame

**Core question:** Should we commit additional capital to our growth initiative given unresolved market signals, or hold current position until ambiguity resolves?

**Why this decision matters now:** Three converging pressures force a decision window:
1. Our current runway at burn rate supports ~14 months of operations, but the growth initiative requires commitment within 60 days to capture favorable vendor terms.
2. Two leading indicators (customer acquisition cost trend and enterprise pipeline velocity) are sending contradictory signals — CAC is rising while pipeline volume increases.
3. A competitor announced a Series C but has not yet deployed capital visibly in market. The window to establish positioning may narrow.

**Decision options on the table:**
- **Option A — Accelerate:** Commit the planned $4M growth tranche now, accepting higher variance in outcomes.
- **Option B — Stage-gate:** Release $1.5M for a 90-day validation sprint with pre-defined go/no-go criteria before committing the remainder.
- **Option C — Hold:** Maintain current spend levels and revisit in Q3 when two key data points (retention cohort maturity, enterprise pilot conversions) will be available.

**Management recommendation:** Option B (Stage-gate), detailed below.

---

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

| Unknown | Impact if resolved unfavorably | What would resolve it | Earliest resolution date |
|---|---|---|---|
| Enterprise pilot-to-paid conversion rate | Undermines entire growth thesis if <20% | Complete current 3 pilot cycles | ~75 days |
| Whether rising CAC reflects channel saturation or seasonal pattern | Misallocation of $2-3M in paid acquisition | Two more monthly cohorts with channel-level attribution | ~60 days |
| Competitor deployment timing and vertical focus | Could invalidate positioning in 1-2 segments | Market intelligence; observable within 90 days of their funding announcement | ~45 days |
| Regulatory proposal impact on unit economics | Could shift margin structure by 3-8 points | Draft rule publication expected Q3 | ~90 days |

**What we do know with confidence:**
- Existing customer retention (18-month cohorts) remains strong at 91% logo retention, 108% net revenue retention.
- Product-market fit signal in our core segment is consistent across qualitative and quantitative indicators.
- The team has capacity to execute either acceleration or stage-gate without additional hiring in the 90-day window.

**Key epistemic risk:** The largest gap is not any single unknown but the correlation structure between them. If rising CAC *and* slow pilot conversion both reflect a market-timing problem rather than independent issues, the downside scenario is materially worse than modeling them independently suggests.

---

## Pass/Fail Readiness

**Are we ready to make the full commitment decision today?** No.

**Readiness assessment against decision criteria:**

| Criterion | Status | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Product-market fit evidence | ✅ Pass | Sufficient for core segment |
| Unit economics clarity | ⚠️ Conditional | CAC trend must stabilize or be explained |
| Competitive positioning durability | ⚠️ Conditional | Pending competitor move observation |
| Team execution capacity | ✅ Pass | Confirmed for 90-day horizon |
| Capital efficiency of growth plan | ❌ Fail | Requires pilot conversion data to validate assumptions |
| Regulatory risk bounded | ⚠️ Conditional | Timeline uncertain; scenario modeling in progress |

**Bottom line:** Two of six criteria pass cleanly. Three are conditional on near-term information. One fails outright pending data that arrives within 75 days. This is not a profile that supports full commitment, but it *does* support a structured bet to close the gaps.

---

## Recommended Next Artifact

**Deliverable:** 90-Day Stage-Gate Validation Plan

**What it contains:**
1. **Go/no-go scorecard** with five binary criteria, each tied to a specific data source and threshold (e.g., "enterprise pilot conversion ≥20% by Day 75").
2. **Capital release schedule** — $1.5M released immediately across three workstreams, with the remaining $2.5M gated on scorecard results.
3. **Kill criteria** — explicit conditions under which we recommend returning uncommitted capital to reserve rather than proceeding (not just "things didn't go well" but specific thresholds).
4. **Board decision checkpoint** — scheduled for Day 80, with the scorecard pre-circulated at Day 70, so the board can deliberate with full data rather than under time pressure.

**What this buys us:**
- Preserves optionality on 85% of the capital at risk.
- Maintains execution momentum (teams stay staffed, vendor terms remain accessible with partial commitment).
- Converts the current ambiguity from a blocker into a structured learning period with defined exit conditions.

**What this costs us:**
- 90 days of calendar time before potential full acceleration.
- Some negotiating leverage on vendor terms (mitigated by partial commitment).
- If the market window is genuinely narrow, a staged approach may mean arriving second rather than first in one segment.

**Requested board action:** Approve Option B (stage-gate) and authorize management to return at the Day 80 checkpoint with the completed scorecard and a go/no-go recommendation.

---

*This update deliberately names what we do not know rather than projecting false confidence. The management team's conviction in the opportunity remains high, but conviction without evidence discipline is how boards get surprised. We would rather be transparent about gaps now and close them systematically than present premature certainty.*
