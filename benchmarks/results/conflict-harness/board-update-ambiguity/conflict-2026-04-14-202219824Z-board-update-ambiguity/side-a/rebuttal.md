# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Under ambiguity, the board should favor a gated strategy that preserves momentum on core priorities while deferring irreversible expansion commitments.

## Evidence Or Reason
The claim asserts that a gated strategy is the correct posture under ambiguity, but this presupposes that the cost of delay is lower than the cost of overcommitment — a judgment the artifact never substantiates. In many board-level scenarios, ambiguity itself is the threat: competitors act while you gate-check, talent windows close, and market timing erodes. The artifact acknowledges it has no operating data, competitive intelligence, or financial trendlines, yet it still recommends a specific strategic posture (gated continuation) as though that posture is risk-neutral. Deferral is itself a bet — a bet that optionality compounds faster than opportunity decays — and the artifact treats it as a default rather than defending it against the equally plausible scenario where disciplined aggression under ambiguity outperforms caution. Without evidence that option value exceeds time-decay risk in this specific context, the recommendation is an undefended preference masquerading as a principled framework.
