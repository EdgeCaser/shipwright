# Strategy Update: Board Audience Under Ambiguity

## Current Position
We are operating in a decision window with material uncertainty on demand shape, execution timing, and downstream impact. The practical implication is that the board should evaluate this period less as a moment for irreversible commitment and more as a controlled phase for preserving option value while forcing faster learning.

## What Has Changed
Signals are directionally relevant but not yet strong enough to justify a full strategic lock. We have enough evidence to narrow the field of credible paths, but not enough to treat any single path as decisively validated. That means the main risk is not only choosing incorrectly; it is also pretending ambiguity is lower than it is.

## Management View
The recommended posture is selective commitment:
1. Continue funding the highest-conviction near-term actions.
2. Defer irreversible scaling decisions until a small set of critical unknowns is resolved.
3. Hold management accountable to explicit trigger conditions, not narrative optimism.

## Decision Frame
The decision before the board is whether to authorize a posture of staged execution under ambiguity rather than force premature strategic certainty. The recommended decision is to approve a bounded plan that protects downside, preserves upside, and converts the next operating period into an evidence-generating interval. Success should be judged by whether uncertainty is reduced fast enough to support a higher-confidence follow-on decision.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The primary evidence gaps are concentrated in three areas: market confirmation, delivery confidence, and economic sensitivity. We do not yet know whether current signals represent durable demand or temporary noise. We also lack enough proof that the organization can execute at the required speed and quality under scaled conditions. Finally, unit economics and risk exposure under multiple scenarios remain insufficiently resolved for a board-grade expansion commitment.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass: the company can show leading indicators that are stable across more than one measurement window, operational milestones are hit on time, and downside exposure remains bounded under a conservative case. Fail: signal quality remains mixed, execution slips against the focused plan, or economics deteriorate when tested against realistic scaling assumptions. At present, this should be considered conditional readiness, not full readiness.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A board decision memo with scenario ranges, trigger-based gates, and a 30/60/90-day learning agenda. That artifact should explicitly map each open uncertainty to an owner, a measurement method, a deadline, and the decision it will unlock.
