# Revised artifact

## Current Position
Management does not yet have a board-grade evidentiary basis for an irreversible strategic commitment. The central fact pattern is not merely that outcomes are uncertain, but that the current update packet contains no quantified market, execution, or economic evidence sufficient to validate either acceleration or deferral with confidence.

## What Has Changed
The decision should therefore be reframed. This is not yet a choice among fully supported strategic paths; it is a governance choice about what management must prove, by when, before asking the board for a larger commitment.

## Management View
The recommended action is narrower than the first-pass version: authorize a time-boxed evidence-generation phase with explicit board gates, rather than a broad staged-execution mandate. That keeps the company moving on low-regret work while preventing ambiguity from becoming an indefinite operating excuse.

## Decision Frame
The board should decide whether to approve a 30/60/90-day proof plan instead of an irreversible scale decision. Approval should be conditioned on three requirements:
1. Management must define the exact indicators that constitute market confirmation, execution readiness, and economic viability.
2. Each indicator must have a baseline, target threshold, owner, and review date.
3. If those thresholds are not met by the next board checkpoint, management must return with a recommendation to pause, narrow scope, or redesign the strategy.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The material gaps are unchanged but are now stated as unresolved rather than implied facts. We do not yet have cited evidence on demand durability, delivery performance under scaled conditions, or downside economics. Because the evidence packet is empty, the board should treat all ambiguity claims as provisional until management supplies concrete measures, historical comparisons, and scenario ranges.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass: management returns with a board-ready scorecard that names the indicators, measurement windows, thresholds, and scenario bounds required for a scale decision, and the observed results meet those thresholds. Fail: management cannot specify the gates, cannot produce baseline data, or produces results that miss threshold on any critical dimension. On the current record, the company is not ready for a full strategic commitment; it is only ready for a disciplined proof phase.

## Recommended Next Artifact
A board decision memo that includes: the three decision gates, the specific metrics under each gate, baseline values, threshold values, scenario ranges, named owners, and a hard escalation rule if evidence remains inconclusive at the end of the proof window.
