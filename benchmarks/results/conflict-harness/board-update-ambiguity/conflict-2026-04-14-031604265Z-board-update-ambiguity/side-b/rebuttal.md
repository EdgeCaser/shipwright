# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-1, side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The board should authorize staged execution with explicit decision gates rather than make an irreversible commitment under current ambiguity, framed as preserving option value while rapidly reducing uncertainty.

## Evidence Or Reason
Both major claims rest entirely on a single generic context reference (ctx-1) with no concrete evidence cited anywhere in the artifact. The strategy asserts material uncertainty in demand shape, execution timing, and economic sensitivity but provides zero quantitative indicators, no baseline metrics, no historical comparisons, and no specific market data to substantiate the degree of ambiguity claimed. A board audience cannot distinguish between genuine strategic uncertainty and management hedging without evidence. The pass/fail criteria are similarly ungrounded — 'leading indicators stable across more than one measurement window' names no actual indicators, no measurement windows, and no thresholds. The artifact effectively asks the board to approve deferral based on the assertion that ambiguity exists, rather than demonstrating it with evidence. This makes the decision frame unfalsifiable: if management controls both the uncertainty narrative and the trigger definitions, staged execution becomes an indefinite delay mechanism with no accountability. The recommended posture of 'selective commitment' is operationally indistinguishable from status quo continuation without the missing specifics.
