# Revised artifact

## Recommendation
Do not treat the Skydance transaction as the final answer until the available process window is fully run, but do not rely on speculative alternatives either. The special committee should use the go-shop period as a short, disciplined market test and be prepared to accept the Skydance deal if no clearly superior, actionable alternative emerges.

This is a narrower recommendation than simply "keep searching." The case packet supports urgency: Paramount faced nearly $15 billion of debt, weak advertising conditions, continued cable declines, and ongoing streaming losses, while Paramount+ had not yet reached profitability. Rejecting the deal outright in favor of an open-ended independent path would leave the company exposed to those pressures. But accepting immediately would also lock in a controller-ending two-step transaction despite stated concerns about governance and valuation fairness and despite the existence of a 45-day go-shop period.

## Core Reasoning
The committee does not have evidence in the packet that alternative bidders are ready with superior board-ready proposals. Apollo / Sony and Edgar Bronfman Jr. are evidence of interest, not proof of deliverable alternatives. That weakens any claim that a better deal is likely.

Even so, the packet does support a meaningful process question: Paramount already has a temporary window to solicit alternatives, and critics have questioned whether the committee is optimizing for all shareholders or primarily for the controller transaction path. In that context, the most defensible course is not immediate acceptance and not indefinite delay. It is to complete the market check quickly, compare any concrete alternatives against Skydance on value, governance, and balance-sheet support, and then act.

The Skydance case remains real. The proposal offers fresh capital, balance-sheet support, and strategic clarity, and Skydance argues it can combine creative leadership, technology, and capital with Paramount's library and distribution footprint. Those benefits matter because Paramount's traditional TV assets still generated cash but were structurally declining. If no better executable path appears during the available window, the committee should accept that the Skydance deal is the best actionable option in a deteriorating industry setting.

## Strongest Counterarguments
The strongest counterargument is that any delay is imprudent because the company is under pressure now and the known alternatives may be illusory. That is materially correct. The packet does not establish that other parties can close, finance, or govern a better outcome.

That counterargument still does not justify immediate acceptance before the existing process has run its course. The committee already has a defined go-shop period. Using that period is not the same as choosing a long independent turnaround; it is a controlled final test before committing to a transaction that reshapes control and shareholder outcomes.

## Decision Frame
The real choice is not "Skydance now" versus a comfortable stand-alone future. It is whether to finalize the current deal immediately or use the already-available process window to verify whether a clearly better executable alternative exists.

Recommendation: complete the go-shop urgently, require concrete and comparable proposals, and default to accepting Skydance if that test does not produce a superior actionable path.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The packet does not show whether any alternative bidder has a fully financeable, governable, and executable proposal.

It also does not resolve whether the current deal's valuation and governance terms are fair to non-controller shareholders given the two-step structure that also ends Redstone control.

A further gap is whether Skydance's strategic rationale is materially differentiated from what another buyer or restructuring path could credibly offer.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if the committee can use the 45-day window to force concrete comparisons across price, governance, balance-sheet support, and execution credibility.

Fail if no alternative emerges that is clearly superior and actionable within that window. In that case, further delay becomes speculative and the Skydance transaction should be accepted.

## Recommended Next Artifact
Produce a final decision memo for the special committee that compares Skydance, any concrete go-shop proposals, and the stand-alone path on four common criteria: shareholder value, governance fairness, balance-sheet support, and execution certainty.
