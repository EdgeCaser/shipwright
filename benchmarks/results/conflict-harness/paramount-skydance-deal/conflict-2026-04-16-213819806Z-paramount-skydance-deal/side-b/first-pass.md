# Strategic Recommendation Memo

## Recommendation
Reject the proposed transaction in its current form and continue the active search for alternatives during the available process window rather than locking in this outcome now.

The core reason is not that the company can comfortably remain independent. It cannot. The core reason is that the current situation still contains live optionality: there is a go-shop period, other interested parties have been discussed, and critics have raised unresolved concerns about governance and valuation fairness. In that setting, accepting immediately risks settling on a controller-driven path before the committee has exhausted the best available market check.

## Why This Is The Better Decision
First, the company is clearly under pressure, but that cuts both ways. Paramount faces heavy debt, weak advertising conditions, continued cable declines, and ongoing streaming losses. Those facts make strategic action necessary, but they do not by themselves prove that this specific transaction is the best available answer.

Second, the committee still has a credible basis to test for a superior outcome. The process includes a 45-day go-shop period, and other parties including Apollo / Sony and later Edgar Bronfman Jr. were discussed as alternatives. That means the relevant choice is not between this deal and a static stand-alone future; it is between accepting now versus using a still-open window to see whether a better structure, price, or governance outcome is attainable.

Third, the criticism around governance and valuation fairness matters materially here. The proposed structure acquires the controlling shareholder's vehicle and then merges Skydance with Paramount. Because the transaction path also resolves control, the committee should be especially careful not to optimize for speed or controller exit over the best outcome for the broader shareholder base.

Fourth, the upside case for the proposal is real but still partly conceptual. Skydance's rationale is that creative leadership, technology, and fresh capital can be combined with Paramount's library and distribution footprint. That may prove attractive, but the existing record here is stronger on strategic narrative than on demonstrated certainty that this is the uniquely best path available right now.

## Strongest Counterarguments
The strongest argument for accepting now is that delay is dangerous. Paramount carries nearly $15 billion of debt, its traditional television assets are structurally declining, the advertising market is weak, and Paramount+ has not yet reached profitability. The proposed transaction also brings balance-sheet support and strategic clarity.

That argument is serious. But the answer is not to accept immediately; it is to run the remaining process with urgency. The same facts that make delay costly also make it important to confirm whether this is truly the best available transaction before foreclosing alternatives.

## Decision Frame
The decision is whether to commit now to a transaction that offers capital support and a defined strategic path, or to use the remaining process to determine whether the company can secure a better combination of value, governance, and strategic flexibility.

Recommendation: do not accept immediately. Continue the search process and require any final deal to clear a higher bar on both fairness and strategic credibility.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The largest unknown is whether any alternative party can deliver a superior proposal with greater value or cleaner governance.

It is also unclear whether the current proposal's terms adequately compensate shareholders for surrendering future upside while also resolving the controller situation.

A final uncertainty is whether the claimed strategic benefits from the proposed partner are differentiated enough to outweigh process concerns and the possibility of a stronger alternative path.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if the committee can use the active process to test alternatives quickly and compare them directly on value, balance-sheet support, governance, and execution credibility.

Fail if no stronger alternative emerges and the committee cannot substantiate a better path than the current proposal within the available window. In that case, the proposed transaction may become the best actionable option despite its weaknesses.

## Recommended Next Artifact
Produce a board-ready alternatives comparison memo that evaluates the current proposal, other live bidder paths, and the stand-alone turnaround case against the same criteria: shareholder value, governance fairness, balance-sheet support, and strategic execution risk.
