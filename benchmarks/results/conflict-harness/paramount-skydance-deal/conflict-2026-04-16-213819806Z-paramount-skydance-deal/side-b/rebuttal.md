# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-3
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The 45-day go-shop period was the appropriate mechanism to surface superior alternatives; a go-shop that concludes without a binding superior offer is itself market evidence that Skydance represents the best available transaction.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim overreads the visible record. The case packet only says there was a 45-day go-shop and that other parties including Apollo/Sony and later Edgar Bronfman Jr. showed interest or were discussed as alternatives. It does not show the process design, bidder access, timing constraints, financing certainty, or whether the two-step controller structure itself discouraged rival bids. A go-shop can test interest, but without evidence on how open and competitively neutral that process actually was, 'no superior binding offer' is not strong proof that Skydance was the best available outcome; it may also reflect process friction or structural bidder deterrence.
