# Strategic Recommendation Memo: Accept the Skydance Transaction

**To:** Paramount Global Special Committee  
**Date:** July 2024  
**Re:** Skydance merger — recommendation to proceed

---

## Decision Frame

The committee faces a binary choice under time pressure: accept a structured transaction that provides immediate capital relief and strategic clarity, or preserve optionality at the cost of continued exposure to structural deterioration.

**Recommendation: Accept the Skydance transaction.**

The case for acceptance rests on three interlocking realities.

**1. The balance sheet cannot sustain a waiting strategy.** Paramount carries nearly $15 billion in debt. Paramount+ has not yet reached profitability. Traditional television assets still generate cash but are structurally declining. This combination — leveraged capital structure, negative-contribution streaming unit, and eroding core business — means that time is not neutral. Every quarter spent searching for a better outcome is a quarter in which the debt burden compounds and the linear TV asset base deteriorates further. Waiting is not a free option; it has a measurable cost that the current capital structure cannot easily absorb.

**2. The Skydance terms provide concrete, near-term balance sheet repair.** The transaction contemplates approximately $1.5 billion in fresh capital to support Paramount's balance sheet, plus the broader $4.5 billion cash-or-stock structure for shareholders. This is not a speculative future benefit — it is committed capital available at close. In the context of a $15 billion debt load and an unprofitable streaming operation, this injection materially changes the risk profile for the company's operating management going forward.

**3. The go-shop period was the correct mechanism to test alternatives, not a reason to delay closing.** The 45-day go-shop window allowed the special committee to solicit competing bids without abandoning the Skydance transaction. Interest from Apollo/Sony and subsequent expressions from other parties were surfaced within this process. The appropriate question is not whether to keep the go-shop open indefinitely, but whether any alternative bid emerged that was both credible and superior on risk-adjusted terms. A go-shop that runs its course without a superior offer is itself a data point — the market spoke.

**Position on the strongest counterargument:** Critics raised legitimate concerns about governance, valuation fairness, and whether the special committee's process was optimizing for all shareholders or primarily for the controller transaction path. These concerns deserve serious weight. However, they are process concerns, not outcome concerns. If the process was sound — if the go-shop was genuinely open and alternatives were rigorously evaluated — then the committee's fiduciary duty is satisfied by the outcome the market produced. A flawed process is a legal and governance problem to address in how the deal is run; it is not a reason to reject the deal itself if no superior alternative exists. The correct remedy for process risk is process discipline, not deal rejection.

---

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps

The following material uncertainties should be acknowledged:

- **Alternative bid quality:** The evidence notes Apollo/Sony interest and Bronfman engagement, but does not establish that any alternative bidder submitted a binding proposal that the committee could evaluate on equivalent terms. The committee's conclusion on alternatives cannot be assessed without that record.
- **Streaming trajectory:** Paramount+ has not reached profitability, but the evidence does not specify the projected timeline or capital requirement to reach profitability under either the Skydance path or an independent path. This is the central operating question and the evidence is silent on it.
- **Debt covenants and near-term liquidity:** The $15 billion debt figure is stated, but the evidence does not reveal the maturity schedule, covenant structure, or whether any near-term refinancing requirements exist. The urgency calculus depends on this.
- **Skydance integration thesis:** Skydance argued it could combine creative leadership, technology, and capital with Paramount's library and distribution. The evidence does not evaluate whether this integration thesis is credible or whether prior Skydance content performance supports the claim.
- **Valuation fairness opinion:** The evidence notes critics questioned valuation fairness but does not include the fairness opinion or the financial analysis underlying the committee's conclusion.

---

## Pass/Fail Readiness

**Readiness assessment: Conditionally ready to proceed, subject to go-shop completion.**

The transaction passes on strategic logic and capital structure necessity. The committee is ready to recommend acceptance provided:

1. The go-shop period closes without a superior proposal that meets or exceeds the Skydance terms on a risk-adjusted basis.
2. A credible fairness opinion is in hand addressing the valuation concerns raised by critics.
3. Governance terms in the post-close structure adequately protect minority shareholders from future controller conflicts.

If these conditions are met, the committee should proceed. If the go-shop produces a materially superior bid, the committee should negotiate or pivot — but the default absent a superior bid is acceptance.

**Failure modes to monitor:**
- A competing bid that offers equivalent capital support but superior governance terms should trigger renegotiation of governance provisions, not automatic deal rejection.
- If the $1.5 billion balance sheet support is structured with conditions that limit its availability during integration, the committee should push for unconditional availability.

---

## Recommended Next Artifact

**Go-shop outcome analysis:** A structured evaluation comparing any alternative bids received during the 45-day window against Skydance on four dimensions: (1) total capital delivered at close, (2) balance sheet support committed, (3) post-close governance structure, and (4) strategic integration credibility. This artifact would allow the committee to make a final recommendation with documented comparison rather than relying on the Skydance transaction as the default by elimination.
