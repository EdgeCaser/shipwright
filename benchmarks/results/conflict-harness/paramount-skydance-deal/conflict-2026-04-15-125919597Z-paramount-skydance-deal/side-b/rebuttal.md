# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
Paramount's $15B debt burden and ongoing streaming losses make independent continuation non-viable; only the Skydance transaction simultaneously resolves the control structure, injects capital, and offers a clear regulatory path — making acceptance the correct fiduciary outcome.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim overreaches beyond the visible record. The case packet supports that Paramount faced heavy debt, streaming losses, and linear decline, but it does not establish near-term insolvency, covenant breach, or that independent continuation was 'non-viable.' Side A itself concedes the specific covenant headroom and maturity schedule are unknown, which is exactly the missing evidence needed to turn pressure into inevitability. The claim also says 'only' Skydance could solve the situation, but the visible evidence only says other parties showed interest and that critics questioned whether the process optimized for the controller path. Without hard evidence that alternatives could not deliver capital, structure around NAI, or clear regulators, the fiduciary-necessity conclusion is asserted rather than proven.
