# Critique: finding-2

- Target Side: side_a
- Target Claim IDs: side_a-claim-2
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: medium

## Claim Under Attack
The 45-day go-shop period was the designated mechanism for surfacing superior bids; if it expired without a binding superior proposal, the market has cleared and rejection of Skydance leaves the company worse off.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim is conditional on facts the artifact does not establish. The memo itself admits the key gap is whether the go-shop produced any binding alternatives and how they were evaluated. Without those outcome details, it is too strong to infer that 'the market cleared' or that rejection necessarily leaves Paramount worse off. A short go-shop in a controller-influenced process may fail to surface full-value alternatives even when interest exists, especially if bidders face structural or diligence constraints.
