# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
The committee should reject immediate acceptance because the active go-shop period and known outside interest mean there is still credible optionality to test for a better outcome.

## Evidence Or Reason
Side B treats the go-shop period and 'known outside interest' as roughly equivalent to a viable alternative bid, but the case packet does not support that inference. Apollo/Sony and Bronfman are described only as parties who 'had shown interest or were discussed as potential alternatives' — not as parties who submitted binding proposals or demonstrated financing certainty. A 45-day go-shop window with soft interest is not the same as live optionality with credible competing bids. Side B acknowledges in its own Unknowns section that it cannot establish whether any alternative party 'can deliver a cleaner structure or better value with equivalent certainty of closing' — which is precisely the condition required for the optionality argument to hold. The claim that optionality exists worth preserving is asserted, not established, and the memo's own evidence gaps undercut it.
