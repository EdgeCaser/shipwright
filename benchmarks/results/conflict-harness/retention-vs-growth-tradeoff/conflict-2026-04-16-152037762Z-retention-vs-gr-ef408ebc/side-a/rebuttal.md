# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Roadmap priority should follow retention because the scenario states it has higher ROI and lower CAC payback than growth investment.

## Evidence Or Reason
The claim treats the scenario's stated ROI and CAC payback figures as sufficient to settle the prioritization decision, but these metrics are measured over different time horizons than a Series B raise window. Retention ROI may be structurally long-tailed — improvement in net revenue retention or expansion revenue often takes 2-4 quarters to compound visibly in ARR, while a Series B raise may occur in the next 1-2 quarters. Side B never interrogates whether the 'higher ROI' holds on a 90-to-180-day measurement window relevant to the raise, or only on a 12-to-24-month horizon where retention programs have time to compound. A memo that recommends retention-first without validating whether retention gains can surface as investor-legible KPI movement within the actual raise timeline has a critical temporal mismatch. The claim is directionally reasonable but premature as a decision anchor without specifying the measurement window.
