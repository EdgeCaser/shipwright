- Target Side: side_a

## Claim Under Attack
The data-narrative conflict is real and must be named explicitly as a choice between two masters — optimizing for internal ROI vs. investor optics — rather than resolved by splitting the roadmap without enforced resource commitments.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim overstates the conflict without showing that the two goals are actually separable or opposed in this case. The visible artifact itself admits retention could improve investor-legible metrics and that investor preferences are unverified, which weakens the framing of a hard two-master tradeoff. Without quantified ROI deltas, timeline data, or evidence that retention cannot support the fundraising story, the forced-choice framing is asserted rather than demonstrated.
