# Revised artifact

## Recommendation
Do not fully override the retention-led roadmap based only on leadership's desire for growth metrics ahead of the Series B. The case packet gives one clear evidentiary signal: internal data favors retention investment on both ROI and CAC payback, while leadership wants a different external narrative. Without evidence that a near-term shift toward growth-visible roadmap work will actually improve fundraising outcomes, the more decision-useful posture is to keep retention as the primary roadmap logic and, if necessary, add only narrowly scoped growth-visible work that does not meaningfully displace the retention agenda.

## Rationale
The scenario is defined by direct conflict between what the internal data supports and what leadership wants to emphasize for the raise. That means the memo should not pretend the narrative preference has the same status as the operating evidence. The right management move is to acknowledge the financing pressure explicitly while refusing to treat it as sufficient proof that growth-first prioritization is the better roadmap choice.

A reasonable compromise is therefore not a broad growth-first override, but a constrained packaging decision: preserve the roadmap elements supported by the stronger internal signal, document the tension openly, and allow only limited growth-visible additions if leadership can defend why those items matter for the raise without undermining the retention case.

## Decision Frame
The decision is whether to let fundraising narrative override the stronger internal retention signal. Recommendation: no full override. Keep retention as the primary roadmap priority, and permit only tightly bounded growth-visible work that does not materially weaken the retention-led plan.

## Unknowns & Evidence Gaps
The packet does not provide underlying evidence showing whether growth-visible roadmap changes will improve fundraising outcomes in the relevant window, nor does it show the cost of deferring retention work beyond the statement that retention has the stronger internal case. It is also unclear which growth metrics leadership believes matter for the raise, what initiatives would influence them, and what limits would keep a narrative concession from becoming an unjustified strategy shift.

## Pass/Fail Readiness
Pass if leadership accepts that the roadmap remains primarily retention-led, any growth-visible additions are explicitly limited, and the tradeoff is documented as a narrative accommodation rather than an evidence-backed change in company fundamentals. Fail if leadership insists on a broad growth-first reprioritization without evidence that doing so improves the fundraising position or without acknowledging the explicit conflict with the stronger internal retention case.

## Recommended Next Artifact
Create a fundraising-roadmap alignment brief that separates three things: the retention-led roadmap justified by internal data, the minimum growth-visible items leadership wants for the Series B narrative, and the explicit rationale for why any exception is narrow, temporary, and not a replacement for the stronger retention case.
