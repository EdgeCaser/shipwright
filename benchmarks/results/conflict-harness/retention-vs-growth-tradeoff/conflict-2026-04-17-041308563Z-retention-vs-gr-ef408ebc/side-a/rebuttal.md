# Critique: finding-1

- Target Side: side_b
- Target Claim IDs: side_b-claim-1
- Attack Type: evidence_gap
- Severity: high

## Claim Under Attack
Roadmap prioritization should temporarily favor growth-visible work because leadership's immediate objective is an upcoming Series B raise and the case explicitly frames the conflict as one between internal ROI and fundraising narrative.

## Evidence Or Reason
This claim accepts leadership's fundraising narrative as the operative constraint without interrogating whether growth-visible roadmap work will actually move the Series B needle in the available time window. Series B investors evaluate sustainable unit economics, not sprint-window activity counts. If the internal retention data shows higher ROI and lower CAC payback, sophisticated investors may penalize visible growth prioritization that appears to mask deteriorating retention metrics — making the claim's assumed benefit (stronger raise narrative) empirically unsubstantiated. Side B presents no evidence that short-cycle roadmap pivots toward growth are legible to investors on a Series B timeline, nor any basis for assuming the narrative benefit outweighs the compounding cost of deferring the higher-ROI path. The claim conflates 'what leadership wants to show' with 'what will improve fundraising outcomes,' treating those as equivalent when they may be inversely related.
